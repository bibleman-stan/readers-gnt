# R1 — Audit Trail

Sweep results and audit-precedent narrative for canon §3.1 (R1 No-Anchor Rule). The operational rule lives in the canon entry; this document carries the sweep history and corpus-impact evidence that the rule-template forbids inline.

## Corpus sweep status

- **2026-04-2x → 2026-05 final scan:** 860 no-anchor merges applied across 26 books. Final scan: 0 unanchored lines remaining corpus-wide.
- The bulk-sweep applier is `scripts/apply_no_anchor_merges.py` (upward-merge default; downward-merge fallback when the unanchored line opens its verse). Drift detection ongoing via `scripts/scan_no_anchor_lines.py`.

## Origin and consolidation

R1 was the first generative-force operationalization codified. It is the canonical baseline against which all later Tier-2+ merge-overrides (M1–M4, M4-GNT-1) and split-determinations (J1–J5) are reasoned: those rules apply *after* the no-anchor test has cleared.

## Anchor-type calibration

The four anchor types (finite verb / infinitive / predicate participle / independently-predicated substantive head) were refined twice during sweep:

- **Participle scope.** Initial drafts allowed "any participle" as an anchor. Sweep produced false-clean lines (adverbial-modifier participles and attributive-adjective participles that left the line proposition-less). Refinement: only predicate participles count.
- **Substantive head scope.** Initial drafts allowed bare-NP lines if the NP was contentful. Sweep produced false-clean lines (compound-list continuations of the prior verb's objects). Refinement: substantive head must be the independently-predicated topic of its own line — list-object continuations do NOT count, and their detection (the "object-continuation failure mode") became the dominant FP class to guard.

## Two-prong exception interaction

R1's exemption #4 — two-prong exception passes — defers to J1–J5. The J-rules are not "no-anchor licenses"; they are independent split-determinations that incidentally license a line that lacks one of the four R1 anchor types. The dependency direction is: J1–J5 fire first; the resulting line is exempt from R1 *because* J1–J5 has already justified its standalone presence (formally-marked parallel-series member / portrait-building accumulation / speech-act announcement / classical comma / substantive adjunct as own focus).
