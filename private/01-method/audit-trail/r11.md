# R11 — Audit Trail

Sweep history and audit-precedent narrative for canon §3.6 (R11 Direct Speech Introduction). The operational rule lives in the canon entry; this document carries the sweep records and corpus-impact evidence the rule-template forbids inline.

## Amen-formula sweep

- **2026-04, commit `c51faf8`:** 15 amen-prefix speech-intro applications across the corpus. Matthew/Mark/Luke use single `ἀμήν`; Johannine corpus uses doubled `ἀμὴν ἀμήν`. Both variants applied per the same R11 structural convention (formula on own line, content on next line).

## OT-attribution / parenthetical exclusion sweep

Exclusion patterns 1 (OT-attribution) and 2 (parenthetical mid-speech attribution) were codified after a corpus survey identified ~30 false-positive R11 fires the original rule would have flagged. After exclusion codification, all 30 were correctly merged. Sweep applied 2026-04 alongside the amen-formula pass.

## ἀπεκρίθη + λέγων Hebraism

Exclusion pattern 4 reflects a Semitic-idiom carve-out. Initial R11 detector iterations split `ἀπεκρίθη` from following `λέγων·`; corpus survey identified ~20 instances across the Synoptics and Acts where the double-verb construction is a single speech-intro Hebraism. Exclusion added; sweep restored merged form.

## Codification provenance for R28-ext relationship

R28-ext (canon §3.6.x) was added 2026-05-11 as a sub-rule of R11 to govern the substantive-frame case that the original frame-aggregation rule under-handled. Three STRONG-SPLIT-CANDIDATEs applied at codification: Heb 1:6, Luke 5:4, Luke 14:15.
