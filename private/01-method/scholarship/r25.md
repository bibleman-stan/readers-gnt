# R25 — ὥστε Short-Consecutive-Result Binding Scholarship

Substrate for canon §3.14a (R25 ὥστε Short-Consecutive-Result Binding). The operational rule + closed-list inventory live in the canon entry; this document carries the defensibility narrative, the 4-cluster illative-ὥστε analysis, and the corpus-survey evidence.

## Why a ὥστε-infinitive short-consecutive clause merges

A ὥστε-infinitive consecutive-result clause is structurally dependent — it cannot stand alone and it names the outcome that the matrix clause caused. **Cause + immediate short result = one cognitive image.** The reader's eye-span covers both in a single perceptual unit. Splitting them imposes a line-break on a semantic boundary that does not exist in the author's composition.

The three merge-test conditions (≤8 words, co-referential subject, no camera shift) operationalize the single-image diagnosis: each condition guards against a specific drift toward two-image territory.

- **≤8 words** — empirically, longer ὥστε-clauses introduce sufficient additional content (their own participants, their own scene-setting) that the reader's image-construction shifts. 9+ words is the threshold at which the result clause becomes its own perceptual unit.
- **Co-referential subject** — when the ὥστε-infinitive's agent differs from the matrix subject, the result clause introduces a new participant; that participant's action is its own image.
- **No camera shift** — when the result clause introduces a new viewpoint / scene element, the single-image diagnosis fails even if the word count is short and the subject is co-referential.

## Illative-ὥστε vs consecutive-result-ὥστε

ὥστε has two distinct discourse functions: (a) consecutive-result (binding a dependent result clause to its matrix — R25 territory), and (b) illative (inferential conjunction launching a new independent sentence — outside R25 scope). The morphological signature distinguishes them: **ὥστε + infinitive** is consecutive-result; **ὥστε + finite verb / vocative / imperative** is illative.

### Four illative-ὥστε pattern clusters

Phase C audit (task `aae0b801a5130b535`, 2026-05-11) identified 22 corpus illatives and clustered them into four canonical pattern families.

#### 1. Hortatory imperative / prohibitive

ὥστε immediately followed by 2p/3p imperative, prohibitive subjunctive, or 1p hortatory subjunctive. The clause is direct exhortation, not a dependent result.

**Canonical exemplar:** 1 Cor 4:5 — `ὥστε μὴ πρὸ καιροῦ τι κρίνετε` (direct 2p prohibition).

**Family:** 1 Cor 3:21, 5:8, 10:12, 14:39; 1 Thess 4:18; 1 Pet 4:19.

#### 2. Vocative + imperative opener

ὥστε + direct vocative address + command. The vocative makes the illative function undeniable.

**Canonical exemplar:** Phil 4:1 — `ὥστε, ἀδελφοί μου ἀγαπητοί, οὕτως στήκετε ἐν κυρίῳ` (cross-chapter scope).

**Family:** 1 Cor 14:39.

#### 3. New 3P doctrinal-principle declaration

ὥστε + new indefinite or general subject + timeless-present predication; no action-continuity with the matrix. Often followed by a purpose clause.

**Canonical exemplar:** Gal 3:24 — `ὥστε ὁ νόμος παιδαγωγὸς ἡμῶν γέγονεν` (doctrinal summary with new subject).

**Family:** Mark 2:28; Rom 7:12, 13:2; 1 Cor 3:7, 14:22; 2 Cor 4:12, 5:17; Gal 3:9.

#### 4. Rhetorical inferential conclusion

ὥστε + retrospective argument summary or rhetorical question; no narrative action to inherit.

**Canonical exemplar:** Gal 4:16 — `ὥστε ἐχθρὸς ὑμῶν γέγονα ἀληθεύων ὑμῖν` (rhetorical question framing makes inferential function explicit).

**Family:** 1 Cor 7:38; 2 Cor 2:7; Gal 4:7.

## How we know the 3-condition test works

Phase A sweep across all NT books used Grep + manual evaluation of ~30 ὥστε-infinitive candidates. The 3-condition test (≤8, co-ref, no camera shift) correctly identified **20 clean merges** and correctly excluded **all illative cases** and all word-count-exceeded cases under adversarial review (task `a894941c72f5cc4e2`, 2026-05-11).

The 22 illatives identified in Phase C are encoded in the validator's `_ILLATIVE_KNOWN` set; they emit `SPLIT-MAINTAINED` directly without re-litigation. Future ὥστε occurrences exhibiting any of the four pattern-cluster signatures should be classified as illative without per-instance re-evaluation.
