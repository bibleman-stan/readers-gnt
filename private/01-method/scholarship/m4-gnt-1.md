# M4-GNT-1 — Grammatical Grounding

Scholarly substrate for canon §3.18 (Subject-Orphan Predicate Completion,
GNT instantiation). The operational rule lives in the canon entry; this
document carries the grammatical-grounding citations, rationale, and
empirical-pattern evidence that the rule-template forbids inline.

## Primary discourse-linguistic source

**Runge §14 — Left-Dislocations** (entire chapter; Runge 2010,
*A Discourse Grammar of the Greek New Testament*, Lexham).
Runge surveys the NT-grammarian aliases for this construction
(cleft / hanging nominative / pendent nominative / casus pendens /
independent nominative — §14.1) and grounds it in Chafe's cross-linguistic
"one new concept at a time" constraint (§14.2). M4-GNT-1 IS Runge's
left-dislocation rendered as a colometric rule.

### Three functional sub-uses (Runge §14.3)

- **§14.3.1 STREAMLINING** — introduces a complex entity in one clause
  not two; pronominal trace in unmarked post-verb position (<20% of NT
  instances).
- **§14.3.2 PROCESSING FUNCTION** — pronominal trace in clause-initial
  P1/P2 frame position to signal end-of-dislocation; entity complex
  enough to warrant the aid.
- **§14.3.3 DISCOURSE-PRAGMATIC FUNCTION** — pronominal trace creates
  thematic highlighting of the topic (majority of NT usage). The
  pronoun's redundancy IS what produces the rhetorical effect.

All three sub-uses receive the same colometric treatment under M4-GNT-1
(subject NP + matrix predicate = one ATU). The taxonomic distinction
is colometrically inert but downstream-rhetorically relevant: R27
authorial-style and §8 Marked Word Order interact with sub-use #3
(discourse-pragmatic highlighting) in particular.

## Classical / philological grounding

- **BDF §466 — Pendent Nominative.** Primary scholarly source for the
  construction as a classical category (Blass-Debrunner-Funk 1961).
- **Wallace, "Nominative Case — Independent Nominative"** (Wallace 1996,
  *Greek Grammar Beyond the Basics*). Modern complement to BDF with
  traditional terminology framing.
- **Burton 1903** has NO dedicated treatment of this construction.
  Burton's monograph scope is verbal syntax (moods / tenses / infinitives
  / participles); pendent constructions are out of scope. Burton is NOT
  a substrate for M4-GNT-1.

## Why this construction warrants its own rule

The GNT canon's pre-existing rules handle complement integrity (R10
ὅτι-complements, §3.5 Period Test), genitive-absolute own-line (R19),
and participial discipline (R20) — but did not operationally codify
subject→predicate integrity, the dual of R10's predicate→complement
integrity. The atomic-thought test in canon §1 served as foundational
principle but was enforced editorially, not mechanically. M4-GNT-1
closes that gap by giving the subject→predicate orphan pattern its
own mechanical detector.

## Empirical pattern

Corpus survey identified ~15-20 clean Cat A merges across the NT
(after applying all 5 G-exclusions + 6 universal exclusions to ~149
surface-pattern candidates). The cleanest clusters:

- **Household-code** — Col 3:18-22, Eph 5:25, Eph 6:1-9, 1 Pet 5:5.
- **Biographical-introduction** — Acts 16:14, Matt 1:19.
- **Deity-NP-with-appositive** — 1 Thess 4:16, Rev 22:6.

Acts 19:24 (G1 attributive participle) and Luke 1:38 (G2 verbless
nominal-sentence) are confirmed exclusions by structural analysis.
