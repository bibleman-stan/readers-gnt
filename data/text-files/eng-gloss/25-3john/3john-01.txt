1:1
The elder to Gaius the beloved,
whom I love in truth.

1:2
Beloved,
concerning all things I pray that you may prosper and be in good health,
just as your soul prospers.

1:3
For I rejoiced greatly when brothers came and testified to your truth,
just as you walk in truth.

1:4
I have no greater joy than this,
that I hear my children are walking in the truth.

1:5
Beloved,
you act faithfully in whatever you do for the brothers
and especially for strangers,

1:6
who have testified to your love before the church,
whom you will do well to send on their way worthily of God;

1:7
for they went out for the sake of the Name,
accepting nothing from the Gentiles.

1:8
We therefore ought to support such people,
so that we may become fellow workers with the truth.

1:9
I wrote something to the church;
but Diotrephes, who loves to be first among them, does not receive us.

1:10
For this reason, if I come,
I will call attention to the deeds he is doing,
talking nonsense about us with evil words;
and not satisfied with these things, he himself does not receive the brothers,
and he hinders those who want to
and throws them out of the church.

1:11
Beloved,
do not imitate what is evil but what is good.
The one who does good is from God;
the one who does evil has not seen God.

1:12
Demetrius has received testimony from everyone and from the truth itself;
and we also testify,
and you know that our testimony is true.

1:13
I had many things to write to you,
but I do not want to write to you with ink and pen;

1:14
but I hope to see you soon,
and we will speak face to face.

1:15
Peace to you.
The friends greet you.
Greet the friends by name.
