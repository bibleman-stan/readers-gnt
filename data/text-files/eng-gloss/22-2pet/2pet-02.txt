2:1
But there arose also false prophets among the people,
as there will also be false teachers among you,
who will secretly bring in destructive heresies,
even denying the Master who bought them,
bringing upon themselves swift destruction.

2:2
And many will follow their sensual ways,
because of whom the way of truth will be blasphemed.

2:3
And in greed they will exploit you with fabricated words;
their condemnation from long ago is not idle,
and their destruction is not asleep.

2:4
For if God did not spare angels when they sinned,
but cast them down to Tartarus with chains of gloom and handed them over,
being kept for judgment,

2:5
and did not spare the ancient world,
but preserved Noah, a herald of righteousness, with seven others
when he brought a flood on the world of the ungodly,

2:6
and condemned the cities of Sodom and Gomorrah to destruction by reducing them to ashes,
having made them an example of what is coming to the ungodly,

2:7
and rescued righteous Lot, who was worn down by the sensual conduct of lawless men—

2:8
for that righteous man, dwelling among them, tormented his righteous soul day after day by what he saw and heard through their lawless deeds—

2:9
the Lord knows how to rescue the godly from trial,
and to keep the unrighteous under punishment until the day of judgment,

2:10
and especially those who walk after the flesh in defiling desire
and despise authority.
Bold and self-willed,
they do not tremble to blaspheme the glorious ones,

2:11
whereas angels, though greater in strength and power, do not bring a blasphemous judgment against them before the Lord.

2:12
But these, like irrational animals, creatures of instinct born for capture and destruction, blaspheming in matters about which they are ignorant,
will also be destroyed in their own destruction,

2:13
suffering wrong as the wage of their wrongdoing.
They count it pleasure to revel in the daytime;
they are stains and blemishes,
reveling in their deceptions
while they feast together with you,

2:14
having eyes full of adultery
and insatiable for sin,
enticing unstable souls,
having hearts trained in greed,
accursed children,

2:15
having forsaken the straight way, they have gone astray,
having followed the way of Balaam the son of Bosor,
who loved the wages of wrongdoing

2:16
but received a rebuke for his own transgression;
a mute donkey, speaking with a human voice,
restrained the madness of the prophet.

2:17
These men are waterless springs and mists
driven by a storm,
for whom the gloom of darkness has been reserved.

2:18
For speaking arrogant words of vanity, they entice with fleshly desires and sensuality
those who are barely escaping from those who live in error,

2:19
promising them freedom,
while they themselves are slaves of corruption;
for by whatever a person is overcome,
to that he is also enslaved.

2:20
For if, after escaping the defilements of the world
through the knowledge of the Lord and Savior Jesus Christ,
they are again entangled in them and overcome,
the last state has become worse for them than the first.

2:21
For it would have been better for them
not to have known the way of righteousness
than, having known it, to turn back from the holy commandment delivered to them.

2:22
What the true proverb says has happened to them:
"A dog returns to its own vomit,"
and, "A sow after washing returns to wallowing in the mire."
