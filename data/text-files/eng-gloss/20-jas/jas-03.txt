3:1
Do not let many of you become teachers, my brothers,
knowing that we will receive a stricter judgment;

3:2
for we all stumble in many ways.
If anyone does not stumble in what he says,
he is a perfect man,
able to bridle the whole body as well.

3:3
Now if we put bits into the mouths of horses
so that they obey us,
we guide their whole body as well.

3:4
Look also at ships,
being so great and driven by strong winds, they are guided by a very small rudder
wherever the impulse of the pilot directs;

3:5
so also the tongue is a small member
and yet boasts great things.
See how great a forest so small a fire sets ablaze;

3:6
and the tongue is a fire;
the world of unrighteousness — the tongue is set among our members,
staining the whole body
and setting on fire the course of life
and being set on fire by hell.

3:7
For every species of beasts and birds, of reptiles and sea creatures,
is tamed and has been tamed by the human species;

3:8
but the tongue no human being can tame;
a restless evil,
full of deadly poison.

3:9
With it we bless the Lord and Father,
and with it we curse people
who are made in the likeness of God;

3:10
from the same mouth come blessing and cursing.
My brothers, it ought not
to be this way.

3:11
Does a spring pour forth from the same opening both fresh and bitter water?

3:12
Can it be, my brothers,
that a fig tree produces olives, or a vine figs?
Neither can salt water produce fresh water.

3:13
Who is wise and understanding among you?
Let him show by his good conduct his works in the gentleness of wisdom.

3:14
But if you have bitter jealousy and selfish ambition in your hearts,
do not boast
and lie against the truth.

3:15
This is not the wisdom that comes down from above,
but is earthly, natural, demonic;

3:16
for where jealousy and selfish ambition exist,
there is disorder and every vile practice.

3:17
But the wisdom from above is first pure,
then peaceable,
gentle,
open to reason,
full of mercy and good fruits,
impartial,
sincere;

3:18
and the fruit of righteousness is sown in peace
by those who make peace.
