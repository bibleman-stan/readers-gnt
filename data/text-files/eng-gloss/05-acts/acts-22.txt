22:1
Brothers and fathers,
hear my defense which I now make to you.

22:2
And when they heard that he was addressing them in the Hebrew language they became even more quiet.
And he said:

22:3
I am a Jewish man,
born in Tarsus of Cilicia,
but brought up in this city
at the feet of Gamaliel,
instructed according to the strict manner of our fathers' law,
being zealous for God
just as all of you are this day,

22:4
and I persecuted this Way to the death,
binding and delivering to prison both men and women,

22:5
as the high priest and all the council of elders can testify about me;
from whom also I received letters to the brothers and I was journeying to Damascus
to bring those who were there in bonds to Jerusalem
so that they might be punished.

22:6
And it happened as I was on my way and approaching Damascus
that about noon suddenly a great light from heaven flashed around me,

22:7
and I fell to the ground
and heard a voice saying to me:
why are you persecuting me?

22:8
And I answered:
Who are you, Lord?
And he said to me:
I am Jesus the Nazarene whom you are persecuting.

22:9
And those who were with me saw the light
but did not hear the voice of the one speaking to me.

22:10
And I said:
What shall I do, Lord?
And the Lord said to me:
Rise and go to Damascus,
and there you will be told about everything that has been appointed for you to do.

22:11
And since I could not see because of the brightness of that light,
I was led by the hand by those who were with me and came into Damascus.

22:12
And a man named Ananias, devout according to the law,
well spoken of by all the Jews who lived there,

22:13
came to me and standing beside me said:
Brother Saul,
receive your sight;
and in that very hour I looked up at him.

22:14
And he said:
The God of our fathers has appointed you
to know his will
and to see the Righteous One
and to hear a voice from his mouth,

22:15
because you will be a witness for him to all people
of what you have seen and heard.

22:16
And now why do you delay?
Rise and be baptized and wash away your sins
calling on his name.

22:17
And it happened when I returned to Jerusalem
and was praying in the temple
that I fell into a trance

22:18
and saw him saying to me:
Hurry and get out of Jerusalem quickly,
because they will not accept your testimony about me.

22:19
And I
Lord,
said: they themselves know that I was imprisoning and beating in the synagogues
those who believe in you;

22:20
and when the blood of your witness Stephen was being shed,
I myself was standing by and approving
and guarding the garments of those who were killing him.

22:21
And he said to me:
Go,
for I will send you far away to the Gentiles.

22:22
And they listened to him up to this word
and then they raised their voices, saying:
Away from the earth with such a man,
for he should not be allowed to live!

22:23
And as they were crying out
and throwing off their cloaks
and flinging dust into the air,

22:24
the tribune ordered him to be brought into the barracks,
saying that he should be examined by flogging
so that he might find out why they were shouting at him like this.

22:25
But when they had stretched him out with the straps
Paul said to the centurion who was standing by:
Is it lawful for you to flog a man who is a Roman citizen and uncondemned?

22:26
And when the centurion heard this he went to the tribune and reported, saying:
What are you about to do?
For this man is a Roman.

22:27
And the tribune came and said to him:
Tell me, are you a Roman?
And he said: Yes.

22:28
And the tribune answered:
I acquired this citizenship for a large sum.
And Paul said:
But I was born a citizen.

22:29
So immediately those who were about to examine him withdrew from him;
and the tribune also was afraid
having realized that he was a Roman and that he had bound him.

22:30
And on the next day, wanting to know for certain
what he was being accused of by the Jews,
he released him,
and ordered the chief priests and all the council to assemble,
and bringing Paul down he set him before them.
