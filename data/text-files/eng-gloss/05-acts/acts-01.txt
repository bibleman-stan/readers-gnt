1:1
The first account I composed about all things,
O Theophilus,
which Jesus began both to do and to teach

1:2
until the day when
having commanded the apostles
through the Holy Spirit
whom he had chosen
he was taken up.

1:3
To whom he also presented himself alive after his suffering
by many proofs,
appearing to them over forty days
and speaking the things concerning the kingdom of God.

1:4
And gathering them together he commanded them,
not to depart from Jerusalem,
but to wait for the promise of the Father which you heard from me:

1:5
that John indeed baptized with water,
but you will be baptized in the Holy Spirit not many days from now.

1:6
So those who had gathered together were asking him saying:
Lord,
are you at this time restoring the kingdom to Israel?

1:7
And he said to them:
It is not for you to know times or seasons
which the Father has set by his own authority,

1:8
but you will receive power
when the Holy Spirit has come upon you,
and you will be my witnesses both in Jerusalem and in all Judea
and Samaria and to the end of the earth.

1:9
And having said these things
while they were watching
he was lifted up
and a cloud took him from their sight.

1:10
And as they were gazing into heaven
while he was going,
behold two men stood beside them in white clothing,

1:11
who also said:
Men of Galilee,
why do you stand looking into heaven?
This Jesus who was taken up from you into heaven
will come in the same way as you saw him going into heaven.

1:12
Then they returned to Jerusalem from the mount called Olivet,
which is near Jerusalem a Sabbath day's journey away.

1:13
And when they entered,
they went up to the upper room where they were staying,
both Peter and John
and James
and Andrew,
Philip and Thomas,
Bartholomew and Matthew,
James son of Alphaeus and Simon the Zealot
and Judas son of James.

1:14
and with his brothers. All these were devoting themselves with one accord to prayer together with the women and Mary the mother of Jesus

1:15
And in those days Peter stood up in the midst of the brothers and said
(the crowd of names together was about one hundred twenty):

1:16
Brothers,
it was necessary for the Scripture to be fulfilled which the Holy Spirit spoke beforehand through the mouth of David
concerning Judas who became a guide to those who arrested Jesus,

1:17
because he was numbered among us
and received the allotment of this ministry.

1:18
Now this man acquired a field with the wages of unrighteousness,
and falling headlong he burst open in the middle,
and all his intestines poured out.

1:19
And it became known to all who dwell in Jerusalem,
so that field was called in their own language Akeldama,
that is, Field of Blood.

1:20
For it is written in the book of Psalms:
Let his dwelling place become desolate
and let there be no one dwelling in it,
and: Let another take his office.

1:21
Therefore of the men who accompanied us the whole time
that the Lord Jesus went in and out among us,

1:22
beginning from the baptism of John until the day he was taken up from us,
one of these must become a witness of his resurrection with us.

1:23
And they put forward two, Joseph called Barsabbas who was surnamed Justus, and Matthias.

1:24
And praying they said:
You, Lord, knower of all hearts,
show which one of these two you have chosen

1:25
to take the place of this ministry and apostleship
from which Judas turned aside to go to his own place.

1:26
And they cast lots for them,
and the lot fell on Matthias,
and he was numbered together with the eleven apostles.
