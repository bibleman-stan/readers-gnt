7:1
Since we have these promises,
beloved,
let us cleanse ourselves from every defilement of body and spirit,
bringing holiness to completion in the fear of God.

7:2
Make room for us in your hearts.
We have wronged no one,
we have corrupted no one, we have taken advantage of no one.

7:3
I do not say this to condemn you,
for I said before that you are in our hearts, to die together and to live together.

7:4
I have great confidence in you;
I have great pride in you.
I am filled with comfort; I am overflowing with joy in all our affliction.

7:5
For even when we came into Macedonia, our bodies had no rest,
but we were afflicted at every turn --
fighting without and fear within.

7:6
But God, who comforts the downcast, comforted us by the coming of Titus,

7:7
and not only by his coming but also by the comfort
with which he was comforted by you,
as he told us of your longing, your mourning, your zeal for me,
so that I rejoiced still more.

7:8
For even if I made you grieve with my letter,
I do not regret it --
though I did regret it (for I see that that letter grieved you, though only for a while).

7:9
As it is,
I rejoice, not because you were grieved,
but because you were grieved into repentance. For you were grieved in a godly way,
so that you suffered no loss through us.

7:10
For godly grief produces a repentance that leads to salvation without regret,
but worldly grief produces death.

7:11
For behold, this very godly grief
what earnestness it has produced in you,
rather, what defense,
rather, what indignation,
rather, what fear,
rather, what longing,
rather, what zeal,
rather, what punishment!
At every point you have proved yourselves
innocent in this matter.

7:12
So although I wrote to you,
it was not for the sake of the one who did wrong,
nor for the sake of the one who was wronged,
but in order that your earnestness
for us to you might be revealed in the sight of God.

7:13
Therefore we are comforted.
And besides our own comfort, we rejoiced still more at the joy of Titus,
because his spirit has been refreshed by all of you.

7:14
For whatever boasting I had made to him about you,
I was not put to shame.
But just as everything we said to you was true,
so also our boasting before Titus has proved true.

7:15
And his affection for you is even greater,
as he remembers the obedience of you all,
how you received him with fear and trembling.

7:16
I rejoice,
because I have complete confidence in you.
