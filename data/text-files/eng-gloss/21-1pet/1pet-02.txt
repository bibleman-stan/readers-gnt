2:1
Therefore, putting away all malice
and all deceit
and hypocrisies and envies
and all slanders,

2:2
as newborn infants, long for the pure spiritual milk,
that by it you may grow up into salvation,

2:3
if indeed you have tasted that the Lord is good.

2:4
Coming to him, a living stone,
rejected indeed by men
but in God's sight chosen and precious,

2:5
you yourselves also, as living stones, are being built up
as a spiritual house into a holy priesthood,
to offer spiritual sacrifices acceptable to God through Jesus Christ.

2:6
For it stands in Scripture:
Behold, I lay in Zion a chosen, precious cornerstone,
and whoever believes in him shall never be put to shame.

2:7
Therefore the honor is for you who believe;
but for those who disbelieve, the stone which the builders rejected,
this has become the head of the corner,

2:8
and a stone of stumbling and a rock of offense;
they stumble
because they disobey the word;
to this also they were appointed.

2:9
But you are a chosen race,
a royal priesthood,
a holy nation,
a people for his possession,
that you may proclaim the excellencies of him who called you out of darkness
into his marvelous light;

2:10
who once were not a people
but now are the people of God,
who had not received mercy but now have received mercy.

2:11
Beloved,
I urge you as sojourners and exiles
to abstain from fleshly desires,
which wage war against the soul;

2:12
keeping your conduct honorable among the Gentiles, so that when they speak against you as evildoers, they may, observing your good works, glorify God on the day of visitation.

2:13
Be subject to every human institution for the Lord's sake:
whether to the king as supreme,

2:14
or to governors as sent by him
for the punishment of evildoers and the praise of those who do good.

2:15
(For such is the will of God,
that by doing good you should silence the ignorance of foolish men);

2:16
as free,
yet not using your freedom as a cover-up for evil,
but as servants of God.

2:17
Honor everyone,
love the brotherhood,
fear God,
honor the king.

2:18
Servants, being subject
with all fear to your masters,
not only to the good and gentle but also to the unjust.

2:19
For this is a gracious thing,
when mindful of God one endures sorrows
while suffering unjustly.

2:20
For what credit is it if, when you sin and are beaten for it, you endure?
But if, when you do good and suffer for it, you endure,
this is a gracious thing in the sight of God.

2:21
For to this you were called,
because Christ also suffered for you,
leaving you an example,
that you should follow in his steps;

2:22
who committed no sin,
nor was deceit found in his mouth;

2:23
who, when he was reviled, did not revile in return;
when he suffered, he did not threaten,
but entrusted himself to the one who judges justly;

2:24
who himself bore our sins
in his body on the tree,
that we, having died to sin, might live to righteousness;
by his wounds you have been healed.

2:25
For you were straying like sheep,
but you have now returned
to the Shepherd and Overseer of your souls.
