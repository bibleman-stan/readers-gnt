1:1
Peter, an apostle of Jesus Christ,
to the elect sojourners of the Dispersion in Pontus, Galatia, Cappadocia, Asia, and Bithynia,

1:2
according to the foreknowledge of God the Father, in the sanctification of the Spirit,
unto obedience and the sprinkling of the blood of Jesus Christ:
Grace to you
and may peace be multiplied.

1:3
Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ,
who according to his great mercy has caused us to be born again
into a living hope through the resurrection of Jesus Christ from the dead,

1:4
into an inheritance imperishable and undefiled and unfading,
kept in heaven for you

1:5
who by the power of God are being guarded through faith for a salvation ready
to be revealed in the last time.

1:6
In this you greatly rejoice,
though now for a little while, if necessary, you have been grieved by various trials,

1:7
so that the tested genuineness of your faith,
more precious than gold which perishes though tested by fire, may be found to result in praise and glory and honor at the revelation of Jesus Christ.

1:8
Whom having not seen you love,
in whom, though not now seeing him, you believe and rejoice with joy inexpressible and full of glory,

1:9
obtaining the outcome of your faith, the salvation of your souls.

1:10
Concerning this salvation the prophets searched
and inquired diligently, those who prophesied of the grace destined for you,

1:11
inquiring into what or what kind of time the Spirit of Christ within them was indicating,
testifying beforehand to the sufferings of Christ
and the glories that would follow;

1:12
to them it was revealed
that not to themselves but to you they were ministering these things,
which now have been announced to you through those who preached the gospel to you by the Holy Spirit
sent from heaven,
things into which angels long to look.

1:13
Therefore, girding up the loins of your mind, being fully sober, set your hope completely on the grace being brought to you at the revelation of Jesus Christ.

1:14
As children of obedience,
not conforming yourselves to the former desires in your ignorance,

1:15
but as the one who called you is holy, you yourselves also be holy in all your conduct,

1:16
because it is written:
You shall be holy,
for I am holy.

1:17
And if you call on as Father
the one who judges impartially according to each one's work,
conduct yourselves in fear throughout the time of your sojourning,

1:18
knowing that not with perishable things, silver or gold, were you redeemed
from the futile way of life handed down from your fathers,

1:19
but with the precious blood of Christ, as of a lamb without blemish and without spot,

1:20
foreknown indeed before the foundation of the world,
but revealed in the last times for your sake,

1:21
who through him are believers in God who raised him from the dead
and gave him glory,
so that your faith and hope are in God.

1:22
Having purified your souls by obedience to the truth
for sincere brotherly love,
love one another earnestly from the heart,

1:23
having been born again not from perishable seed
but from imperishable,
through the living and abiding word of God;

1:24
because all flesh is like grass, and all its glory like the flower of grass;
the grass withers,
and the flower falls,

1:25
but the word of the Lord abides forever.
And this is
the word that was preached to you.
