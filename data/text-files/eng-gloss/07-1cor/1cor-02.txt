2:1
And when I came to
brothers,
you,
I came not with superiority of speech or wisdom, proclaiming to you the testimony of God.

2:2
For I decided to know nothing among you except Jesus Christ and him crucified.

2:3
And I was in
weakness and in fear
and in much trembling when
I came to you,

2:4
and my speech and my preaching were not in persuasive
words of wisdom but in
demonstration of the Spirit and of power,

2:5
so that your faith would not rest on the wisdom of men
but on the power of God.

2:6
Yet among the mature we do speak wisdom,
but not the wisdom of this age, nor
of the rulers of this age, who are passing away.

2:7
But we speak the wisdom of God in a mystery,
the hidden wisdom, which God predestined before the ages for our glory,

2:8
which none of the rulers of this age understood, for if
they had understood it,
they would not have crucified the Lord of glory.

2:9
But as it is
written, "What no eye has seen
and no ear has heard, and
what has never entered the human heart, all
that God has prepared for those who love him."

2:10
For God has revealed it to us through the
Spirit, for the Spirit searches all
things, even the depths of God.

2:11
For who among men knows the things of a man except the spirit of the man that is in him? In
the same way no one knows the things of God except the Spirit of God.

2:12
Now we have received not the spirit of the world,
but the Spirit who is from God, so that
we might know the things freely given to us by God.

2:13
And we speak of these things not in words taught by human wisdom, but in
words taught by the Spirit, interpreting
spiritual things to spiritual people.

2:14
The natural person does not accept the things of the Spirit of God, for they
are foolishness to him, and he
is not able to understand them,
because they are spiritually discerned.

2:15
The spiritual person discerns all things, but he
himself is discerned by no one.

2:16
"For who has known the mind of the
Lord, that he may instruct him?"
But we have the mind of Christ.
