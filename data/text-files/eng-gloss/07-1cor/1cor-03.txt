3:1
And I,
brothers,
could not speak to you as spiritual people,
but as people of the flesh, as infants in Christ.

3:2
I gave you milk to drink,
not solid food, for you were not yet able to receive it.
And even now you are still not able,

3:3
for you are still fleshly. For where there is jealousy and strife among you,
are you not fleshly
and walking according to man?

3:4
For when someone says,
"I belong to Paul," and another
says, "I belong to Apollos,"
are you not merely human?

3:5
What then is Apollos? And what
is Paul?
Servants through whom you believed, and to each one as the Lord gave.

3:6
I planted, Apollos watered,
but God was causing the growth.

3:7
So neither the one who plants is anything,
nor the one who waters, but God who causes the growth.

3:8
Now the one who plants and the one who waters are one,
and each will receive his own reward according to his own labor.

3:9
For we are God's fellow workers;
you are God's field, God's building.

3:10
According to the grace of God given to me, like a skilled master builder I laid a foundation,
and another is building on it. But let each one
be careful how
he builds on it.

3:11
For no one can lay a foundation other than the one that is laid,
which is Jesus Christ.

3:12
Now if anyone builds on the foundation with gold, silver, precious stones, wood, hay, straw,

3:13
each one's work will become evident,
for the day will reveal it,
because it is revealed with fire, and the fire itself will test each one's work,
of what sort it is.

3:14
If anyone's work that he has built on the foundation remains,
he will receive a reward.

3:15
If anyone's work is burned up, he will suffer loss,
but he himself will be saved, yet so as through fire.

3:16
Do you not know that you are God's temple and that the Spirit of God dwells in you?

3:17
If anyone destroys God's temple,
God will destroy that person;
for the temple of God is holy,
which is what you are.

3:18
Let no one deceive himself.
If anyone among you thinks he is wise in this age,
let him become foolish
so that he may become wise.

3:19
For the wisdom of this world is foolishness before God. For it is written,
"He catches the wise in their craftiness."

3:20
And again, "The Lord knows that the thoughts of the wise
are futile."

3:21
So let no one boast in men,
for all things belong to you,

3:22
whether Paul
or Apollos
or Cephas
or the world
or life
or death
or things present
or things to come -- all things belong to you,

3:23
and you belong to Christ,
and Christ belongs to God.
