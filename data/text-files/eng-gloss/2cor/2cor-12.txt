12:1
Boasting is necessary.
It is not profitable, but I will go on to visions and revelations of the Lord.

12:2
I know a man in Christ who fourteen years ago --
whether in the body I do not know, or out of the body I do not know,
God knows -- was caught up to the third heaven.

12:3
And I know that this man --
whether in the body or apart from the body I do not know, God knows --

12:4
that he was caught up into paradise
and heard inexpressible words
which a man is not permitted to speak.

12:5
On behalf of such a man I will boast,
but on behalf of myself I will not boast, except in my weaknesses.

12:6
For if I wish to boast,
I will not be foolish,
for I will speak the truth. But I refrain,
so that no one may credit me with more than what he sees in me
or hears from me,

12:7
even because of the surpassing greatness of the revelations. Therefore,
so that I would not exalt myself,
there was given to me a thorn in the flesh,
a messenger of Satan, so that he might torment me,
so that I would not exalt myself.

12:8
Concerning this I pleaded with the Lord three times, that it might depart from me.

12:9
And he has said to me,
"My grace is sufficient for you,
for power is perfected in weakness."
Most gladly, therefore, I will rather boast
in my weaknesses,
so that the power of Christ
may dwell upon me.

12:10
Therefore I am well content with weaknesses, with insults, with hardships,
with persecutions and difficulties, for the sake of Christ;
for when I am weak,
then I am strong.

12:11
I have become foolish;
you yourselves compelled me.
For I ought to have been commended by you.
For in nothing
was I inferior to the most eminent apostles, even though I am nothing.

12:12
The signs of a true apostle were performed among you
with all perseverance, by signs and wonders and mighty works.

12:13
For in what respect were you treated as less than the rest of the churches,
except that I myself did not burden you?
Forgive me this wrong!

12:14
Look, for this third time I am ready to come to you,
and I will not be a burden;
for I do not seek what is yours
but you. For children are not obligated to save up for their parents,
but parents for their children.

12:15
I will most gladly spend and be utterly spent for your souls.
If I love you more abundantly,
am I loved less?

12:16
But be that as it may, I did not burden you;
nevertheless, being crafty, I caught you by deceit.

12:17
Surely I did not take advantage of you through any of those
I have sent to you?

12:18
I urged Titus and sent the brother with him.
Titus did not take advantage of you, did he?
Did we not walk in the same spirit?
Did we not walk in the same steps?

12:19
Have you been thinking all along that we are defending ourselves to you?
Before God in Christ we speak.
And all these things,
beloved,
are for your upbuilding.

12:20
For I am afraid that perhaps when I come
I may find you not as I wish,
and I may be found by you
not as you wish -- perhaps quarreling, jealousy, anger, selfish ambition,
slander, gossip, arrogance, disorder.

12:21
I am afraid that when I come again my God may humble me
before you, and I may mourn over many who have previously sinned and have
not repented of the impurity and sexual immorality and sensuality which they practiced.
