3:1
What then is the advantage of the Jew,
or what is the benefit of circumcision?

3:2
Much in every way.
For in the first place, they were entrusted with the oracles of God.

3:3
What then if some were unfaithful?
Will their unfaithfulness nullify the faithfulness of God?

3:4
By no means!
Let God be true,
and every person a liar,
as it is written:
"That you may be justified in your words
and prevail when you are judged."

3:5
But if our unrighteousness demonstrates the righteousness of God,
what shall we say?
Is God unjust who inflicts wrath?
(I am speaking in human terms.)

3:6
By no means!
For otherwise, how will God judge the world?

3:7
But if the truth of God has abounded through my falsehood to his glory,
why am I still being condemned as a sinner?

3:8
And why not say -- as we are slandered
and as some claim that we say --
"Let us do evil
so that good may come"?
Their condemnation is deserved.

3:9
What then? Are we any better?
Not at all, for we have already charged that both Jews and Greeks are all under sin,

3:10
as it is written:
"There is no one righteous, not even one;

3:11
there is no one who understands, there is no one who seeks God.

3:12
All have turned aside; together they have become worthless.
There is no one who does good,
there is not even one.

3:13
Their throat is an open grave; with their tongues they practice deceit.
The venom of asps is under their lips.

3:14
Their mouth is full of cursing and bitterness.

3:15
Their feet are swift to shed blood;

3:16
ruin and misery are in their paths,

3:17
and they have not known the way of peace.

3:18
There is no fear of God before their eyes."

3:19
Now we know that whatever the law says, it speaks to those in the law,
so that every mouth may be silenced
and the whole world may be held accountable to God.

3:20
For by works of law no flesh will be justified in his sight,
for through law comes the knowledge of sin.

3:21
But now apart from law the righteousness of God has been revealed,
being witnessed by the law and the Prophets,

3:22
the righteousness of God through faith in Jesus Christ for all who believe, for there is no distinction.

3:23
For all have sinned
and fall short of the glory of God,

3:24
being justified freely by his grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus,

3:25
whom God set forth as a propitiation through faith in his blood, as a demonstration of his righteousness,
because of the passing over of sins previously committed

3:26
in the forbearance of God, for the demonstration of his righteousness at the present time, so that he might be just
and the justifier of the one who has faith in Jesus.

3:27
Where then is boasting? It is excluded. By what kind of principle? Of works? No, but by a principle of faith.

3:28
For we maintain that a person is justified by faith apart from works of law.

3:29
Or is God the God of Jews only? Is he not also the God of Gentiles? Yes, of Gentiles also,

3:30
since God is one,
who will justify the circumcised by faith
and the uncircumcised through faith.

3:31
Do we then nullify the law through faith? By no means!
Rather, we uphold the law.
