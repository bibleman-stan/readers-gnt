7:1
Or do you not know,
brothers -- I am speaking to those who know Torah --
that the Torah is lord over a person for as long as he lives?

7:2
For the married woman is bound by Torah to her living husband;
but if her husband dies, she is released from the Torah concerning her husband.

7:3
So then, while her husband is living, she will be called an adulteress if she belongs to another man;
but if her husband dies, she is free from the Torah,
so that she is not an adulteress though she belongs to another man.

7:4
Therefore you also, brothers,
were put to death to the Torah through the body of Christ,
so that you might belong to another, to the one raised from the dead,
in order that we might bear fruit for God.

7:5
For when we were in the flesh,
the sinful passions aroused through the Torah were at work in our members,
bearing fruit for death.

7:6
But now we have been released from the Torah, having died to that which held us
bound, so that we serve in newness of the Spirit and not in oldness of the letter.

7:7
What shall we say
then? Is the Torah
sin? May it
never be! Rather, I would not have come to know sin except through
Torah; for I would not have known coveting if the Torah had not said, "You
shall not covet."

7:8
But sin, seizing the opportunity through the commandment, produced in me every kind of coveting;
for apart from Torah, sin is dead.

7:9
I was once alive apart from Torah;
but when the commandment came,
sin became alive,

7:10
and I died;
and this commandment, which was to result in life, was found to result in death.

7:11
For sin, seizing the opportunity through the commandment, deceived me
and through it killed me.

7:12
So then, the Torah is holy,
and the commandment is holy and righteous and good.

7:13
Therefore did that which is good become death to me?
May it never be!
Rather it was sin,
in order that sin might be shown to be sin
by effecting death through that which is good,
so that through the commandment sin would become utterly sinful.

7:14
For we know that the Torah is spiritual;
but I am of flesh, sold into bondage to sin.

7:15
For what I am producing,
I do not understand;
for what I want, this I do not practice,
but what I hate, this I do.

7:16
But if what I do not want, this I do, I
agree with the Torah that it is good.

7:17
So now, it is no longer I who
am producing it, but sin dwelling in me.

7:18
For I know that nothing good dwells in me, that is, in my flesh;
good is absent;
for the willing is present in me,
but the doing of what is good is not.

7:19
For the good that I want, I do not do;
but the evil that I do not want, this I practice.

7:20
But if what I do not want, this I do,
it is no longer I who am doing it,
but sin dwelling in me.

7:21
I find then this principle: for the one who wants to do good,
evil lies close at hand.

7:22
For I joyfully agree with the law of God in the inner man,

7:23
but I see a different law in my members, waging war against the law of my mind,
and making me a prisoner of the law of sin
which is in my members.

7:24
Wretched man that I am!
Who will deliver me from this body of death?

7:25
Thanks be to God through Jesus Christ our Lord!
So then, I myself with my mind serve the law of God,
but with my flesh I serve the law of sin.
