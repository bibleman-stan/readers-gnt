23:1
And the whole assembly of them rose up and led him before Pilate.

23:2
And they began to accuse him, saying:
We found this man perverting our nation
and forbidding the payment of taxes to Caesar
and saying that he himself is Christ, a king.

23:3
And Pilate asked him, saying:
Are you the King of the Jews?
And answering him he said:
You say so.

23:4
And Pilate said to the chief priests and the crowds:
I find no basis for a charge against this man.

23:5
But they kept pressing,
saying:
He stirs up the people,
teaching throughout all Judea,
having begun from Galilee even to here.

23:6
And Pilate, hearing this, asked whether the man was a Galilean.

23:7
And learning that he was from Herod's jurisdiction he sent him over to Herod,
who was himself also in Jerusalem in those days.

23:8
And Herod, seeing Jesus, was very glad,
for he had been wanting to see him for a long time
because of hearing about him,
and he was hoping to see some sign performed by him.

23:9
And he questioned him at considerable length.
But he answered him nothing.

23:10
And the chief priests and the scribes stood there
vehemently accusing him.

23:11
And treating him with contempt, Herod also, together with his soldiers, and mocking him, dressed him in splendid clothing and sent him back to Pilate.

23:12
And they became friends,
both Herod and Pilate with each other on that very day.
For before this they had been at enmity with each other.

23:13
And Pilate, calling together the chief priests and the rulers and the people,

23:14
said to them:
You brought this man to me as one who misleads the people,
and behold, I having examined him before you found nothing in this man deserving of a charge
of which you accuse him.

23:15
But neither did Herod, for he sent him back to us.
And behold, nothing deserving death has been done by him.

23:16
I will therefore punish him and release him.

23:18
But they all cried out together, saying:
Away with this man!
Release to us Barabbas—

23:19
who had been thrown into prison for a certain rebellion in the city and for murder.

23:20
And again Pilate addressed them,
wanting to release Jesus.

23:21
But they kept shouting, saying:
Crucify, crucify him!

23:22
And a third time he said to them:
Why, what evil has this man done?
I have found no basis for a death sentence in him.
I will therefore punish him and release him.

23:23
But they were insistent with loud voices
demanding that he be crucified,
and their voices prevailed.

23:24
And Pilate decided that their demand should be granted.

23:25
And he released the one who had been thrown into prison for rebellion and murder,
the one they were asking for,
but Jesus he handed over to their will.

23:26
And as they led him away,
seizing a certain Simon of Cyrene coming from the countryside, they placed the cross on him to carry behind Jesus.

23:27
And a great multitude of the people followed him, and of women
who were mourning and lamenting for him.

23:28
But turning to them, Jesus said:
Daughters of Jerusalem,
do not weep for me.
Rather weep for yourselves and for your children,

23:29
because behold, days are coming
in which they will say: Blessed are the barren and the wombs
that never bore
and the breasts that never nursed.

23:30
Then they will begin to say to the mountains:
Fall on us,
and to the hills: Cover us.

23:31
For if they do these things when the wood is green,
what will happen when it is dry?

23:32
And two others, who were criminals, were also led away with him to be put to death.

23:33
And when they came to the place called The Skull,
there they crucified him and the criminals,
one on the right,
and one on the left.

23:34
And Jesus was saying:
Father, forgive them,
for they do not know what they are doing.
And dividing his garments they cast lots.

23:35
And the people stood watching.
And the rulers also sneered, saying:
He saved others,
let him save himself,
if this is the Christ of God, the Chosen One.

23:36
And the soldiers also mocked him,
coming up and offering him sour wine ³⁷and saying:

23:37
If you are the King of the Jews, save yourself.

23:38
And there was also an inscription over him:
This is the King of the Jews.

23:39
And one of the criminals hanging there was blaspheming him, saying:
Are you not the Christ?
Save yourself and us.

23:40
But the other, answering, rebuked him and said:
Do you not even fear God,
since you are under the same sentence?

23:41
And we indeed justly, for we are receiving what we deserve
for what we did.
But this man has done nothing wrong.

23:42
And he was saying:
Jesus,
remember me
when you come into your kingdom.

23:43
And he said to him:
Truly I say to you,
today you will be with me in paradise.

23:44
And it was already about the sixth hour
and darkness came over the whole land until the ninth hour,

23:45
the sun having been eclipsed.
And the curtain of the temple was torn in the middle.

23:46
And crying out with a loud voice, Jesus said:
Father,
into your hands I commit my spirit.
And having said this he breathed his last.

23:47
And the centurion, seeing what had happened, glorified God, saying:
Certainly this man was righteous.

23:48
And all the crowds who had gathered for this spectacle, having seen the things that took place, returned home beating their breasts.

23:49
And all his acquaintances stood at a distance,
and the women who had followed him from Galilee,
seeing these things.

23:50
And behold, a man named Joseph, a council member, a good and righteous man—

23:51
this man had not consented to their plan and action— from Arimathea, a city of the Jews,
who was waiting for the kingdom of God.

23:52
This man went to Pilate and asked for the body of Jesus.

23:53
And taking it down he wrapped it in a linen cloth,
and placed him in a tomb cut from rock
where no one had ever yet been laid.

23:54
And it was the day of Preparation,
and the Sabbath was dawning.

23:55
And the women who had followed, who had come with him from Galilee,
saw the tomb and how his body was placed.

23:56
And returning they prepared spices and ointments.
And on the Sabbath they rested according to the commandment.
