15:1
Now all the tax collectors and sinners were drawing near to him to hear him.

15:2
And the Pharisees and the scribes were grumbling, saying:
This man receives sinners
and eats with them.

15:3
And he told them this parable, saying:

15:4
What man among you, having a hundred sheep and losing one of them, does not leave the ninety-nine in the wilderness
and go after the lost one until he finds it?

15:5
And finding it he places it on his shoulders, rejoicing.

15:6
And coming to his house he calls together his friends and neighbors, saying to them:
Rejoice with me
because I have found my sheep that was lost.

15:7
I tell you
that in the same way there will be joy in heaven over one sinner who repents
more than over ninety-nine righteous ones who have no need of repentance.

15:8
Or what woman, having ten silver coins,
if she loses one coin, does not
light a lamp and sweep the house
and search carefully until she finds it?

15:9
And finding it she calls together her friends and neighbors, saying: Rejoice
with me because
I have found the coin that I lost.

15:10
In the same way, I tell you,
there is joy before the angels of God over one sinner who repents.

15:11
And he said:
A certain man had two sons.

15:12
And the younger of them said to his father:
give me the share of the property that falls to me.
And he divided his estate between them.

15:13
And not many days later, gathering everything together, the younger son went on a journey to a distant country,
and there he squandered his property in reckless living.

15:14
And when he had spent everything a severe famine arose in
that country,
and he began to be in need.

15:15
And going out he attached himself to one of the citizens of that country,
and he sent him into his fields to feed pigs.

15:16
And he was longing to fill his stomach with the pods that the pigs were eating,
and no one was giving anything to him.

15:17
But coming to himself he said:
How many of my father's hired workers have more than enough bread,
but I am perishing here with hunger.

15:18
I will rise and go to my father and say to him:
I have sinned against heaven
Father,
and before you.

15:19
I am no longer worthy to be called your son.
Make me like one of your hired workers.

15:20
And rising he went to his own father.
But while he was still far off
his father saw him
and was filled with compassion
and running he fell on his neck
and kissed him.

15:21
And the son said to him:
Father,
I have sinned against heaven and before you.
I am no longer worthy to be called your son.

15:22
But the father said to his servants:
Quickly bring out the best robe
and put it on him,
and put a ring on his hand
and sandals on his feet.

15:23
And bring the fattened calf, slaughter it,
and let us eat and celebrate.

15:24
Because this son of mine was dead and has come to life,
he was lost and has been found.
And they began to celebrate.

15:25
Now his older son was in the field.
And as he came and drew near to the house,
he heard music and dancing.

15:26
And calling over one of the servants he asked what these things might be.

15:27
And he said to him:
Your brother has come,
and your father has slaughtered the fattened calf,
because he received him back in good health.

15:28
But he became angry and did not want to go in.
And his father came out and was pleading with him.

15:29
But answering he said to his father:
Look, so many years I have served you
and I never disobeyed your command,
yet you never gave me a young goat
so that I might celebrate with my friends.

15:30
But when this son of yours who has devoured your livelihood with prostitutes came,
you slaughtered for him the fattened calf.

15:31
But he said to him:
you are always with me,
and all that is mine is yours.

15:32
But it was necessary to celebrate and rejoice,
because this brother of yours was dead and has come to life,
and was lost and has been found.
