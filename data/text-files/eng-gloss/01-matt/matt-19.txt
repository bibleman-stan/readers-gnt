19:1
And it happened
when Jesus finished these words,
he departed from Galilee
and came to the region of Judea beyond the Jordan.

19:2
And great crowds followed him,
and he healed them there.

19:3
And Pharisees came to him testing him and saying:
Is it lawful for a man to divorce his wife for any cause?

19:4
And he answered and said:
Have you not read that the one who made them from the beginning made them male and female

19:5
and said:
For this reason a man will leave his father and mother
and be joined to his wife,
and the two will become one flesh?

19:6
So they are no longer two
but one flesh.
What therefore God has joined together, let no one separate.

19:7
They said to him:
Why then did Moses command to give a certificate of divorce and send her away?

19:8
He said to them:
Because of your hardness of heart Moses permitted you to divorce your wives,
but from the beginning it was not so.

19:9
And I say to you
that whoever divorces his wife, except for sexual immorality, and marries another commits adultery,
and the one who marries a divorced woman commits adultery.

19:10
The disciples said to him:
If such is the case of a man with his wife,
it is better not to marry.

19:11
But he said to them:
Not everyone can accept this word but only those to whom it is given.

19:12
For there are eunuchs who were born that way from their mother's womb,
and there are eunuchs who were made eunuchs by men,
and there are eunuchs who made themselves eunuchs for the sake of the kingdom of heaven.
The one who is able to accept this, let him accept it.

19:13
Then children were brought to him
so that he might lay his hands on them and pray;
and the disciples rebuked them.

19:14
But Jesus said:
Let the children come and do not hinder them from coming to me,
for the kingdom of heaven belongs to such as these.

19:15
And after laying his hands on them he went on from there.

19:16
And one came to him and said:
Teacher,
what good thing shall I do
so that I may have eternal life?

19:17
And he said to him:
Why do you ask me about what is good?
There is only one who is good.
But if you want to enter life, keep the commandments.

19:18
He said to him:
Which ones?
And Jesus said:
You shall not murder,
You shall not commit adultery,
You shall not steal,
You shall not bear false witness,

19:19
Honor your father and mother,
and: You shall love your neighbor as yourself.

19:20
The young man said to him:
All these I have kept;
what do I still lack?

19:21
Jesus said to him:
If you want to be perfect,
go sell your possessions
and give to the poor,
and you will have treasure in heaven,
and come follow me.

19:22
And when the young man heard this word he went away grieving,
for he had many possessions.

19:23
And Jesus said to his disciples:
Truly I say to you
that it is difficult for a rich person to enter the kingdom of heaven.

19:24
And again I say to you,
it is easier for a camel to pass through the eye of a needle
than for a rich person to enter the kingdom of God.

19:25
And when the disciples heard this they were greatly astonished saying:
Who then can be saved?

19:26
And Jesus looked at them and said:
With men this is impossible,
but with God all things are possible.

19:27
Then Peter answered and said to him:
Look, we have left everything and followed you;
what then will there be for us?

19:28
And Jesus said to them:
Truly I say to you
that you who have followed me, in the renewal of all things,
when the Son of Man sits on his glorious throne,
you also will sit on twelve thrones
judging the twelve tribes of Israel.

19:29
And everyone who has left houses or brothers or sisters or father or mother or wife or children or fields for the sake of my name,
will receive a hundred times as much
and will inherit eternal life.

19:30
But many who are first will be last
and the last first.
