20:1
For the kingdom of heaven is like a landowner
who went out early in the morning to hire workers for his vineyard.

20:2
And after agreeing with the workers for a denarius a day he sent them into his vineyard.

20:3
And going out about the third hour he saw others standing idle in the marketplace.

20:4
And he said to them:
You also go into the vineyard,
and whatever is fair I will give you.

20:5
And they went.
Going out again about the sixth and ninth hour he did the same.

20:6
And about the eleventh hour going out he
found others standing, and he said to them: Why
have you been standing here idle all day?

20:7
They said to him:
Because no one hired us.
He said to them:
You also go into the vineyard.

20:8
And when evening came the owner of the vineyard said to his manager:
Call the workers
and pay them their wages
beginning with the last and going to the first.

20:9
And those hired about the eleventh hour came and each received a denarius.

20:10
And when the first came they expected to receive more;
and they also each received a denarius.

20:11
And when they received it they grumbled against the landowner

20:12
saying:
These last ones worked only one hour,
and you have made them equal to us
who bore the burden of the day and the scorching heat.

20:13
But he answered one of them and said:
Friend,
I am not wronging you;
did you not agree with me for a denarius?

20:14
Take what is yours and go;
but I wish to give to this last one the same as to you.

20:15
Am I not permitted to do what I wish with what is mine?
Or is your eye envious
because I am generous?

20:16
So the last will be first
and the first last.

20:17
And as Jesus was going up to Jerusalem he took the twelve disciples aside,
and on the way he said to them:

20:18
Look, we are going up to Jerusalem,
and the Son of Man will be delivered to the chief priests and scribes,
and they will condemn him to death,

20:19
and they will deliver him to the Gentiles
to mock
and flog
and crucify,
and on the third day he will be raised.

20:20
Then the mother of the sons of Zebedee came to him with her sons
bowing down and asking something from him.

20:21
And he said to her:
What do
you want?
She
said to him: Say that these two sons
of mine may sit one
at your right hand and one
at your left in your kingdom.

20:22
And Jesus answered and said:
You do not know what you are asking;
are you able to drink the cup that I am about to drink?
They said to him:
We are able.

20:23
He said to them:
My cup you will drink,
but to sit at my right and at my left
is not mine to give,
but it is for those for whom it has been prepared by my Father.

20:24
And when the ten heard this they were indignant with the two brothers.

20:25
But Jesus called them to himself and said:
You know that the rulers of the nations lord it over them
and the great ones exercise authority over them.

20:26
It shall not be so among you;
but whoever wants to become great among you
shall be your servant,

20:27
and whoever wants to be first among you
shall be your slave;

20:28
just as the Son of Man did not come to be served
but to serve
and to give his life as a ransom for many.

20:29
And as they were going out from Jericho a large crowd followed him.

20:30
And two blind men sitting beside the road, hearing that Jesus was
Lord,
mercy on us, Son of David.

20:31
And the crowd rebuked them
to be silent;
but they cried out all the more saying:
Lord,
have mercy on us, Son of David.

20:32
And Jesus stopped and called them and said:
What do you want me to do for you?

20:33
They said to him:
Lord,
that our eyes may be opened.

20:34
And moved with compassion Jesus touched their eyes,
and immediately they received their sight
and followed him.
