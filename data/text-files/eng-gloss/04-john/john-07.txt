7:1
And after these things Jesus was walking in Galilee,
for he did not want to walk in Judea,
because the Jews were seeking to kill him.

7:2
Now the feast of the Jews, the Feast of Tabernacles, was near.

7:3
So his brothers said to him:
Leave here
and go into Judea,
so that your disciples also will see your works that you are doing.

7:4
For no one does anything in secret
and himself seeks to be known publicly.
If you do these things,
show yourself to the world.

7:5
For not even his brothers were believing in him.

7:6
So Jesus says to them:
My time has not yet arrived,
but your time is always ready.

7:7
The world is not able to hate you,
but it hates me,
because I testify about it that its works are evil.

7:8
You go up to the feast.
I am not going up
to this feast,
because my time has not yet been fulfilled.

7:9
Having said these things he remained in Galilee.

7:10
But when his brothers went up to the feast,
then he also went up, not openly
but as if in secret.

7:11
So the Jews were seeking him at the feast
and were saying:
Where is that one?

7:12
And there was much grumbling about him among the crowds.
Some were saying: He is a good man.
But others were saying:
No, rather he deceives the crowd.

7:13
Yet no one was speaking openly about him because of fear of the Jews.

7:14
But already in the middle of the feast Jesus went up to the temple and was teaching.

7:15
So the Jews were marveling saying:
How does this man know letters without having studied?

7:16
So Jesus answered them
and said:
My teaching is not mine
but belongs to the one who sent me.

7:17
If anyone wants to do his will, he will know about the teaching whether it is from God
or whether I speak from myself.

7:18
The one who speaks from himself seeks his own glory;
but the one who seeks the glory of the one who sent him,
this one is true
and there is no unrighteousness in him.

7:19
Did not Moses give you the law?
And none of you keeps the law.
Why do you seek to kill me?

7:20
The crowd answered:
You have a demon.
Who is seeking to kill you?

7:21
Jesus answered
and said to them:
One work I did
and you all marvel.

7:22
For this reason Moses gave you circumcision—
not
that it is from Moses
but from the fathers—
and on a Sabbath you circumcise a man.

7:23
If a man receives circumcision on a Sabbath, so
that the law of Moses may not be broken,
are you angry
with me because I made a whole man well on a Sabbath?

7:24
Do not judge by appearances,
but judge with right judgment.

7:25
So some of the people of Jerusalem were saying:
Is this not the one they are seeking to kill?

7:26
And look, he speaks openly
and they say nothing to him.
Could it be that the rulers truly know that this is the Christ?

7:27
But this one we know where he is
from. Yet when the
Christ comes no
one knows where he is from.

7:28
So Jesus cried out in the temple while teaching
and saying:
You both know me
and you know where I am from.
And I have not come from myself,
but the one who sent me is true,
whom you do not know.

7:29
I know him, because
I am from him and
he sent me.

7:30
So they were seeking to seize him,
and no one laid a hand on him,
because his hour had not yet come.

7:31
But many from the crowd believed in him, and were
saying: When
the Christ
comes will
he perform more signs than
this one has performed?

7:32
The Pharisees heard
the crowd grumbling these things about him,
and the chief priests and the Pharisees sent officers
to arrest him.

7:33
So Jesus said:
Yet a little while I am with you
and then I go to the one who sent me.

7:34
You will seek me
and will not find me,
and where I am
you are not able to come.

7:35
So the Jews said to one another:
Where is this one about to go
that we will not find him?
Surely not to the Dispersion among the Greeks
is he about to go
and teach the Greeks?

7:36
What is this word
that he said:
You will seek me
and will not find me,
and where I am
you are not able to come?

7:37
Now on the last day, the great day of the feast, Jesus stood
and cried out saying:
If anyone thirsts
let him come to me and drink.

7:38
The one who believes in me, as the scripture said,
rivers of living water will flow from within him.

7:39
Now he said this about the Spirit whom were
about to receive those
who believed in him; for the
Spirit was not yet given, because
Jesus was not yet glorified.

7:40
So some from the crowd, having heard these
words, were saying: This is truly the Prophet.

7:41
Others were saying:
This is the Christ.
But others were saying:
Surely the Christ does not come from Galilee, does he?

7:42
Has not the scripture said that from the seed of
David, and from Bethlehem the village
where David was,
the Christ comes?

7:43
So a division arose in the crowd because of him.

7:44
And some of them wanted to seize him,
but no one laid hands on him.

7:45
So the officers came to the chief priests and Pharisees,
and those ones said to them:
Why did you not bring him?

7:46
The officers answered:
Never has a person spoken like this.

7:47
So the Pharisees answered them:
Have you also been deceived?

7:48
Has any of the rulers believed in him
or any of the Pharisees?

7:49
But this crowd that does not know the law, they are accursed.

7:50
Nicodemus says to them, the one who had come to him before,
being one of them:

7:51
Our law does not judge a man unless it first hears from him
and knows what he does, does it?

7:52
They answered
and said to him:
You are not from Galilee too, are you?
Search
and see that no prophet arises from Galilee.

7:53
[And each one went to his own house.
