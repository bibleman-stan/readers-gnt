1:1
Paul a prisoner of Christ Jesus
and Timothy the [our] brother
To Philemon the beloved and fellow worker of us

1:2
and to Apphia the our sister and to Archippus the fellow soldier of us
and to the at [the] house of you church:

1:3
Grace to you and peace from God Father of us and [the] Lord Jesus Christ.

1:4
I thank the God of mine always
mention of you making upon the prayers of mine,

1:5
hearing of your the love and the faith
that you have toward the Lord Jesus and toward all the saints,

1:6
so that the fellowship of the faith of you effective may become in [the] acknowledgment of every good [thing] that [is] in us in Christ

1:7
Joy for great I have and encouragement by occasion of the love of you,
because the hearts of the saints have been refreshed through you,
brother.

1:8
Therefore much in Christ boldness having to order you what [is] befitting

1:9
for the sake of the love rather I exhort [you],
such a one being as Paul aged now then also a prisoner of Christ Jesus;

1:10
I exhort you for the my child
whom I have begotten in the chains of mine

1:11
the once to you useless now however both to you and to me myself useful,

1:12
whom I have sent back to you now in person — who is the my very heart —

1:13
whom I myself was wishing with myself to keep,
so that on behalf of you me he may serve in the chains of the gospel;

1:14
Apart from however the your consent no [thing] I wished to do,
so that not as according to necessity the good of you may be but according to willingness.

1:15
Perhaps for because of this he was separated [from you] for a time,
so that eternally him you may possess;

1:16
no longer as a slave but above a slave, a brother beloved, especially to me myself,
how much however more to you both in [the] flesh and in [the] Lord?

1:17
If therefore me you consider a partner,
do yourself receive him as me myself.

1:18
If however in any way he has wronged you or he owes [you],
this to me myself do charge.

1:19
I myself Paul did write the with my own hand, I myself will repay [it];
that not I may say to you
that even yourself to me you owe also.

1:20
Yes, brother,
I myself from you I would have profit in [the] Lord;
do refresh my the heart in Christ.

1:21
Having been persuaded of the obedience of you I write to you knowing
that even above what [things] I say you will do.

1:22
At the same time now also do prepare for me a lodging;
I hope for that through the prayers of you I will be granted to you.

1:23
He greets you Epaphras the fellow prisoner of mine in Christ Jesus,

1:24
[as do] Mark,
Aristarchus,
Demas,
[and] Luke, the fellow workers of mine.

1:25
The grace of the Lord of us Jesus Christ [be] with the spirit of you.
