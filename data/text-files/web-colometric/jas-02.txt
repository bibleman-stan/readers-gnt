2:1
My brothers, do not hold the faith of our Lord Jesus Christ of glory with favoritism.

2:2
For if a man with a gold ring in splendid clothing comes into your assembly,
and a poor person in shabby clothing also comes in,

2:3
and you look with favor on the one wearing the splendid clothing
and say:
You sit here in a good place,
and to the poor person you say:
You stand
or sit there under my footstool,

2:4
have you not made distinctions among yourselves
and become judges with evil reasoning?

2:5
Listen, my beloved brothers.
Did not God choose the poor of the world to be rich
in faith and heirs of the kingdom
which he promised to those who love him?

2:6
But you have dishonored the poor person.
Do not the rich oppress you,
and do they themselves not drag you into courts?

2:7
Do they not blaspheme the good name that was invoked over you?

2:8
If indeed you fulfill the royal law according to the Scripture
You shall love your neighbor as yourself, you do well;

2:9
but if you show favoritism,
you commit sin, being convicted by the law as transgressors.

2:10
For whoever keeps the whole law
but stumbles in one point, has become guilty of all.

2:11
For the one who said: Do not commit adultery
also said: Do not murder;
and if you do not commit adultery but you do murder,
you have become a transgressor of the law.

2:12
So speak
and so act
as those who are going to be judged by the law of freedom.

2:13
For judgment is merciless to the one who has shown no mercy; mercy triumphs over judgment.

2:14
What good is it, my brothers, if someone claims to have faith
but has no works?
Can that faith save him?

2:15
If a brother or sister is naked
and lacking daily food,

2:16
and one of you says to them:
Go in peace,
be warmed
and be filled,
but you do not give them the necessities of the body, what good is it?

2:17
So also faith, if it has no works,
is dead by itself.

2:18
But someone will say:
You have faith and I have works.
Show me
your faith apart from the works, and I will show you my faith by my works.

2:19
You believe that God is one?
You do well;
even the demons believe
and shudder.

2:20
But do you want to know, O empty person, that faith apart from works is useless?

2:21
Was not Abraham our father justified by works,
having offered up Isaac his son on the altar?

2:22
You see that faith was working together with his works
and by the works faith was brought to completion,

2:23
and the Scripture was fulfilled that says:
And Abraham believed God, and it was counted to him as righteousness,
and he was called a friend of God.

2:24
You see that a person is justified by works
and not by faith alone.

2:25
And likewise was not Rahab the prostitute also justified by works,
having received the messengers
and sent them out by another way?

2:26
For just as the body apart from the spirit is dead,
so also faith apart from works is dead.
