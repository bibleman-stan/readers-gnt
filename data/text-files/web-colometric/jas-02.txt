2:1
My brothers, do not hold your faith in our Lord Jesus Christ, the Lord of glory, with partiality;

2:2
for if a man comes into your assembly wearing a gold ring and fine clothing,
and a poor man also comes in wearing dirty clothing,

2:3
and you look with favor on the one wearing the fine clothing
and say:
You sit here in a good place,
and you say to the poor man:
You stand over there
or sit here below my footstool,

2:4
have you not made distinctions among yourselves
and become judges with evil motives?

2:5
Listen, my beloved brothers.
Did not God choose the poor of this world to be rich
in faith and heirs of the kingdom
which he promised to those who love him?

2:6
But you have dishonored the poor man.
Is it not the rich who oppress you,
and are they not the ones who drag you into court?

2:7
Do they not blaspheme the good name by which you have been called?

2:8
If however you fulfill the royal law according to the Scripture
You shall love your neighbor as yourself, you are doing well;

2:9
but if you show partiality,
you are committing sin, being convicted by the law as transgressors.

2:10
For whoever keeps the whole law
but stumbles in one point, has become guilty of all.

2:11
For the one who said: Do not commit adultery
also said: Do not murder;
now if you do not commit adultery but you do murder,
you have become a transgressor of the law.

2:12
So speak
and so act
as those who are about to be judged by the law of freedom.

2:13
For judgment is merciless to the one who has shown no mercy; mercy triumphs over judgment.

2:14
What good is it, my brothers, if someone says he has faith
but does not have works?
Can that faith save him?

2:15
If a brother or sister is poorly clothed
and lacking in daily food,

2:16
and one of you says to them:
Go in peace,
be warmed
and be filled,
but you do not give them what the body needs, what good is that?

2:17
So also faith, if it does not have works,
is dead by itself.

2:18
But someone will say:
You have faith and I have works.
Show me
your faith apart from the works, and I will show you my faith by my works.

2:19
You believe that God is one;
you do well;
even the demons believe
and shudder.

2:20
But do you want to know, O empty man, that faith without works is useless?

2:21
Was not Abraham our father justified by works,
when he offered up Isaac his son on the altar?

2:22
You see that faith was working together with his works
and by the works faith was perfected,

2:23
and the Scripture was fulfilled which says:
And Abraham believed God, and it was credited to him as righteousness,
and he was called a friend of God.

2:24
You see that a person is justified by works
and not by faith alone.

2:25
In the same way was not also Rahab the prostitute justified by works,
when she received the messengers
and sent them out by another way?

2:26
For just as the body without the spirit is dead,
so also faith without works is dead.
