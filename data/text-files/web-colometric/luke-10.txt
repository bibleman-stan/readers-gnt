10:1
And after these things the Lord appointed seventy-two others
and sent them two by two before his face into every city and place
where he himself was about to go.

10:2
And he was saying to them,
The harvest indeed is plentiful,
but the workers are few;
pray therefore to the Lord of the harvest
that he would send out workers into his harvest.

10:3
Go;
behold, I send you out as lambs in the midst of wolves.

10:4
Do not carry a moneybag,
nor a bag, nor sandals, and greet no one along the road.

10:5
And whatever house you enter,
first say,
Peace to this house.

10:6
And if a son of peace is there, your peace will rest upon him;
but if not,
it will return to you.

10:7
And remain in that same house,
eating and drinking what they provide, for the worker is worthy of his wages.
Do not move from house to house.

10:8
And whatever city you enter
and they receive you,
eat what is set before you,

10:9
and heal the sick in it,
and say to them,
The kingdom of God has come near to you.

10:10
But whatever city you enter
and they do not receive you, going out into its streets say,

10:11
Even the dust from your city that clings to us,
to our feet we wipe off against you;
yet know this, that the kingdom of God has come near.

10:12
I tell you, it will be more bearable for Sodom on that day than for that city.

10:13
Woe to you, Chorazin!
Woe to you, Bethsaida!
For if the mighty works done in you had been done in Tyre and Sidon, long ago
they would have repented, sitting in sackcloth and ashes.

10:14
But it will be more bearable for Tyre and Sidon in the judgment than for you.

10:15
And you, Capernaum, will you be exalted to heaven?
To Hades
you will be brought down.

10:16
The one who hears you hears me,
and the one who rejects you
rejects me;
and the one who rejects me rejects
the one who sent me.

10:17
And the seventy-two returned with joy, saying,
Lord, even the demons submit to us in your name.

10:18
And he said to them,
I was watching Satan fall like lightning from heaven.

10:19
Behold, I have given you authority to tread on snakes and scorpions,
and over all the power of the enemy,
and nothing will ever harm you.

10:20
Nevertheless, do not rejoice in this,
that the spirits submit to you,
but rejoice that your names are written in the heavens.

10:21
In that same hour he rejoiced in the Holy Spirit
and said,
I praise you, Father, Lord of heaven and earth,
that you have hidden these things from the wise and understanding
and revealed them to little children;
yes, Father, for such was your good pleasure before you.

10:22
All things have been handed over to me by my Father,
and no one knows who the Son is except the Father,
and who the Father is except the Son
and anyone to whom the Son wills to reveal him.

10:23
And turning to the disciples privately he said,
Blessed are the eyes that see what you see.

10:24
For I tell you that many prophets and kings wished to see
what you see
and did not see it,
and to hear what you hear and did not hear it.

10:25
And behold, a certain lawyer stood up,
testing him,
saying,
Teacher, what must I do
to inherit eternal life?

10:26
And he said to him,
What is written in the law? How do you read it?

10:27
And he answered and said,
You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart and with all your soul
and with all your strength and with all your mind,
and your neighbor as yourself.

10:28
And he said to him,
You have answered rightly;
do this and you will live.

10:29
But he, wishing to justify himself,
said to Jesus, And who is my neighbor?

10:30
And Jesus replied and said,
A certain man was going down from Jerusalem to Jericho
and fell among robbers,
who both stripped him
and inflicted wounds on him,
and departed,
leaving him half dead.

10:31
Now by coincidence a certain priest was going down on that road,
and seeing him he passed by on the other side.

10:32
And likewise a Levite, coming upon the place and seeing, passed by on the other side.

10:33
But a certain Samaritan, traveling, came upon him
and seeing him had compassion,

10:34
and coming near he bound up his wounds,
pouring on oil and wine,
and setting him on his own animal,
he brought him to an inn and took care of him.

10:35
And on the next day, taking out two denarii,
he gave them to the innkeeper
and said,
Take care of him,
and whatever more you spend,
I will repay you when I return.

10:36
Which of these three do you think proved to be a neighbor to the one who fell among the robbers?

10:37
And he said,
The one who showed mercy to him.
And Jesus said
to him,
Go
and you do likewise.

10:38
And as they were going along,
he entered a certain village;
and a certain woman named Martha welcomed him.

10:39
And she had a sister called Mary,
who, sitting at the feet of Jesus, was listening to his word.

10:40
But Martha was distracted with much serving; and coming up
she said,
Lord, do you not care
that my sister has left me to serve alone?
Tell her therefore to help me.

10:41
But the Lord answered and said to her, Martha, Martha, you are worried and troubled about many things,

10:42
but few things are needed, or only one; for Mary
has chosen the good portion,
which will not be taken from her.
