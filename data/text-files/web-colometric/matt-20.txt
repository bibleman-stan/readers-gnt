20:1
For the kingdom of heaven is like a man, a master of a house,
who went out early in the morning to hire workers for his vineyard.

20:2
And having agreed with the workers for a denarius
a day he sent them into his vineyard.

20:3
And going out about the third hour he saw others
standing idle in the marketplace;

20:4
and he said to them,
You go also into the vineyard,
and whatever is right I will give you;

20:5
and they went.
Going out again about the sixth and ninth hour he did likewise.

20:6
And about the eleventh hour going out he found others standing, and says to them,
Why have you been standing here idle all day?

20:7
They say to him,
Because no one hired us.
He says to them,
You go also into the vineyard.

20:8
And when evening came the owner of the vineyard says to his manager,
Call the workers
and pay them their wages beginning from the last up to the first.

20:9
And those hired about the eleventh hour came and received a denarius each.

20:10
And the first ones came and supposed that they would receive more;
and they also received a denarius each.

20:11
And receiving it they grumbled against the master of the house

20:12
saying,
These last ones worked a single hour,
and you made them equal to us who bore the burden of the day and the scorching heat.

20:13
And he answered one of them and said,
Friend, I am not wronging you;
did you not agree with me for a denarius?

20:14
Take what is yours
and go;
but I want to give to this last one
the same as to you;

20:15
is it not lawful for me
to do what I wish with what is mine?
Or is your eye envious because I am generous?

20:16
So the last will be first
and the first last.

20:17
And as Jesus was going up to Jerusalem he took the twelve disciples aside,
and on the way he said to them,

20:18
Look — we are going up to Jerusalem,
and the Son of Man will be handed over to the chief priests and scribes,
and they will condemn him to death,

20:19
and will hand him over to the nations to mock
and flog
and crucify,
and on the third day he will be raised.

20:20
Then the mother of the sons of Zebedee came to him with her sons
bowing down and asking something from him.

20:21
And he said to her,
What do you want?
She says to him,
Say that these two sons of mine may sit
one at your right and one at your left
in your kingdom.

20:22
And Jesus answered and said,
You do not know what you are asking;
are you able to drink the cup
that I am about to drink?
They say to him, We are able.

20:23
He says to them,
My cup indeed you will drink,
but to sit at my right and at my left is not
mine to give,
but it is for those for whom it has been prepared by my Father.

20:24
And the ten, hearing this, were indignant about the two brothers.

20:25
But Jesus, summoning them,
said,
You know that the rulers of the nations lord it over them
and the great ones exercise authority over them.

20:26
It shall not be so among you;
but whoever wants to become great among you shall be your servant,

20:27
and whoever wants to be first among you shall be your slave;

20:28
just as the Son of Man did not come to be served
but to serve
and to give his life as a ransom for many.

20:29
And as they were going out from Jericho
a great crowd followed him.

20:30
And look — two blind men sitting beside the road,
hearing that Jesus was passing by,
cried out saying,
Lord, have mercy on us, Son of David.

20:31
And the crowd rebuked them
to be silent;
but they cried out all the more saying,
Lord, have mercy on us, Son of David.

20:32
And Jesus stopped and called them
and said,
What do you want me to do for you?

20:33
They say to him,
Lord, that our eyes may be opened.

20:34
And Jesus, moved with compassion, touched their eyes,
and immediately they received sight
and followed him.
