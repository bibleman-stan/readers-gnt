2:1
We implore now you,
Brothers,
By the coming our of Lord Jesus Christ
Our and gathering together unto Him,

2:2
For not quickly to be shaken you in mind
Nor to be troubled neither by spirit nor by word nor by letter as if by us
As that is present the day of the Lord.

2:3
No one you should deceive in not one way;
Because it is if not until shall have come the apostasy first
And shall have been revealed the man of lawlessness the son of destruction,

2:4
The one opposing and exalting himself above every so-called god or object of worship,
So as for him in the temple of God to sit down
Setting forth he himself that is God.

2:5
Not do you remember that yet being with you these things I was saying to you?

2:6
And now that which is restraining you know for to be revealed him in his time;

2:7
For the mystery already is working of lawlessness;
Only there is the one restraining it at present until out of the midst he might be gone.

2:8
And then will be revealed the lawless one,
Whom the Lord Jesus will slay with the breath of the mouth his
And will annul by the appearing of the coming his,

2:9
Whose is coming according to the working of Satan
In every power and in signs and in wonders of falsehood

2:10
And in every deceit of wickedness unto those perishing in return for
Which the love of the truth not they received in order for to be saved them;

2:11
And because of this will send to them God a working of delusion
For to believe them what is false,

2:12
In order that should be judged all
Those not having believed the truth
But having delighted in unrighteousness.

2:13
But we ought to give thanks to God always concerning you,
Brothers beloved by the Lord,
That has chosen you God beginning unto salvation
In the sanctification of the Spirit and by faith of the truth,

2:14
To this He called you our through gospel,
To the obtaining of the glory our of Lord Jesus Christ.

2:15
So then,
Brothers,
Stand firm,
And hold fast to the traditions
That you were taught whether by word or by letter from us.

2:16
Himself our now Lord Jesus Christ and our God Father who loved us
And having given us comfort eternal and hope good by grace,

2:17
May He your encourage hearts
And may He strengthen them in every work and word good.
