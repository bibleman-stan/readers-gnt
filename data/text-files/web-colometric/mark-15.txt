15:1
And at once in the early morning, having held a council,
the chief priests with the elders and scribes and the whole council,
having bound Jesus, led him away
and handed him over to Pilate.

15:2
And Pilate questioned him:
"Are you the king of the Jews?"
And answering him he says: "You say so."

15:3
And the chief priests were accusing him of many things.

15:4
And Pilate was again questioning him, saying:
"Do you answer nothing? See how many charges they bring against you."

15:5
But Jesus no longer answered anything,
so that Pilate was amazed.

15:6
Now at the feast he used to release to them one prisoner whom they requested.

15:7
And there was one called Barabbas
bound with the rebels
who had committed murder in the uprising.

15:8
And the crowd came up and began to ask him to do as he usually did for them.

15:9
And Pilate answered them, saying:
"Do you want me to release to you the king of the Jews?"

15:10
For he knew that out of envy the chief priests had handed him over.

15:11
But the chief priests stirred up the crowd
so that he would release Barabbas to them instead.

15:12
And Pilate again answering was saying to them:
"What then do you want me to do
with the one you call the king of the Jews?"

15:13
And they shouted again:
"Crucify him!"

15:14
And Pilate was saying to them:
"Why? What evil has he done?"
But they shouted all the more:
"Crucify him!"

15:15
And Pilate, wanting to satisfy the crowd,
released Barabbas to them,
and handed over Jesus, having had him flogged, so that he might be crucified.

15:16
And the soldiers led him away inside the courtyard,
which is the governor's headquarters,
and they call together the whole cohort.

15:17
And they dress him in purple
and place on him
a crown of thorns they had woven.

15:18
And they began to salute him:
"Hail, king of the Jews!"

15:19
And they were striking his head with a reed
and spitting on him,
and bending their knees
they were bowing down before him.

15:20
And when they had mocked him,
they stripped off the purple from him
and put his own clothes on him.
And they lead him out so that they might crucify him.

15:21
And they force a passerby, Simon of Cyrene, coming from the countryside,
the father of Alexander and Rufus,
to carry his cross.

15:22
And they bring him to the place Golgotha,
which means, translated, Place of a Skull.

15:23
And they were giving him wine mixed with myrrh,
but he did not take it.

15:24
And they crucify him
and divide his clothes,
casting lots for them to decide who would take what.

15:25
Now it was the third hour
and they crucified him.

15:26
And the inscription of the charge against him read: The King of the Jews.

15:27
And with him they crucify two bandits,
one on his right and one on his left.

15:29
And those passing by were reviling him,
shaking their heads
and saying:
"Ha! You who would tear down the temple
and build it in three days --

15:30
save yourself by coming down from the cross!"

15:31
Likewise also the chief priests, mocking among themselves with the scribes, were saying:
"He saved others;
himself he cannot save.

15:32
Let the Christ, the king of Israel, come down now from the cross, so that we may see
and believe."
And those crucified with him were taunting him.

15:33
And when the sixth hour came
darkness fell over the whole land until the ninth hour.

15:34
And at the ninth hour Jesus cried out with a loud voice: "Eloi, Eloi, lema sabachthani?"
which means, translated: "My God, my God,
why have you forsaken me?"

15:35
And some of those standing by, hearing it, were saying:
"Look, he is calling Elijah."

15:36
And someone ran and filled a sponge with sour wine,
put it on a reed and was giving him a drink, saying:
"Wait -- let us see if Elijah comes to take him down."

15:37
But Jesus, letting out a loud cry, breathed his last.

15:38
And the curtain of the temple was torn in two from top to bottom.

15:39
And the centurion,
standing opposite him, seeing that he breathed his last in this way,
said:
"Truly this man was a son of God."

15:40
Now there were also women
watching from a distance,
among whom were Mary Magdalene
and Mary the mother of James the younger and of Joses, and Salome,

15:41
who when he was in Galilee
were following him
and serving him, and many other women who had come up with him to Jerusalem.

15:42
And when evening had already come, since it was Preparation Day,
that is, the day before the Sabbath,

15:43
Joseph of Arimathea came, a respected council member
who was himself also waiting for the kingdom of God.
He gathered his courage and went in to Pilate
and asked for the body of Jesus.

15:44
But Pilate was surprised that he was already dead,
and summoning the centurion
he asked him whether he had already died.

15:45
And learning this from the centurion, he granted the body to Joseph.

15:46
And having bought a linen cloth,
he took him down,
wrapped him in the linen cloth,
and laid him in a tomb
that had been cut from rock,
and he rolled a stone against the door of the tomb.

15:47
And Mary Magdalene and Mary the mother of Joses were watching where he was laid.
