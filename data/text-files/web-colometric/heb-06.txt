6:1
Therefore leaving behind the elementary word about Christ
let us press on toward maturity,
not again laying a foundation of repentance from dead works, and of faith toward God,

6:2
of instruction about washings and laying on of hands, and resurrection of the dead
and eternal judgment.

6:3
And this we will do
if indeed God permits.

6:4
For it is impossible for those who were once enlightened and have tasted the heavenly gift
and have become sharers of the Holy Spirit

6:5
and have tasted the good word of God and the powers of the coming age,

6:6
and then have fallen away, to renew them again to repentance,
since they crucify the Son of God to themselves and hold him up to public disgrace.

6:7
For land that has drunk
the rain that often falls upon it
and produces vegetation useful for those
on whose account it is also cultivated,
receives a blessing from God;

6:8
but if it bears thorns and thistles
it is worthless and near to a curse,
whose end is burning.

6:9
But we are convinced concerning you, beloved, of better things that accompany salvation,
even though we speak in this way;

6:10
for God is not unjust so as to forget your work and the love
which you demonstrated toward his name, having served the saints and still serving.

6:11
And we desire each one of you to show the same eagerness
toward the fullness of hope, until the end,

6:12
so that you may not become sluggish,
but imitators of those who through faith and patience inherit the promises.

6:13
For when God made his promise to Abraham, since he had no one
greater to swear by,
he swore by himself,

6:14
saying:
Surely blessing I will bless you
and multiplying I will multiply you;

6:15
and so having patiently waited he obtained the promise.

6:16
For human beings swear by the one greater than themselves,
and for them the oath is an end to every dispute as a confirmation;

6:17
in which God, wanting even more to demonstrate to the heirs of the promise the unchangeableness of his purpose
intervened with an oath,

6:18
so that through two unchangeable things
in which it is impossible for God to lie,
we who have fled for refuge might have strong encouragement to seize the hope set before us;

6:19
which we have as an anchor of the soul, both sure and firm
and entering into the inner place behind the curtain,

6:20
where Jesus has entered as a forerunner on our behalf,
having become a high priest forever according to the order of Melchizedek.
