23:1
And the whole multitude of them rose up and led him before Pilate.

23:2
And they began to accuse him, saying,
We found this man misleading our nation
and forbidding the payment of taxes to Caesar
and saying that he himself is Christ, a king.

23:3
And Pilate asked him, saying,
Are you the King of the Jews?
And he answered him and said, You say so.

23:4
And Pilate said to the chief priests and the crowds,
I find no guilt in this man.

23:5
But they kept insisting,
saying, He stirs up the people,
teaching throughout all Judea,
having started from Galilee even to here.

23:6
And when Pilate heard this, he asked whether the man was a Galilean,

23:7
and learning that he was under Herod's jurisdiction,
he sent him to Herod,
who was himself also in Jerusalem during those days.

23:8
And when Herod saw Jesus, he was very glad,
for he had been wanting to see him for a long time,
because he had heard about him,
and he was hoping to see some sign performed by him.

23:9
And he questioned him at considerable length;
but he answered him nothing.

23:10
And the chief priests and the scribes stood there,
vehemently accusing him.

23:11
And Herod with his soldiers treated him with contempt
and mocked him, and dressing him in splendid clothing,
sent him back to Pilate.

23:12
And Herod and Pilate became friends
with each other that very day;
for previously they had been at enmity with each other.

23:13
And Pilate called together the chief priests and the rulers and the people

23:14
and said to them,
You brought this man to me as one who was misleading the people,
and behold, having examined him before you, I found no guilt in this man
regarding the charges you bring against him.

23:15
And neither did Herod, for he sent him back to us;
and behold, nothing deserving of death has been done by him;

23:16
therefore, after punishing him, I will release him.

23:18
But they all cried out together, saying,
Away with this man,
and release to us Barabbas--

23:19
a man who had been thrown into prison for a certain insurrection in the city, and for murder.

23:20
And Pilate addressed them again,
wanting to release Jesus.

23:21
But they kept shouting, saying,
Crucify, crucify him!

23:22
And he said to them a third time,
Why, what evil has this man done?
I have found no guilt deserving death in him;
therefore, after punishing him, I will release him.

23:23
But they were insistent with loud voices,
demanding that he be crucified,
and their voices prevailed.

23:24
And Pilate decided that their demand should be granted;

23:25
and he released the man who had been thrown into prison for insurrection and murder,
the one they were asking for,
but Jesus he delivered over to their will.

23:26
And as they led him away,
they seized a certain Simon of Cyrene,
coming from the country, and laid the cross on him to carry behind Jesus.

23:27
And a great multitude of the people followed him, and of women
who were mourning and lamenting him.

23:28
But turning to them, Jesus said,
Daughters of Jerusalem,
do not weep for me;
but weep for yourselves
and for your children,

23:29
because behold, days are coming
in which they will say, Blessed are the barren and the wombs
that never bore
and the breasts that never nursed.

23:30
Then they will begin to say to the mountains,
Fall on us,
and to the hills, Cover us;

23:31
for if they do these things when the wood is green,
what will happen when it is dry?

23:32
And two others, who were criminals, were also led away to be put to death with him.

23:33
And when they came to the place called The Skull,
there they crucified him and the criminals,
one on his right and one on his left.

23:34
And Jesus was saying,
Father,
forgive them,
for they do not know what they are doing.
And dividing up his garments,
they cast lots.

23:35
And the people stood watching.
And the rulers also were sneering, saying,
He saved others;
let him save himself,
if this is the Christ of God, the Chosen One.

23:36
And the soldiers also mocked him,
coming up and offering him sour wine

23:37
and saying,
If you are the King of the Jews, save yourself.

23:38
And there was also an inscription over him:
This is the King of the Jews.

23:39
And one of the criminals who were hanged was blaspheming him, saying,
Are you not the Christ?
Save yourself and us.

23:40
But the other answered and rebuked him,
saying,
Do you not even fear God, since you are under the same sentence?

23:41
And we indeed justly, for we are receiving what we deserve for what we did;
but this man has done nothing wrong.

23:42
And he was saying,
Jesus,
remember me
when you come into your kingdom.

23:43
And he said to him,
Truly I say to you,
today you will be with me in Paradise.

23:44
And it was already about the sixth hour,
and darkness came over the whole land until the ninth hour,

23:45
the sun's light failing,
and the curtain of the temple was torn in the middle.

23:46
And crying out with a loud voice, Jesus said,
Father,
into your hands I commit my spirit.
And having said this, he breathed his last.

23:47
And when the centurion saw what had happened, he glorified God, saying,
Certainly this man was righteous.

23:48
And all the crowds that had assembled for this spectacle,
when they saw the things that had taken place,
were returning, beating their chests.

23:49
And all his acquaintances were standing at a distance,
and the women who had followed him from Galilee,
seeing these things.

23:50
And behold, a man named Joseph, a member of the council, a good and righteous man--

23:51
he had not consented to their decision and action--
from Arimathea, a city of the Jews,
who was waiting for the kingdom of God,

23:52
this man went to Pilate and asked for the body of Jesus,

23:53
and he took it down and wrapped it in a linen cloth,
and laid him in a tomb cut in stone
where no one had ever yet been laid.

23:54
And it was the day of Preparation,
and the Sabbath was about to begin.

23:55
And the women followed,
who had come with him from Galilee,
and they saw the tomb and how his body was laid,

23:56
and they returned and prepared spices and ointments.
And on the Sabbath they rested according to the commandment,
