3:1
And he entered again into a synagogue,
and a man was there
having a withered hand.

3:2
And they were watching him closely
to see whether he would heal him on the Sabbath,
so that they might accuse him.

3:3
And he says to the man who had the withered hand:
Rise into the middle.

3:4
And he says to them:
Is it lawful on the Sabbath to do good
or to do harm,
to save a life
or to kill?
But they were silent.

3:5
And having looked around at them with anger,
grieved at the hardness of their hearts, he says to the man:
Stretch out your hand;
and he stretched it out, and his hand was restored.

3:6
And having gone out, the Pharisees immediately
with the Herodians were making a plan against him,
how they might destroy him.

3:7
And Jesus with his disciples withdrew to the sea;
and a great crowd from Galilee followed,
and from Judea

3:8
hearing all that he was doing,
and from Jerusalem and from Idumea
and beyond the Jordan and around Tyre and Sidon, a great crowd,
came to him.

3:9
And he told his disciples that a small boat should be kept ready for him because of the crowd
so that they would not crush him;

3:10
for he had healed many,
so that they were pressing upon him in order to touch him,
as many as had diseases.

3:11
And the unclean spirits,
whenever they saw him,
were falling before him and crying out,
saying: You are the Son of God.

3:12
And he was sternly warning them many times,
that they should not make him known.

3:13
And he goes up onto the mountain
and summons
those whom he himself wanted,
and they came to him.

3:14
And he appointed twelve,
so that they might be with him
and so that he might send them out to proclaim

3:15
and to have authority to cast out demons;

3:16
and he appointed the twelve,
and he gave the name Peter to Simon,

3:17
and James the son of Zebedee
and John the brother of James (and he gave them the name Boanerges,
which means Sons of Thunder),

3:18
and Andrew and Philip and Bartholomew and Matthew and Thomas
and James the son of Alphaeus and Thaddaeus and Simon the Zealot

3:19
and Judas Iscariot,
who also betrayed him.

3:20
And he comes into a house;
and the crowd gathers again,
so that they were not even able to eat bread.

3:21
And when his family heard this they went out to seize him,
for they were saying: He is out of his mind.

3:22
And the scribes who had come down from Jerusalem were saying: He has Beelzebul,
and: By the ruler of the demons he casts out demons.

3:23
And having called them to himself
he was speaking to them in parables:
How is Satan able to cast out Satan?

3:24
And if a kingdom is divided against itself,
it is not able to stand,
that kingdom;

3:25
and if a household is divided against itself,
that household will not be able to stand;

3:26
and if Satan has risen up against himself
and is divided,
he is not able to stand
but has come to an end.

3:27
But no one is able, having entered the house of the strong man, to plunder his possessions
unless he first binds the strong man,
and then he will plunder his house.

3:28
Truly I say to you that all things will be forgiven the sons of men, their sins and their blasphemies,
however much they blaspheme;

3:29
but whoever blasphemes against the Holy Spirit
does not have forgiveness to the age,
but is guilty of an eternal sin.

3:30
Because they were saying:
He has an unclean spirit.

3:31
And his mother and his brothers come
and standing outside they sent word to him,
calling for him.

3:32
And a crowd was sitting around him,
and they say to him:
Look, your mother and your brothers are outside seeking you.

3:33
And answering them he says:
Who is my mother, or my brothers?

3:34
And having looked around
at those sitting in a circle around him
he says:
Look, my mother and my brothers;

3:35
for whoever does the will of God,
this one is my brother and sister and mother.
