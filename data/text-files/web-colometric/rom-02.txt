2:1
Therefore you are without
O man, every one who judges!
excuse ; for in that you judge the other,
you condemn yourself,
for you practice the same things
you who judge;

2:2
and we know that the judgment of God is
according to truth against those who practice such things.

2:3
Do you reckon this
O man who judges
those who practice such things
and does
them
yourself, that you will escape the judgment of God?

2:4
Or do you despise the riches of his kindness
and forbearance and patience,
not knowing that the kindness of God leads you to repentance?

2:5
But by your hardness and unrepentant heart you are storing up wrath for yourself
on the day of wrath and revelation of the righteous judgment of God,

2:6
who will render to each according to his works:

2:7
to those who by perseverance in good work
seek glory and honor and incorruptibility, eternal life;

2:8
but to those who act from selfish ambition and disobey the truth
but obey unrighteousness,
wrath and fury,

2:9
affliction and distress upon every soul of the person who works evil,
of the Jew first and also of the Greek;

2:10
but glory and honor and peace to everyone who works good,
to the Jew first and also to the Greek;

2:11
for there is no partiality with God.

2:12
For as many as sinned without the law
will also perish without the law;
and as many as sinned under the law
will be judged by the law;

2:13
for it is not the hearers of the law who are righteous before God,
but the doers of the law will be justified.

2:14
For when nations who do not have the law
do by nature the things of the law,
these, not having the law, are a law to themselves;

2:15
who demonstrate the work of the law written in their hearts,
their conscience bearing witness together
and their thoughts among themselves accusing
or even defending,

2:16
on the day when God judges the hidden things of people
according to my gospel through Christ Jesus.

2:17
But if you call yourself a Jew
and rely upon the law
and boast in God

2:18
and know his will
and approve what is excellent, being instructed from the law,

2:19
and you are confident that you yourself are a guide to the blind, a light to those in darkness,

2:20
an instructor of the foolish, a teacher of the immature,
having the embodiment of knowledge and of truth in the law --

2:21
you then who teach another,
do you not teach yourself?
You who preach not to steal, do you steal?

2:22
You who say not to commit adultery,
do you commit adultery?
You who abhor idols, do you rob temples?

2:23
You who boast in the law,
do you dishonor God through the transgression of the law?

2:24
For the name of God is blasphemed among the nations because of you, as it is written.

2:25
For circumcision indeed profits if you practice the law;
but if you are a transgressor of the law,
your circumcision has become uncircumcision.

2:26
If then the uncircumcised keeps the requirements of the law,
will not his uncircumcision be counted as circumcision?

2:27
And the one who is physically uncircumcised but fulfills the law will judge you who through the letter
and circumcision are a transgressor of the law.

2:28
For the one who is a Jew outwardly is not truly a Jew,
nor is outward circumcision in the flesh true circumcision;

2:29
but the one who is a Jew inwardly,
and circumcision of the heart by the spirit not the letter,
whose praise is not from people but from God.
