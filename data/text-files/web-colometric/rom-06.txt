6:1
What then will we say
Shall we continue in sin
that grace may abound

6:2
Never may it be
Who we died to sin
how still shall we live in it

6:3
Or are you unaware that as many as have been baptized into Christ Jesus
into the death of him have been baptized

6:4
We were buried therefore with him through baptism into death
so that just as was raised up Christ out from the dead by the glory of the Father
so also we in newness of life should walk

6:5
If for united we have become in the likeness of the death of him
certainly also of the resurrection we will be

6:6
this knowing that old of us self was crucified with him
so that might be annulled the body of sin that no longer are enslaved we to sin

6:7
The one for having died has been freed from sin

6:8
If now we died with Christ
we believe that also we will live with him

6:9
knowing that Christ
having been raised up out from the dead no more dies
Death Him no longer rules over

6:10
That which for He died
to sin He died once for all
that which however He lives He lives to God

6:11
So also you consider yourselves to be dead indeed to sin
living however to God in Christ Jesus

6:12
Not therefore let reign sin in the mortal of you body
in order to obey the desires of it

6:13
Neither yield the members of you as instruments of unrighteousness to sin
but yield yourselves to God as
out from the dead living and the members of you as instruments of righteousness to God

6:14
Sin for you not will rule over
not for you are under law
but under grace

6:15
What then Shall we sin
because not we are under law
but under grace
Never may it be

6:16
Not know you that to whom you yield yourselves as slaves for obedience slaves you are
to him whom you obey whether of sin to death
or of obedience to righteousness

6:17
Thanks be however to God that you used to be slaves of sin
you have become obedient now from the heart
to which you were handed over to the form of teaching

6:18
Having been set free now from sin you have become slaves to righteousness

6:19
In human terms I speak on account of the weakness of the flesh of you
For as for you yielded the members of you in bondage
to impurity and to lawlessness unto lawlessness
so now yield the members of you
in bondage to righteousness unto sanctification

6:20
When for slaves you were of sin
free you were from righteousness

6:21
What therefore fruit had you then
in the things of which now you are ashamed
The for end of those things is death

6:22
Now however having been set free from sin
having become slaves now to God you have the fruit of you unto sanctification
now the end is eternal life

6:23
The for wages of sin is death
but the gift of God eternal life in Christ Jesus the Lord of us
