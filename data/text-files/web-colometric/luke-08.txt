8:1
And it happened in the time that
followed that he was traveling through every city and village,
proclaiming and announcing the good news of the kingdom of
God, and the twelve were with him,

8:2
and certain women who had been healed of evil spirits and infirmities: Mary
who was called Magdalene, from whom seven demons had gone out,

8:3
and Joanna the wife of Chuza, Herod's
steward, and Susanna and many others,
who were serving them from their own resources.

8:4
And when a large crowd was
gathering and those from every city were coming to him,
he spoke through a parable:

8:5
The sower went out to sow his seed. And
as he was sowing, some
fell along the path, and it
was trampled,
and the birds of the sky devoured it.

8:6
And other seed fell on the rock,
and having sprung up it withered because it had no moisture.

8:7
And other seed fell in the midst of the thorns,
and the thorns grew up with it and choked it.

8:8
And other seed fell into the good soil, and having sprung up it
produced fruit a hundredfold. Saying these things he
called out, The one who
has ears to hear, let him hear.

8:9
And his disciples were asking him what this parable might mean.

8:10
And he said,
To you it has been given to know the mysteries of the kingdom of God,
but to the rest it is in parables,
so that seeing they may not see
and hearing they may not understand.

8:11
Now this is the parable:
The seed is the word of God.

8:12
And those along the path are those who have heard,
then the devil comes
and takes away the word from their hearts,
so that they may not believe and be saved.

8:13
And those on the rock are
the ones who, when they hear, receive the word with
joy, and these have no root;
they believe for a time
and in a time of testing fall away.

8:14
And what fell among the thorns, these are those who have heard, and by the
cares and riches and pleasures of life as they go along they are choked
and do not bring fruit to maturity.

8:15
And what is in the good soil, these are the ones
who, having heard the word with a noble and good heart, hold it fast
and bear fruit with endurance.

8:16
And no one after lighting a lamp covers it with a
vessel or puts it under a
bed, but puts it on a
lampstand, so that those who enter may see the light.

8:17
For nothing is hidden that will not become manifest,
nor anything secret that will not be known and come to light.

8:18
Take care therefore how you hear;
for to the one who has, more will be given,
and from the one who does not have, even what he thinks he has will be taken from him.

8:19
And his mother and his brothers came to him,
and they were unable to reach him because of the crowd.

8:20
And it was reported to him,
Your mother and your brothers are standing outside, wanting to see you.

8:21
But he answered and said to them,
My mother and my brothers are these:
those who hear the word of God and do it.

8:22
And it happened on one of the days
that he got into a boat,
he and his disciples, and he said to them,
Let us cross over to the other side of the lake,
and they set out.

8:23
And as they were sailing he fell
asleep. And a windstorm came down on the lake, and they were
being swamped and were in danger.

8:24
And they came and woke him, saying
Master, Master,
we
are perishing! And he arose and rebuked the wind and the surging of the water, and
they ceased, and
there was a calm.

8:25
And he said to
them, Where is your faith?
And being afraid they marveled, saying to one
another, Who then is this,
that he commands even the winds and the water and they obey him?

8:26
And they sailed to the region of the Gerasenes,
which is opposite Galilee.

8:27
And when he stepped out onto the land,
a certain man from the city met him who had demons;
and for a long time he had not
worn clothing, and he was not living in a house but among the tombs.

8:28
And seeing Jesus
he cried out and fell before him
and said with a loud voice,
What have you to do with me, Jesus, Son of the Most High God?
I beg you, do not torment me.

8:29
For he was commanding the unclean spirit to come out of the man. For
many times it had seized him, and
he was being bound with chains and shackles and
kept under guard, yet breaking the bonds he was driven by the demon into the wilderness.

8:30
And Jesus asked him,
What is your name?
And he said,
Legion, because many demons had entered him.

8:31
And they were begging
him not to command them to go away into the abyss.

8:32
Now there was a herd of many pigs feeding there on the mountain;
and they begged him to allow them to enter those;
and he allowed them.

8:33
And the demons came out of the man and entered into the pigs,
and the herd rushed down the steep bank into the lake and was drowned.

8:34
And when those who were feeding them saw what had happened, they fled
and reported it in the city and in the countryside.

8:35
And they went out to see what had happened
and they came to Jesus,
and they found the man sitting,
from whom the demons had gone out, clothed and in his right mind,
at the feet of Jesus, and they were afraid.

8:36
And those who had seen it reported to them how the demonized man had been saved.

8:37
And the whole multitude of the surrounding region of the Gerasenes asked him to depart from them,
for they were gripped with great fear;
and he got into a boat and returned.

8:38
And the man from whom the demons had gone out was begging to be with him; but
he sent him away, saying,

8:39
Return to your house, and tell
all that God has done for you. And
he went away, proclaiming through the whole city all that Jesus had done for him.

8:40
And when Jesus returned,
the crowd welcomed him,
for they were all expecting him.

8:41
And behold, a man came whose name was Jairus,
and he was a ruler of the
synagogue, and falling at the feet of Jesus he
was begging him to come into his house,

8:42
because he had an only daughter, about twelve years old, and she was dying.
And as he was going, the crowds were pressing in on him.

8:43
And a woman who had had a flow of blood for twelve years,
who had spent all her livelihood on physicians,
was not able to be healed by anyone.

8:44
Coming up behind him she touched the fringe of his garment,
and immediately her flow of blood stopped.

8:45
And Jesus said, Who is
the one who touched me?
And when all were denying it, Peter said
Master,
the crowds are surrounding you and pressing in.

8:46
But Jesus said,
Someone touched me,
for I knew that power had gone out from me.

8:47
And the woman, seeing that she had not escaped notice, came trembling
and falling before him,
she declared for what reason she had touched him
before all the people, and how she had been healed immediately.

8:48
And he said to her,
Daughter, your faith has saved you;
go in peace.

8:49
While he was still
speaking, someone comes from the synagogue ruler's house, saying,
Your daughter has died; do not trouble the teacher any longer.

8:50
But Jesus, hearing this, answered him,
Do not be afraid; only believe, and she will be saved.

8:51
And when he came to the house he did not allow anyone to
enter with him except Peter and John and
James and the father of the child and the mother.

8:52
And all were weeping and mourning for her.
But he said,
Do not weep, for she has not died but is sleeping.

8:53
And they were laughing at him, knowing that she had died.

8:54
But he, taking hold of her hand,
called out saying,
Child, arise.

8:55
And her spirit returned,
and she rose immediately,
and he commanded that she be given something to eat.

8:56
And her parents were astounded;
but he charged them to tell no one what had happened.
