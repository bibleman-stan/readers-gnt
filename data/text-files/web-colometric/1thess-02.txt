2:1
You yourselves for know,
Brothers,
Our coming to you that not in vain has been,

2:2
But having previously suffered and having been mistreated just as you know
In Philippi we had boldness our in God
To speak to you the gospel of God amid much conflict.

2:3
Our For exhortation is not of error nor of impurity nor in trickery,

2:4
But just as we have been approved by God to be entrusted with the gospel so we speak,
Not as men pleasing but God
The one our examining hearts.

2:5
For never at any time with word of flattery were we just as you know,
Nor with a pretext for greed God is witness,

2:6
Nor seeking from men glory,
Nor from you
Nor from others,

2:7
Though having authority with weight to be
As Christ’s apostles;
But we were gentle in the midst of you,
As if a nursing mother would cherish her own children;

2:8
So yearning over you
We were pleased to have imparted to you not only the gospel of God but our also own lives,
Because beloved to us you have become.

2:9
You remember for,
Brothers,
Our labor and hardship Night
And day working in order not to burden anyone of you
We proclaimed to you the gospel of God.

2:10
You are witnesses and God,
How holily and righteously and blamelessly toward you those believing we were,

2:11
Just as you know how one each of you as a father children his own

2:12
Exhorting you and comforting and charging,
Unto to walk you worthily of God who calls you
Into the of Himself kingdom and glory.

2:13
And because of this also we give thanks to God unceasingly,
That having received the word your by hearing from us of God you accepted not the word of men
But even as truly it is
The word of God which also works in you who believe.

2:14
For you imitators became,
Brothers,
Of the churches of God being
In Judea in Christ Jesus,
For the same as them suffered also you from the own countrymen,
As also did they from the Jews

2:15
Who both the Lord having killed Jesus and their own prophets
And us having driven out,
And God not pleasing,
And all men who are set against,

2:16
Hindering us to the Gentiles to speak,
That they might be saved so as to fill up their sins always.
Has come now upon them the wrath to the utmost.

2:17
We however,
Brothers,
Having been bereaved of you for the time of an hour,
In face not in heart more abundantly were eager the face of you to see
With great desire.

2:18
Therefore we wanted to come to you,
I indeed Paul both once and twice and hindered us Satan.

2:19
Who is our for hope or joy or crown
Or are not even our before Lord Jesus at His coming?

2:20
For you our are glory and joy.
