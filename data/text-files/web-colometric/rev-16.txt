16:1
And I heard a loud voice from the temple saying to the seven angels:
Go
and pour out the seven bowls of the wrath of God on the earth.

16:2
And the first went
and poured out his bowl on the earth;
and a foul and painful sore came upon the people
who bore the mark of the beast
and who worshiped its image.

16:3
And the second poured out his bowl into the sea;
and it became blood like that of a dead man,
and every living thing in the sea died.

16:4
And the third poured out his bowl into the rivers
and the springs of water;
and they became blood.

16:5
And I heard the angel of the waters saying:
Righteous are you, the one who is
and who was, the Holy One, because you have judged these things,

16:6
because they shed the blood of saints and prophets,
and you have given them blood to drink;
they deserve it.

16:7
And I heard the altar saying:
Yes, Lord God Almighty, true and righteous are your judgments.

16:8
And the fourth poured out his bowl on the sun;
and it was allowed to scorch people with fire.

16:9
And people were scorched by the fierce heat;
and they blasphemed the name of God who has authority
over these plagues,
and they did not repent and give him glory.

16:10
And the fifth poured out his bowl on the throne of the beast;
and its kingdom
was plunged into
darkness, and people gnawed their tongues
in anguish,

16:11
and they blasphemed the God of heaven because of their pains and their sores,
and they did not repent of their works.

16:12
And the sixth poured out his bowl on the great river Euphrates;
and its water
was dried up,
to prepare the way for the kings from the rising of the sun.

16:13
And I saw from the mouth of the dragon and from the mouth of the beast
and from the mouth of the false prophet three unclean spirits like frogs;

16:14
for they are demonic spirits
performing signs,
who go out to the kings of the whole inhabited world,
to assemble them for the battle of the great day of God the Almighty—

16:15
Look, I am coming like a thief.
Blessed is the one who stays awake
and keeps his garments,
so that he does not walk about naked
and they see his shame—

16:16
and they assembled them at the place called in Hebrew Armageddon.

16:17
And the seventh poured out his bowl into the air—
and a loud voice came from the temple, from the throne, saying: It is done!—

16:18
and there came flashes of lightning and rumblings and peals of thunder,
and a great earthquake struck,
such as had never occurred
since people came to be on the earth,
so violent was that great earthquake,

16:19
and the great city was split into three parts,
and the cities of the nations fell;
and Babylon the great was remembered before God
to give her the cup of the wine of his furious wrath;

16:20
and every island fled away,
and mountains could not be found.

16:21
And great hailstones, about a hundred pounds each, fell from heaven on people;
and people blasphemed God because of the plague of hail,
because that plague was extremely severe.
