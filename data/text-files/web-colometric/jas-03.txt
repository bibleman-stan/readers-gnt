3:1
Not many of you should become teachers, my brothers,
knowing that we will receive a stricter judgment;

3:2
for we all stumble in many ways.
If anyone does not stumble in speech,
this is a mature man, able to bridle even the whole body.

3:3
Now if we put bits
into the mouths of horses to make them obey us,
we also guide their whole body.

3:4
Look also at ships, so great they are
and driven by harsh winds, they are steered by a very small rudder
wherever the impulse of the helmsman wills;

3:5
so also the tongue is a small member
and boasts of great things.
See how great a forest so small a fire sets ablaze;

3:6
and the tongue is a fire,
the world of unrighteousness; the tongue is set among our members,
the one that stains the whole body
and sets on fire the course of existence
and is itself set on fire by hell.

3:7
For every species of beast and bird and reptile and sea creature
is tamed and has been tamed by humankind;

3:8
but the tongue no human being can tame;
a restless evil, full of deadly poison.

3:9
With it we bless the Lord and Father,
and with it we curse human beings who are made in the likeness of God;

3:10
out of the same mouth come blessing and cursing.
It should not be so, my brothers.

3:11
Does a spring pour out from the same opening what is sweet and what is bitter?

3:12
Can a fig tree, my brothers, produce olives, or a vine figs?
Neither can salt water produce
fresh water.

3:13
Who is wise and understanding among you?
Let him show by his good conduct his works in the gentleness of wisdom.

3:14
But if you have bitter jealousy and selfish ambition in your hearts,
do not boast
and lie against the truth.

3:15
This is not the wisdom
that comes down from above, but earthly, unspiritual, demonic;

3:16
for where there is jealousy and selfish ambition,
there is disorder and every worthless practice.

3:17
But the wisdom from above is first pure,
then peaceable, gentle, open to reason, full of mercy
and good fruits, impartial, sincere;

3:18
and the fruit of righteousness is sown in peace by those who make peace.
