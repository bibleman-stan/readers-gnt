1:1
The beginning of the good news of Jesus Christ.

1:2
Just as it has been written in Isaiah the prophet:
Look, I am sending my messenger before your face,
who will prepare your way;

1:3
a voice crying out in the wilderness:
Prepare the way of the Lord,
make his paths straight,

1:4
John appeared, the one baptizing in the wilderness
and proclaiming a baptism of repentance for the forgiveness of sins.

1:5
And all the Judean countryside and all the people of Jerusalem were going out to him,
and they were being baptized by him in the Jordan River
while confessing their sins.

1:6
And John was
dressed in camel's hair
with a leather belt around his waist, and he ate locusts and wild honey.

1:7
And he was proclaiming, saying: The one stronger than me is coming after me,
of whom I am not worthy to stoop down and untie the strap of his sandals;

1:8
I baptized you with water,
but he will baptize you in the Holy Spirit.

1:9
And it happened in those days
that Jesus came from Nazareth of Galilee
and was baptized in the Jordan by John.

1:10
And immediately, coming up out of the water, he saw
the heavens being torn open
and the Spirit like a dove descending upon him;

1:11
and a voice came from the heavens:
You are my beloved Son,
in you I am well pleased.

1:12
And immediately the Spirit drives him out into the wilderness.

1:13
And he was in the wilderness forty days
being tested by Satan, and he was with the wild animals,
and the angels were serving him.

1:14
And after John was handed over
Jesus came into Galilee
proclaiming the good news of God

1:15
and saying: The time has been fulfilled and the kingdom of God has drawn near;
repent
and believe in the good news.

1:16
And passing along by the Sea of Galilee he saw Simon and Andrew the brother of Simon casting nets into the sea,
for they were fishermen;

1:17
and Jesus said to them:
Come after me, and I will make you become fishers of people.

1:18
And immediately, leaving their nets,
they followed him.

1:19
And going on a little farther he saw James the son of Zebedee and John his brother
and them in the boat mending their nets,

1:20
and immediately he called them.
And leaving their father Zebedee in the boat with the hired workers
they went away after him.

1:21
And they enter into Capernaum.
And immediately on the Sabbath he was teaching
in the synagogue.

1:22
And they were astounded at his teaching,
for he was teaching them as one having authority
and not as the scribes.

1:23
And immediately there was in their synagogue a man with an unclean spirit and he cried out

1:24
saying:
What do you have to do with us, Jesus of Nazareth?
Have you come to destroy us?
I know who you are, the Holy One of God.

1:25
And Jesus rebuked him saying:
Be silent
and come out of him.

1:26
And the unclean spirit, having convulsed him
and having cried out with a loud voice, came out of him.

1:27
And they were all amazed,
so that they were debating among themselves saying:
What is this?
A new teaching!
With authority he commands even the unclean spirits,
and they obey him.

1:28
And the report about him went out immediately everywhere into all the surrounding region of Galilee.

1:29
And immediately, having come out of the synagogue, they went into the house of Simon and Andrew
with James and John.

1:30
Now Simon's mother-in-law was lying down with a fever, and immediately they tell him about her.

1:31
And having come to her he raised her up,
taking hold of her hand;
and the fever left her, and she began serving them.

1:32
Now when evening came, after the sun had set,
they were bringing to him all those who were ill
and those who were possessed by demons;

1:33
and the whole city was gathered at the door.

1:34
And he healed many who were ill with various diseases,
and he cast out many demons,
and he would not allow the demons to speak,
because they knew him.

1:35
And rising very early while it was still dark he went out
and departed to a deserted place
and there he was praying.

1:36
And Simon and those with him went searching for him,

1:37
and they found him
and they say to him: Everyone is looking for you.

1:38
And he says to them:
Let us go elsewhere to the neighboring
towns, so that I may proclaim there also,
for this is why I came out.

1:39
And he went proclaiming in their synagogues throughout all of Galilee
and casting out demons.

1:40
And a leper comes to him, begging him and kneeling down,
saying to him: If you are willing
you are able to cleanse me.

1:41
And moved with anger, stretching out his hand, he touched him
and says to him:
I am willing; be cleansed!

1:42
And immediately the leprosy left him,
and he was cleansed.

1:43
And having sternly warned him
he immediately sent him away,

1:44
and says to him:
See that you say nothing to anyone,
but go,
show yourself to the priest
and offer for your cleansing
what Moses commanded
as a witness to them.

1:45
But having gone out he began to proclaim freely and to spread the word,
so that he was no longer able to enter openly into a city,
but he stayed outside in deserted places;
and they were coming to him from everywhere.
