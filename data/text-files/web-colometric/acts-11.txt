11:1
Now the apostles and the brothers who were throughout Judea heard
that the Gentiles also had received the word of God.

11:2
And when Peter went up to Jerusalem,
those of the circumcision took issue with him

11:3
saying: You went to men
who are uncircumcised
and ate with them.

11:4
But Peter began and explained to them in order saying:

11:5
I was in the city of Joppa praying
and I saw in a trance a vision, something descending
like a great sheet let down by four corners from heaven,
and it came to me.

11:6
Looking into it I observed closely
and saw the four-footed animals of the earth and the wild beasts and the reptiles
and the birds of heaven.

11:7
And I also heard a voice saying to me:
Rise, Peter, kill and eat.

11:8
But I said:
By no means, Lord, for nothing common or unclean has ever entered my mouth.

11:9
But a voice answered a second time from heaven:
What God has made clean you must not call common.

11:10
And this happened three times,
and everything was pulled back up into heaven.

11:11
And behold immediately three men stood at the house
in which we were, sent to me from Caesarea.

11:12
And the Spirit told me to go with them
without hesitation.
And these six brothers also went
with me,
and we entered the man's house.

11:13
And he reported to us how he saw the angel standing in his house
and saying:
Send to Joppa
and summon Simon who is called Peter,

11:14
who will speak words to you
by which you will be saved, you and all your household.

11:15
And as I began to speak
the Holy Spirit fell on them just as on us at the beginning.

11:16
And I remembered the word of the Lord how he said:
John indeed baptized with water,
but you will be baptized in the Holy Spirit.

11:17
If then God gave them the same gift as he gave to us
when we believed in the Lord Jesus Christ,
who was I
that I could stand in God's way?

11:18
And hearing these things they fell silent
and glorified God saying:
Then to the Gentiles also God has granted repentance that leads to life.

11:19
Now those who were scattered because of the tribulation
that arose over Stephen traveled as far as Phoenicia and Cyprus and Antioch
speaking the word to no one except Jews only.

11:20
But there were some of them men from Cyprus and Cyrene,
who coming to Antioch spoke also to the Hellenists,
proclaiming the good news of the Lord Jesus.

11:21
And the hand of the Lord was with them,
and a great number who believed turned to the Lord.

11:22
And the report about them came to the ears of the church in Jerusalem,
and they sent Barnabas as far as Antioch,

11:23
who arriving and seeing the grace of God rejoiced
and encouraged them all to remain faithful to the Lord with devoted hearts,

11:24
for he was a good man and full of the Holy Spirit and of faith.
And a considerable crowd was added to the Lord.

11:25
And he went to Tarsus to look for Saul,

11:26
and finding him he brought him to Antioch.
And it happened that for a whole year
they met together in the church
and taught a considerable crowd, and in Antioch
the disciples were first called Christians.

11:27
Now in those days prophets came down from Jerusalem to Antioch,

11:28
and one of them named Agabus stood up
and indicated through the Spirit that a great famine was about to come upon the whole world,
which took place under Claudius.

11:29
And each of the disciples according to his ability
determined to send relief to the brothers
living in Judea,

11:30
which they also did sending it to the elders
by the hand of Barnabas and Saul.
