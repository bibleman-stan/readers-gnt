3:1
What then is the advantage of the Jew,
or what is the benefit of circumcision?

3:2
Much in every way.
For first of all, they were entrusted with the oracles of God.

3:3
What then if some were unfaithful?
Will their unfaithfulness nullify the faithfulness of God?

3:4
May it never be!
Rather, let God be true,
and every person a liar,
as it is written:
That you may be justified in your words
and prevail when you are judged.

3:5
But if our unrighteousness demonstrates the righteousness of God,
what shall we say?
Is God unrighteous who inflicts wrath?
I speak in human terms.

3:6
May it never be!
For otherwise how will God judge the world?

3:7
But if the truth of God through my falsehood abounded to his glory,
why am I still also being judged as a sinner,

3:8
and not as we are slandered
and as some report us to say, Let us do evil
so that good may come?
Their condemnation is deserved.

3:9
What then? Are we better off?
Not at all, for we have already charged that both Jews and Greeks are all under sin,

3:10
as it is written: There is no one righteous, not even one,

3:11
there is no one who understands, there is no one who seeks God;

3:12
all have turned aside, together they have become worthless;
there is no one who does good,
there is not even one.

3:13
Their throat is an opened grave, with their tongues they practiced deceit,
the venom of asps is under their lips,

3:14
whose mouth is full of cursing and bitterness;

3:15
swift are their feet to shed blood,

3:16
ruin and misery are in their paths,

3:17
and the way of peace they have not known.

3:18
There is no fear of God before their eyes.

3:19
Now we know that whatever the law says it speaks to those under the law, so that every mouth may be stopped
and the whole world may become accountable to God;

3:20
because by works of the law no flesh will be justified before him,
for through the law comes knowledge of sin.

3:21
But now apart from the law the righteousness of God has been revealed,
being witnessed by the law and the prophets,

3:22
the righteousness of God through faith in Jesus Christ, for all who believe,
for there is no distinction.

3:23
For all have sinned
and fall short of the glory of God,

3:24
being justified freely by his grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus;

3:25
whom God set forth as a propitiation through faith in his blood
as a demonstration of his righteousness, because of the passing over of former sins

3:26
in the forbearance of God, for the demonstration of his righteousness
in the present time, so that he might be righteous
and the one who justifies the person who has faith in Jesus.

3:27
Where then is the boasting?
It is excluded.
By what kind of law? Of works?
No,
but by the law of faith.

3:28
For we reckon that a person is justified by faith apart from works of the law.

3:29
Or is God the God of Jews only?
Is he not also of the nations?
Yes, of the nations also,

3:30
since indeed God is one,
who will justify the circumcised by faith
and the uncircumcised through faith.

3:31
Do we then nullify the law through faith?
May it never be,
but we uphold the law.
