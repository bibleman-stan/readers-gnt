9:1
I speak the truth in Christ, I do not lie,
my conscience bearing witness with me in the Holy Spirit,

9:2
that I have great sorrow
and unceasing anguish in my heart;

9:3
for I could wish that I myself were accursed and cut off from Christ for the sake of my brothers, my kinsmen according to the flesh,

9:4
who are Israelites,
to whom belong the adoption and the glory
and the covenants and the giving of the law
and the worship and the promises,

9:5
to whom belong
the fathers, and from whom is the Christ according to the
flesh, who is over all, God blessed forever; amen.

9:6
But it is not as though the word of God has failed.
For not all who are descended from Israel are Israel;

9:7
nor because they are the seed of Abraham are they all
children, but: In Isaac shall your seed be called.

9:8
That is, it is not the children of the flesh who are children of
God, but the children of the promise are counted as seed;

9:9
for the word of promise is this:
At this time I will come
and Sarah shall have a son.

9:10
And not only so, but also Rebecca, having conceived by one man, Isaac our father;

9:11
for though the twins were not yet born and had not done anything good or bad,
so that the purpose of God according to election might stand,

9:12
not from works but from the one who calls,
it was said to her: The older shall serve the younger;

9:13
as it is written:
Jacob I loved,
but Esau I hated.

9:14
What then shall we say?
Is there injustice with God?
May it never be!

9:15
For he says to Moses:
I will have mercy on whom I have mercy,
and I will have compassion on whom I have compassion.

9:16
So then it does not depend on the one who wills or the one who runs, but on God who has mercy.

9:17
For the scripture says to Pharaoh: For this very purpose I raised you up,
so that I might demonstrate my power in you,
and so that my name might be proclaimed in all the earth.

9:18
So then he has mercy on whom he wills,
and he hardens whom he wills.

9:19
You will say to me then: Why does he still
find fault? For who has resisted his will?

9:20
on the contrary, who are
you, the one who answers back to God? Will the thing
formed say to the one who formed it, Why
did you make me this way?

9:21
Or does not the potter have authority over the clay,
from the same lump to make
one vessel for honor and another for dishonor?

9:22
And what if God, willing to demonstrate his wrath and to make known his power,
endured with much patience vessels of wrath prepared for destruction,

9:23
and so that he might make known the riches of his glory upon vessels of mercy,
which he prepared beforehand for glory,

9:24
even us whom he also called,
not from the Jews only but also from the nations? --

9:25
As he also says in Hosea:
I will call those who were not my people, my people,
and her who was not beloved, beloved;

9:26
and it shall be in the place
where it was said to them:
You are not my people, there they shall be called sons of the living God.

9:27
And Isaiah cries out concerning Israel:
Though the number of the sons of Israel be as the sand of the sea,
the remnant will be saved;

9:28
for the Lord will carry out his word upon the earth, completing it and cutting it short.

9:29
And as Isaiah has said before:
Unless the Lord of hosts had left us a seed,
we would have become like Sodom
and would have been made like Gomorrah.

9:30
What then shall we say?
That the nations who were not pursuing righteousness
attained righteousness, the righteousness that is from faith;

9:31
but Israel, pursuing a law of righteousness,
did not arrive at that law.

9:32
Why? Because it was not from faith but as from works;
they stumbled over the stumbling stone,

9:33
as it is written:
Behold, I lay in Zion a stone of stumbling and a rock of offense,
and the one who believes in him shall not be put to shame.
