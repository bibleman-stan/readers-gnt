27:1
And when it was decided that we should sail to Italy,
they delivered Paul and some other prisoners to a centurion
named Julius of the Augustan Cohort.

27:2
And embarking on a ship of Adramyttium
that was about to sail to the ports along the coast of Asia
we put to sea,
Aristarchus, a Macedonian from Thessalonica, being with us;

27:3
and the next day we put in at Sidon,
and Julius treated Paul kindly
and allowed him to go to his friends and receive care.

27:4
And putting out from there we sailed under the lee of Cyprus because the winds were against us,

27:5
and having sailed across the open sea along Cilicia and Pamphylia we came down
to Myra in Lycia.

27:6
And there the centurion found an Alexandrian ship
sailing for Italy and put us aboard it.

27:7
And sailing slowly for a number of days
and arriving with difficulty off Cnidus,
since the wind did not allow us to go further,
we sailed under the lee of Crete off Salmone,

27:8
and coasting along it with difficulty we came to a place
called Fair Havens
near which was the city of Lasea.

27:9
And since considerable time had passed
and the voyage was now dangerous because the Fast had already gone by,
Paul advised them

27:10
saying to them:
Men, I perceive that the voyage will be with damage and much loss not only of the cargo and the ship but also of our lives.

27:11
But the centurion was more persuaded by the pilot and the ship's owner
than by what Paul said.

27:12
And since the harbor was unsuitable for wintering
the majority decided to put out to sea from there,
if somehow they could reach Phoenix and winter there, a harbor of Crete
facing southwest and northwest.

27:13
And when the south wind blew gently
thinking they had obtained their purpose
they weighed anchor and sailed along Crete, close to shore.

27:14
But before long a violent wind called the northeaster rushed down from it;

27:15
and when the ship was caught
and could not face the wind
we gave way to it and were driven along.

27:16
And running under the lee of a small island
called Cauda
we managed with difficulty to secure the ship's boat,

27:17
which they hoisted up
and used supports to undergird
the ship;
and fearing that they would run aground on the Syrtis
they lowered the gear,
and so were driven along.

27:18
And since we were violently storm-tossed
the next day they began to throw the cargo overboard,

27:19
and on the third day they threw the ship's tackle overboard with their own hands.

27:20
And when neither sun nor stars appeared for many days,
and no small storm was pressing upon us,
all hope of our being saved was at last abandoned.

27:21
And since they had been without food for a long time
Paul stood up among them
and said:
Men, you should have listened to me
and not set sail from Crete
and so avoided this damage and loss.

27:22
And now I urge you to take heart,
for there will be no loss of life among you, only of the ship;

27:23
for this very night there stood before me an angel of the God
whose I am,
whom I serve,

27:24
saying:
Do not be afraid, Paul;
you must stand before Caesar,
and behold, God has granted you all those who sail with you.

27:25
Therefore take heart, men;
for I believe God that it will be exactly
as it has been told to me.

27:26
But we must run aground on some island.

27:27
And when the fourteenth night had come
as we were being driven across the Adriatic Sea,
about midnight the sailors suspected that they were approaching some land.

27:28
And taking soundings they found twenty fathoms;
and a little farther on taking soundings again they found fifteen fathoms;

27:29
and fearing that we might run aground on the rocks
they dropped four anchors from the stern
and prayed for day to come.

27:30
And as the sailors were seeking to escape from the ship
and had lowered the boat into the sea on the pretense of laying out anchors from the bow,
intending to flee,

27:31
Paul said to the centurion and the soldiers:
Unless these men stay in the ship,
you cannot be saved.

27:32
Then the soldiers cut away the ropes of the boat
and let it drift away.

27:33
And as day was about to dawn
Paul urged them all to take some food, saying:
Today is the fourteenth day that you have been waiting
and going without food,
having taken nothing;

27:34
therefore I urge you to take some food,
for this is for your preservation;
for not a hair from the head of any of you will perish.

27:35
And having said these things
and taking bread
he gave thanks to God in the presence of all
and breaking it he began to eat.

27:36
And they all became encouraged and also took food themselves.

27:37
And we were in all two hundred and seventy-six persons on the ship.

27:38
And when they had eaten enough
they lightened the ship
by throwing the wheat into the sea.

27:39
And when day came
they did not recognize the land,
but they noticed a bay
with a beach
onto which they planned to run the ship ashore if they could.

27:40
And casting off the anchors
they left them in the sea,
at the same time loosening the ropes of the rudders,
and hoisting the foresail to the wind
they made for the beach.

27:41
But striking a reef they ran the ship aground,
and the bow stuck and remained immovable,
but the stern was being broken up by the force of the waves.

27:42
And the soldiers' plan was
to kill the prisoners
lest any should swim away and escape;

27:43
but the centurion, wanting to save Paul,
kept them from their plan,
and he ordered those who could swim to jump overboard first and make for land,

27:44
and the rest
some on planks
and some on pieces from the ship;
and so it happened
that all were brought safely
to land.
