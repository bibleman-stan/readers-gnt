1:1
together with all the saints,
Paul, an apostle of Christ Jesus through the will of God, and Timothy the brother,
to the church of God that is in Corinth,
together with all the saints in the whole of Achaia;

1:2
grace to you and peace from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.

1:3
Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ,
the Father of mercies and God of all encouragement,

1:4
who encourages us in all our affliction,
so that we are able to encourage those in every affliction through the encouragement
with which we ourselves are encouraged by God.

1:5
Because just as the sufferings of Christ abound to us,
so also through Christ our encouragement abounds.

1:6
And if we are afflicted,
it is for your encouragement and salvation;
and if we are encouraged,
it is for your
encouragement that is produced in the endurance of the same sufferings
which we also suffer,

1:7
and our hope for you is firm;
knowing that as you are sharers of the sufferings,
so also of the encouragement.

1:8
For we do not want you to be unaware, brothers, of our affliction that happened in Asia,
that we were burdened excessively beyond our power,
so that we despaired even of living;

1:9
but we ourselves had the sentence of death within ourselves
so that we would not trust in ourselves
but in God who raises the dead;

1:10
who delivered us from so great a death and will deliver,
in whom we have hoped
that he will also still deliver,

1:11
as you also join in helping us through your prayer,
so that the gift granted to us through many may be thanked for by many on our behalf.

1:12
For our boasting is this, the testimony of our conscience,
that in holiness and sincerity of God,
not in fleshly wisdom but in the grace of God, we conducted ourselves in the world,
and especially toward you;

1:13
for we write nothing else to you than what you read
or also understand,
and I hope that you will understand until the end,

1:14
just as you also understood us in part,
that we are your boast just as
you also are ours in the day of our Lord Jesus.

1:15
And in this confidence I planned to come to you first,
so that you might have a second grace,

1:16
and through you to pass on into Macedonia,
and again from Macedonia to come to you
and by you to be sent on my way to Judea.

1:17
Therefore when I was planning this, I did not
use fickleness, did I?
Or what I plan,
do I plan according to the flesh, so that with me it is Yes yes and No no?

1:18
But God is faithful,
that our word to you is not Yes and No.

1:19
For the Son of God, Jesus Christ,
who was proclaimed among you through us,
through me and Silvanus and Timothy, did not become Yes and No,
but in him it has become Yes;

1:20
for as many as are the promises of God, in him is the Yes;
therefore also through him
is the Amen to God for glory through us.

1:21
Now the one who confirms us with you in Christ
and anointed us is God,

1:22
who also sealed us
and gave the pledge of the spirit in our hearts.

1:23
But I call God as witness upon my soul, that it was to spare you
that I did not come again to Corinth.

1:24
Not that we lord it over your faith,
but we are fellow workers for your joy,
for by faith you stand.
