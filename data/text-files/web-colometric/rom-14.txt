14:1
Accept the one who is weak in faith,
not for the purpose of disputes over opinions.

14:2
One person believes he may eat all things,
but the one who is weak eats only vegetables.

14:3
Let not the one who eats despise the one who does not eat, and let not the one who does not eat judge the one who eats,
for God has accepted him.

14:4
Who are you, the one who judges the servant of another?
To his own master he stands or falls;
and he will stand,
for the Lord is able to make him stand.

14:5
One person judges one day above another,
and another judges every day alike;
let each be fully convinced in his own mind;

14:6
the one who regards the day
regards it for the Lord.
And the one who eats, eats for the Lord,
for he gives thanks to God;
and the one who does not eat, for the Lord he does not eat,
and he gives thanks to God.

14:7
For none of us lives to himself,
and none dies to himself;

14:8
for if we live,
we live to the Lord,
and if we die,
we die to the Lord.
Whether
then we live
or we die,
we belong to the Lord.

14:9
For to this end Christ died and lived again,
so that he might be Lord of both the dead and the living.

14:10
But you, why do you judge your brother?
Or you also,
why do you despise your brother? For we shall all stand before the judgment seat of God,

14:11
for it is written:
As I live,
says the Lord,
every knee shall bow to me,
and every tongue shall confess to God.

14:12
So then each of us shall give an account of himself to God.

14:13
Therefore let us no longer judge one another;
but rather determine this, not to place a stumbling block or an offense before a brother.

14:14
I know and am persuaded in the Lord Jesus
that nothing is unclean in itself;
but to the one who considers something to be unclean, to that one it is unclean.

14:15
For if your brother is grieved because of food,
you are no longer walking according to love.
Do not by your food
destroy that one
for whom Christ died.

14:16
Therefore do not let your good be spoken of as evil.

14:17
For the kingdom of God is not eating and drinking,
but righteousness and peace and joy in the Holy Spirit;

14:18
for the one who serves Christ in this
is pleasing to God
and approved by people.

14:19
So then let us pursue the things of peace and the things that build up one another.

14:20
Do not tear down the work of God for the sake of food.
All things indeed are clean,
but it is wrong for the person who eats with offense.

14:21
It is good not to eat meat
nor to drink wine
nor to do anything by which your brother stumbles
or is offended or is weakened;

14:22
The faith that you have, keep to yourself
before God.
Blessed is the one who does not condemn himself in what he approves;

14:23
but the one who doubts is condemned if he eats,
because it is not from faith;
and everything
that is not from faith is sin.
