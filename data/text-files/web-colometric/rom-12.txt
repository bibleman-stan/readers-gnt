12:1
I urge you therefore
by the mercies of God, to present your bodies as a living sacrifice, holy, pleasing to
God, your reasonable worship;

12:2
and do not be conformed to this age,
the good and pleasing and perfect.
but be transformed by the renewing of the mind,
so that you may discern what is the will of God,

12:3
For by the grace given to me I say to everyone among you not to think more highly of yourself
than you ought to think, but to think so as to think soundly, to each one
as God has apportioned a measure of faith.

12:4
For just as in one body we have many members,
and the members do not all have the same function,

12:5
so we, the many, are one body in Christ,
and individually members of one another.

12:6
And having gifts that differ according to the grace given to us,
whether prophecy, in proportion to the faith,

12:7
or service, in the service, or the one who teaches, in the teaching,

12:8
or the one who exhorts, in the exhortation, the one who gives, with generosity, the one who leads,
with diligence, the one who shows mercy, with cheerfulness.

12:9
Let love be without hypocrisy.
Abhor what is evil,
cling to what is good;

12:10
in brotherly love, be devoted to one another,
in honor, give preference to one another,

12:11
in diligence, not lagging,
in the spirit, fervent,
serving the Lord,

12:12
in hope, rejoicing,
in affliction, enduring,
in prayer, persevering,

12:13
contributing to the needs of the saints,
pursuing hospitality.

12:14
Bless those who persecute you, bless
and do not curse.

12:15
Rejoice with those who rejoice, weep with those who weep.

12:16
Be of the same mind toward one another,
do not set your mind on high things
but associate with the lowly. Do not become
wise in your own estimation.

12:17
Repay no one evil for evil;
give thought to what is honorable before all people;

12:18
if possible, so far as it depends on you, be at peace with all people;

12:19
do not avenge yourselves
but give place to the wrath, for it is written: Vengeance is mine, I will repay, says the Lord.

12:20
But if your enemy is hungry,
feed him;
if he is thirsty, give him drink;
for by doing this
you will heap coals of fire on his head.

12:21
Do not be overcome by evil,
but overcome evil with good.
