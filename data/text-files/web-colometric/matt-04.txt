4:1
Then Jesus was led up into the wilderness by the Spirit,
to be tempted by the devil.

4:2
And having fasted forty days and forty nights he was hungry afterward.

4:3
And the tempter came and said to him,
If you are the Son of God,
command that these stones become bread.

4:4
But he answered and said, It is written: Not on bread alone will a person live
but on every word that proceeds from the mouth of God.

4:5
Then the devil takes him to the holy city,
and set him on the pinnacle of the temple,

4:6
and says to him,
If you are the Son of God,
throw yourself down;
for it is written that He will command his angels concerning you
and on their hands they will bear you up,
lest you strike your foot against a stone.

4:7
Jesus said to him,
Again it is written:
You shall not put the Lord your God to the test.

4:8
Again the devil takes him to a very high mountain,
and shows him all the kingdoms of the world and their glory

4:9
and said to him,
All these I will give you, if you fall down
and worship me.

4:10
Then Jesus says to him,
Go away, Satan;
for it is written
The Lord your God you shall worship
and him alone you shall serve.

4:11
Then the devil leaves him,
and look — angels came
and were ministering to him.

4:12
And hearing that John had been handed over
he withdrew to Galilee.

4:13
And leaving Nazareth
he went and settled in Capernaum by the sea
in the region of Zebulun and Naphtali;

4:14
so that what was spoken through Isaiah the prophet might be fulfilled, saying,

4:15
Land of Zebulun and land of Naphtali, the way of the sea,
beyond the Jordan, Galilee of the nations,

4:16
the people sitting in darkness saw a great light,
and for those sitting in the region and shadow of death a light has dawned on them.

4:17
From then on Jesus began to proclaim and say,
Repent,
for the kingdom of heaven has drawn near.

4:18
And walking beside the Sea of Galilee
he saw two brothers, Simon
the one called Peter
and Andrew his brother, casting a net into the sea,
for they were fishermen;

4:19
and he says to them,
Come after me, and I will make you fishers of people.

4:20
And they immediately left the nets
and followed him.

4:21
And going on from there he saw two other brothers, James the son of Zebedee and John his brother,
in the boat with Zebedee their father mending their nets,
and he called them.

4:22
And they immediately left the boat and their father
and followed him.

4:23
And he went about in all Galilee, teaching in their synagogues
and proclaiming the gospel of the kingdom
and healing every disease and every illness among the people.

4:24
And the report about him went out into all Syria;
and they brought to him all who were sick
afflicted with various diseases and torments, the demon-possessed
and the epileptic and the paralyzed, and he healed them.

4:25
and beyond the Jordan.
And great crowds followed him from Galilee and the Decapolis
and Jerusalem and Judea
