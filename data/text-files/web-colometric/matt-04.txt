4:1
Then Jesus was led up into the wilderness by the Spirit,
to be tempted by the devil.

4:2
And after fasting forty days and forty nights, he was hungry.

4:3
And the tempter came and said to him,
If you are the Son of God,
command these stones to become bread.

4:4
But he answered and said,
It is written: Man shall not live on bread alone,
but on every word that comes from the mouth of God.

4:5
Then the devil takes him to the holy city
and set him on the pinnacle of the temple,

4:6
and says to him,
If you are the Son of God,
throw yourself down;
for it is written that he will command his angels concerning you,
and on their hands they will bear you up,
lest you strike your foot against a stone.

4:7
Jesus said to him,
Again it is written:
You shall not put the Lord your God to the test.

4:8
Again the devil takes him to a very high mountain,
and shows him all the kingdoms of the world and their glory,

4:9
and said to him,
All these things I will give you,
if you fall down and worship me.

4:10
Then Jesus says to him, Be gone,
For
Satan!
it is written:
You shall worship the Lord your God,
and him only shall you serve.

4:11
Then the devil leaves him,
and look -- angels came
and were ministering to him.

4:12
Now when he heard that John had been arrested,
he withdrew to Galilee.

4:13
And leaving Nazareth behind,
he went and settled in Capernaum by the sea,
in the region of Zebulun and Naphtali;

4:14
so that what was spoken through Isaiah the prophet might be fulfilled:

4:15
Land of Zebulun and land of Naphtali,
the way of the sea, beyond the Jordan,
Galilee of the Gentiles --

4:16
the people sitting in darkness saw a great light,
and for those sitting in the region and shadow of death, a light has dawned on them.

4:17
From that time Jesus began to preach and say,
Repent,
for the kingdom of heaven has drawn near.

4:18
Now walking beside the Sea of Galilee,
he saw two brothers,
Simon called Peter and Andrew his brother,
casting a net into the sea,
for they were fishermen;

4:19
and he says to them,
Come, follow me,
and I will make you fishers of people.

4:20
And immediately leaving their nets,
they followed him.

4:21
And going on from there he saw two other brothers,
James the son of Zebedee and John his brother,
in the boat with Zebedee their father, mending their nets,
and he called them.

4:22
And immediately leaving the boat and their father,
they followed him.

4:23
And he went throughout all Galilee,
teaching in their synagogues
and proclaiming the gospel of the kingdom
and healing every disease and every affliction among the people.

4:24
And the report about him spread throughout all Syria;
and they brought to him all who were sick,
afflicted with various diseases and pains,
the demon-possessed and epileptics and paralytics,
and he healed them.

4:25
And great crowds followed him from Galilee and the Decapolis
and Jerusalem and Judea
and beyond the Jordan.
