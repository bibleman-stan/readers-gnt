1:1
The elder to the beloved Gaius,
whom I love in truth.

1:2
Beloved, I pray that in all things you may prosper and be in good health,
just as your soul prospers.

1:3
For I rejoiced greatly when brothers came and testified to your truth,
just as you walk in the truth.

1:4
I have no greater joy than this, that I hear my children
walking in the truth.

1:5
Beloved, you act faithfully
in whatever you do for the brothers,
and this for strangers,

1:6
who testified to your love before the church.
You will do well to send them on their way in a manner worthy of God.

1:7
For on behalf of the Name they went out,
receiving nothing from the Gentiles.

1:8
Therefore we ought to support such ones,
so that we may become fellow workers for the truth.

1:9
I wrote something to the church,
but Diotrephes, who loves to be first among them, does not receive us.

1:10
Because of this, if I come,
I will bring up his works that he does,
slandering us with evil words.
And not being satisfied with this, he himself does not receive the brothers,
and those who want to he prevents,
and casts them out of the church.

1:11
Beloved, do not imitate what is evil
but what is good.
The one who does good is of God.
The one who does evil
has not seen God.

1:12
Demetrius has been testified about by everyone and by the truth itself.
And we also testify,
and you know that our testimony is true.

1:13
I had many things to write to you,
but I do not want to write to you with ink and pen.

1:14
But I hope to see you shortly,
and we will speak face to face.

1:15
Peace to you.
The friends greet you.
Greet the friends by name.
