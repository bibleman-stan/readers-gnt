15:1
Then Pharisees and scribes from Jerusalem come to Jesus saying

15:2
Why do your disciples transgress the tradition of the elders?
For they do not wash their hands
when they eat bread.

15:3
And he answered and said to them,
Why do you also transgress the commandment of God for the sake of your tradition?

15:4
For God said,
Honor your father and your mother,
and, The one who speaks evil of father or mother must surely die;

15:5
but you say,
Whoever says to his father or his mother,
Whatever you would have gained from me is a gift to God,

15:6
he need not honor his father;
and you have nullified the word of God for the sake of your tradition.

15:7
Hypocrites, well did Isaiah prophesy about you saying,

15:8
This people honors me with their lips,
but their heart is far from me;

15:9
and in vain they worship me,
teaching as doctrines the commandments of men.

15:10
And summoning the crowd
he said to them,
Hear and understand:

15:11
it is not what goes into the mouth that defiles a person,
but what comes out of the mouth — this defiles a person.

15:12
Then the disciples came and say to him,
Do you know that the Pharisees were offended when they heard this saying?

15:13
And he answered and said,
Every plant that my heavenly Father did not plant will be uprooted.

15:14
Leave them;
they are blind guides of the blind;
and if a blind man
guides a blind man, both will fall into a pit.

15:15
And Peter answered and said to him,
Explain this parable to us.

15:16
And he said,
Are you also still without understanding?

15:17
Do you not see that everything that enters the mouth goes into the stomach
and is expelled?

15:18
But the things that come out of the mouth come from the heart,
and those things defile a person.

15:19
For from the heart come evil thoughts, murders, adulteries, sexual immoralities, thefts, false testimonies, slanders.

15:20
These are what defile a person,
but eating with unwashed hands
does not defile a person.

15:21
And going out from there Jesus withdrew to the region of Tyre and Sidon.

15:22
And look — a Canaanite woman from that region came out and was crying,
Have mercy on me, Lord, Son of David;
my daughter is severely demon-possessed.

15:23
But he did not answer her a word.
And his disciples came and were urging him saying,
Send her away, for she keeps crying out after us.

15:24
And he answered and said,
I was not sent except to the lost sheep of the house of Israel.

15:25
But she came and was worshiping him saying,
Lord, help me.

15:26
And he answered and said,
It is not good to take the bread of the children
and throw it to the dogs.

15:27
And she said,
Yes, Lord, yet even the dogs eat
from the crumbs falling from the table of their masters.

15:28
Then Jesus answered and said to her,
O woman, great is your faith;
let it be done for you as you wish.
And her daughter was healed from that hour.

15:29
And departing from there Jesus came beside the Sea of Galilee,
and going up on the mountain he sat down there.

15:30
And great crowds came to him
having with them the lame, blind, crippled, mute, and many others,
and they laid them at his feet,
and he healed them;

15:31
so that the crowd marveled
seeing the mute speaking, the crippled made whole,
and the lame walking and the blind seeing;
and they glorified the God of Israel.

15:32
And Jesus, summoning his disciples,
said, I have compassion on the crowd, because they have stayed with me already three days and have nothing
to eat;
and to send them away hungry
I am unwilling, lest they collapse on the way.

15:33
And the disciples say to him,
Where in a desolate place would we get enough bread
to satisfy so great a crowd?

15:34
And Jesus says to them,
How many loaves do you have?
And they said,
Seven,
and a few small fish.

15:35
And he commanded the crowd to sit down on the ground

15:36
and took the seven loaves and the fish
and giving thanks he broke them
and gave them to the disciples
and the disciples to the crowds.

15:37
And they all ate
and were satisfied,
and they picked up the remaining broken pieces — seven baskets full.

15:38
And those who ate were four thousand men, besides women and children.

15:39
And dismissing the crowds
he got into the boat,
and went to the region of Magadan.
