14:1
And it happened when he came
into the house of one of the rulers of the Pharisees on a Sabbath to eat bread,
and they were watching him closely.

14:2
And behold, a certain man who had dropsy was before him.

14:3
And Jesus answered and spoke to the lawyers and Pharisees, saying,
Is it lawful to heal on the Sabbath or not?

14:4
But they were silent.
And taking hold of him he healed him and let him go.

14:5
And he said to them,
Which of you, having a son or an ox fall into a well,
will not immediately pull him out on the day of the Sabbath?

14:6
And they were not able to reply to these things.

14:7
And he was telling a parable to those who had been invited,
noticing how they were choosing the places of honor,
saying to them,

14:8
When you are invited by someone to a wedding feast,
do not recline at the place of honor,
lest someone more distinguished than you may have been invited by him,

14:9
and the one who invited both you and him
will say to you,
Give this man your place,
and then you will begin with shame to take the last place.

14:10
But when you are invited, go and recline at the last place,
so that when the one who invited you comes,
he will say to you,
Friend, move up higher;
then there will be honor for you before all who recline at table with you.

14:11
For everyone who exalts himself will be humbled,
and the one who humbles himself will be exalted.

14:12
And he was also saying to the one who had invited him,
When you give a lunch or a dinner,
do not call your friends or your brothers or your relatives or rich neighbors,
lest they also invite you in return
and it become repayment to you.

14:13
But when you give a feast,
invite the poor, the crippled, the lame, the blind;

14:14
and you will be blessed,
for they have nothing to repay you; for you will be repaid
at the resurrection of the righteous.

14:15
And one of those reclining at table with him, hearing these things, said to him,
Blessed is the one who will eat bread in the kingdom of God.

14:16
But he said to him,
A certain man was giving a great banquet,
and he invited many,

14:17
and he sent his servant at the hour of the banquet to say to those who had been invited,
Come, for everything is already prepared.

14:18
And they all alike began to make excuses.
The first said to him,
I have bought a field
and I must go out and see it;
I ask you, have me excused.

14:19
And another said,
I have bought five yoke of oxen
and I am going to test them;
I ask you, have me excused.

14:20
And another said,
I have married a wife
and therefore I am not able to come.

14:21
And the servant came and reported these things to his master.
Then the master of the house, becoming angry,
said to his servant,
Go out quickly into the streets
and lanes of the city,
and bring in here the poor and crippled and blind and lame.

14:22
And the servant said,
Master, what you ordered has been done,
and there is still room.

14:23
And the master said to the servant,
Go out to the roads and hedges
and compel them to come in,
so that my house may be filled;

14:24
for I tell you that none of those men who were invited
will taste my banquet.

14:25
And large crowds were going along with him,
and turning he said to them,

14:26
If anyone comes to me
and does not hate his own father and mother and wife and children
and brothers and sisters, and even his own life,
he is not able to be my disciple.

14:27
Whoever does not carry his own cross
and come after me,
is not able to be my disciple.

14:28
For which of you, wanting to build a tower,
does not first sit down and count the cost, whether he has enough to complete it?

14:29
Lest after he has laid a foundation
and is not able to finish,
all who observe begin to mock him,

14:30
saying, This man began to build
and was not able to finish.

14:31
Or what king, going to engage another king in battle,
will not first sit down and consider whether he is able
with ten thousand to meet the one coming against him with twenty thousand?

14:32
Otherwise,
while the other is still far away,
he sends a delegation
and asks for terms of peace.

14:33
So therefore every one of you
who does not renounce all his own possessions
is not able to be my disciple.

14:34
Salt therefore is good;
but if
even the salt has lost its flavor,
with what will it be seasoned?

14:35
It is fit neither for the soil nor for the manure pile;
they throw it out.
The one who has ears to hear, let him hear.
