1:1
Jude, a servant of Jesus Christ and brother of James, to those who are loved in God the Father
and kept for Jesus Christ, called:

1:2
may mercy and peace and love be multiplied to you.

1:3
Beloved, while making every effort to write to you about our common salvation
I found it necessary to write to you
urging you to contend earnestly
for the faith once for all delivered to the saints.

1:4
For certain people have crept in unnoticed, those who long ago were designated for this condemnation,
ungodly, who pervert the grace of our God into sensuality
and deny our only Master and Lord Jesus Christ.

1:5
Now I want to remind you, though you once fully knew all this,
that Jesus, having saved a people out of the land of Egypt,
the second time destroyed those who did not believe,

1:6
and angels who did not keep their own domain
but abandoned their proper dwelling
he has kept in eternal bonds under darkness for the judgment of the great day;

1:7
just as Sodom and Gomorrah and the surrounding cities,
which in the same manner as these indulged in sexual immorality
and went after different flesh, are exhibited as an example
undergoing the punishment of eternal fire.

1:8
Yet in the same way these also, dreaming, defile the flesh,
and reject authority,
and blaspheme the glorious ones.

1:9
But Michael the archangel,
when contending with the devil he disputed about the body of Moses,
did not dare to bring a judgment of blasphemy,
but said: The Lord rebuke you.

1:10
But these blaspheme whatever they do not understand,
and whatever they know by instinct like irrational animals, by these things they are destroyed.

1:11
Woe to them,
because they walked in the way of Cain,
and rushed headlong into the error of Balaam for reward,
and perished in the rebellion of Korah.

1:12
These are the hidden reefs at your love feasts,
feasting together without fear, shepherding only themselves, waterless clouds
carried along by winds, fruitless autumn trees, twice dead, uprooted,

1:13
wild waves of the sea foaming up their own shame,
wandering stars
for whom the gloom of darkness has been reserved forever.

1:14
And Enoch also, the seventh from Adam, prophesied about these, saying:
Behold, the Lord came with his holy myriads,

1:15
to execute judgment against all
and to convict all the ungodly of all their works of ungodliness
which they committed in ungodliness
and of all the harsh things
which ungodly sinners spoke against him.

1:16
These are grumblers, malcontents,
walking according to their own desires, and their mouth speaks arrogant words,
flattering people for the sake of advantage.

1:17
But you, beloved, remember the words spoken beforehand by the apostles of our Lord Jesus Christ;

1:18
that they were saying to you:
In the last time there will be scoffers
walking according to their own desires of ungodliness.

1:19
These are the ones who cause divisions,
worldly-minded, not having the Spirit.

1:20
But you, beloved, building yourselves up on your most holy faith,
praying in the Holy Spirit,

1:21
keep yourselves in the love of God
waiting for the mercy of our Lord Jesus Christ leading to eternal life.

1:22
And have mercy on those who are doubting,

1:23
and others save
snatching them out of the fire,
and on others have mercy with fear,
hating even the garment stained by the flesh.

1:24
Now to the one who is able to keep you from stumbling
and to present you blameless before his glory with great joy

1:25
before all time and now and forever and ever. Amen.
to the only God our Savior
through Jesus Christ our Lord be glory, majesty, dominion, and authority
