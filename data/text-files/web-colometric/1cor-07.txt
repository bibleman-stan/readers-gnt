7:1
Now concerning the matters about which you wrote:
"It is good for a man not to touch a woman."

7:2
But because of sexual immorality, each man should have his own wife,
and each woman should have her own husband.

7:3
The husband should fulfill his obligation to his wife,
and likewise the wife to her husband.

7:4
The wife does not have authority over her own body,
but the husband does. Likewise,
the husband does not have authority over his own body, but the wife does.

7:5
Do not deprive one another, except perhaps
by mutual agreement for a time,
so that you may devote yourselves to prayer,
and then come together again,
so that Satan may not tempt you because of your lack of self-control.

7:6
I say this as a concession,
not as a command.

7:7
I wish that all people were as I myself am.
But each has his own gift from God,
one of one kind
and one of another.

7:8
To the unmarried and the widows I say that it is good for them if they remain as I am.

7:9
But if they cannot exercise self-control, they should marry,
for it is better to marry than to burn with passion.

7:10
To the married I give this charge -- not I, but the Lord -- that a wife should not separate from her husband

7:11
(but if she does separate,
let her remain unmarried or be reconciled to her husband),
and a husband should not divorce his wife.

7:12
To the rest I say -- I, not the Lord:
if any brother has an unbelieving wife,
and she consents to live with him,
he should not divorce her.

7:13
And if any woman has an unbelieving husband,
and he consents to live with her,
she should not divorce her husband.

7:14
For the unbelieving husband is made holy through his wife,
and the unbelieving wife is made holy through the brother.
Otherwise your children would be unclean,
but as it is, they are holy.

7:15
But if the unbelieving partner separates,
let him separate.
The brother or the sister is not enslaved
in such cases.
God has called you in peace.

7:16
For how do you know,
wife,
whether you will save your husband?
Or how do you know,
husband,
whether you will save your wife?

7:17
Only as the Lord has assigned to each,
as God has called each,
so let him walk.
And so I direct in all the churches.

7:18
Was anyone called while circumcised?
Let him not undo it.
Was anyone called while uncircumcised? Let him not be circumcised.

7:19
Circumcision is nothing,
and uncircumcision is nothing,
but keeping the commandments of God is what matters.

7:20
Each person should remain in the calling
in which he was called.

7:21
Were you called as a slave? Do not let it trouble you.
But if indeed you can gain your freedom, make the most of it.

7:22
For the one called in the Lord as a slave is the Lord's freedman. Likewise,
the one who was free when called is a slave of Christ.

7:23
You were bought with a price.
Do not become slaves of men.

7:24
Each one, in the condition in which he was called,
brothers,
let him remain there with God.

7:25
Now concerning the virgins, I have no command from the Lord,
but I give my judgment as one who by the Lord's mercy is trustworthy.

7:26
I consider then that this is good because of the present distress:
that it is good for a person to remain as he is.

7:27
Are you bound to a wife?
Do not seek release.
Are you free from a wife?
Do not seek a wife.

7:28
But if you do marry,
you have not sinned.
And if a virgin marries,
she has not sinned. Yet those
who marry will have worldly troubles,
and I would spare you that.

7:29
This is what I mean,
brothers:
the appointed time has grown very short.
From now on, let those who have wives
live as though they had none,

7:30
and those who mourn as though not mourning, and those who rejoice as though not rejoicing,
and those who buy as though they had no goods,

7:31
and those who deal with the world
as though they had no dealings with it.
For the present form of this world is passing away.

7:32
I want you to be free from anxieties.
The unmarried man is anxious about the things of the Lord, how he may please the Lord.

7:33
But the married man is anxious about worldly things, how he may please his wife,

7:34
and his interests are divided. The unmarried woman or virgin is anxious about the things of the Lord, to be holy
both in body and in spirit.
But the married woman is anxious about worldly things, how she may please her husband.

7:35
I say this for your own benefit, not to lay any restraint upon you,
but to promote what is seemly and devoted attendance upon the Lord without distraction.

7:36
If anyone thinks he is behaving improperly toward his betrothed,
if she is past her youth and it has to be, let him do as he wishes;
he does not sin.
Let them marry.

7:37
But whoever stands firm in his heart,
being under no compulsion,
but having authority over his own will,
and has determined in his own heart to keep her as his betrothed, he will do well.

7:38
So then the one who marries his betrothed
does well, and the one who refrains from marriage will do even better.

7:39
A wife is bound
for as long as her husband lives.
But if her husband dies, she is free
to marry whomever she wishes,
only in the Lord.

7:40
Yet she is happier if she remains as she is, in my judgment,
and I think that I too have the Spirit of God.
