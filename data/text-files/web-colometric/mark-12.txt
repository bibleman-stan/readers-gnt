12:1
And He began to them in parables to speak
A vineyard a man planted
and he placed around it a fence
and dug a wine vat
and built a tower
and rented out it to farmers
and traveled abroad

12:2
And he sent to the farmers at the due time a servant
that from the farmers he might receive from the fruit of the vineyard

12:3
But having taken him they beat him
and sent him away empty-handed

12:4
And again he sent to them another servant
and him they struck on the head
and treated shamefully

12:5
And another He sent
and him they killed
also many others
some indeed beating
some then killing

12:6
Yet one having beloved son
he sent him last to them
saying They will have respect for the son of me

12:7
Those however farmers to themselves said This is the heir
come let us kill him
and ours will be the inheritance

12:8
And having taken they killed him
and cast forth him outside the vineyard

12:9
What will do the master of the vineyard
He will come
and will destroy the farmers
and will give the vineyard to others

12:10
Not even the Scripture this have you read
The stone which rejected those building
this has become the chief corner

12:11
from the Lord was this
and it is marvelous in the eyes of us

12:12
And they were seeking Him to lay hold of
and they feared the crowd
they knew for that against them the parable He had spoken
And having left Him they went away

12:13
And they send to him some of the Pharisees and of the Herodians
that Him they might catch in discourse

12:14
And having come they say to him
Teacher we know that true You are
and not there is care to You about no one
not for You look on the appearance of men
but on the basis of the truth the way of God teach
Is it lawful to give tribute to Caesar or not
Should we pay or not pay

12:15
And knowing of them the hypocrisy
He said to them
Why Me do you test
Bring Me a denarius that I might see it

12:16
And they brought it
And He says to them
Whose is the likeness this and the inscription
And they said to him Caesar’s

12:17
And Jesus said to them
The things of Caesar give back to Caesar
and the things of God to God
And they were amazed at Him

12:18
And come Sadducees to him
who say a resurrection not there is
and they began questioning Him saying

12:19
Teacher Moses wrote for us
that if of anyone a brother should die
and leave behind a wife
and not leave children
that should take the brother of him the wife
and raise up seed for the brother of him

12:20
Seven brothers there were
and the first took a wife
and dying not left seed

12:21
And the second took her and died
not having left seed
and the third
likewise

12:22
And the seven not left seed
Last of all
also the woman died

12:23
In the resurrection when they rise
of which of them will she be wife
For the seven had her as wife

12:24
Was saying to them Jesus Not because of this do you err not knowing the Scriptures
nor the power of God

12:25
When for out from the dead they rise neither do they marry nor are given in marriage
but are like angels in the heavens

12:26
Concerning now the dead that they rise
not have you read in the book of Moses on the bush how spoke to him God saying
I am the God of Abraham and the God of Isaac and the God of Jacob

12:27
Not He is God of the dead
but of the living
Greatly you err

12:28
And having come up one of the scribes having heard them reasoning together
saw that well He answered them
he questioned Him
Which is commandment the first of all

12:29
Answered Jesus The foremost is
Hear this O Israel
The Lord God of us the Lord One is

12:30
and with all the mind of you and with all the strength of you
and you shall love the Lord God of you
with all the heart of you and with all the soul of you

12:31
The second this
You shall love the neighbor of you as yourself
Greater than these another commandment not there is

12:32
And said to him the scribe
Right Teacher according to truth You have spoken that One He is
and not there is another besides Him

12:33
and to love Him with all the heart
and with all the understanding and with all the strength
and to love the neighbor
as oneself more important is than all the burnt offerings and sacrifices

12:34
And Jesus having seen him
that wisely he answered
said to him
Not far are you from the kingdom of God
And no one no longer dared Him to question

12:35
And answering Jesus was saying teaching in the temple
How say the scribes that the Christ the son of David is

12:36
Himself David said by the Holy Spirit
Said the Lord to the Lord of me
Sit at the right hand of me
until I place of the enemies of You as a footstool of the feet of You

12:37
Himself David calls Him Lord
then how of him is He son
And the great crowd was listening to him gladly

12:38
And in the teaching of him He was saying
Beware of the scribes desiring
in robes to walk about
and greetings in the marketplaces

12:39
and first seats in the synagogues and first places at the feasts

12:40
those devouring the houses of the widows
and as a pretext at great length praying
These will receive greater judgment

12:41
And having sat down opposite the treasury He was watching
how the crowd cast money into the treasury
and many rich were casting in much

12:42
And having come one widow poor cast in lepta two
which is a kodrantes

12:43
And having called to him the disciples of him
He says to them
Truly
I say to you
that the widow this poor more than all has cast in of those casting into the treasury

12:44
All for out of that which was abounding to them cast in
she however out of the poverty of her all as much as she had
cast in all the livelihood of her
