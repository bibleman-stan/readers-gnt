5:1
And they came to the other side of the sea into the region of the Gerasenes.

5:2
And when he had come out of the boat,
at once a man with an unclean spirit met him from the tombs,

5:3
who had his dwelling among the tombs,
and no one was able to bind him anymore, not even with a chain

5:4
because he had often been bound with shackles and chains
and the chains had been torn apart by him
and the shackles shattered,
and no one had the strength to subdue him.

5:5
And through every night and day among the tombs and in the mountains he was crying out
and cutting himself with stones.

5:6
And seeing Jesus from far off he ran
and fell on his knees before him,

5:7
and crying out with a loud voice he says:
What have you to do with me, Jesus, Son of the Most High God?
I put you under oath by God — do not torment me.

5:8
For he had been saying to him:
Come out of the man, you unclean spirit.

5:9
And he asked him:
What is your name?
And he says to him: Legion
is my name, because we are many.

5:10
And he kept begging him urgently
not to send them out of the region.

5:11
Now there was a large herd of pigs feeding there near the mountain.

5:12
And they begged him saying:
Send us into the pigs,
so that we may enter them.

5:13
And he gave them permission.
And the unclean spirits came out and entered the pigs,
and the herd rushed down the steep bank into the sea — about two thousand —
and they were drowned in the sea.

5:14
And those tending them fled
and reported it in the city and in the countryside.
And they came to see
what it was that had happened.

5:15
And they come to Jesus,
and they see the possessed man
sitting, clothed and in his right mind,
the one who had had the legion,
and they were afraid.

5:16
And those who had seen it described to them how it happened to the possessed man
and about the pigs.

5:17
And they began to beg him to leave their region.

5:18
And as he was getting into the boat,
the man who had been possessed begged him
that he might be with him.

5:19
And he did not allow him,
but says to him:
Go to your home, to your own people,
and report to them
all that the Lord has done for you
and had mercy on you.

5:20
And he went away and began to proclaim in the Decapolis
all that Jesus had done for him,
and everyone was amazed.

5:21
And when Jesus had crossed again in the boat to the other side,
a great crowd gathered around him,
and he was beside the sea.

5:22
And one of the synagogue rulers comes,
Jairus by name,
and seeing him
falls at his feet

5:23
and begs him urgently
saying: My little daughter is at the point of death — come
and lay your hands on her so that she may be healed and live.

5:24
And he went with him.
And a great crowd was following him
and pressing against him.

5:25
And a woman who had had a flow of blood for twelve years

5:26
and had suffered much under many physicians
and had spent all she had
and had gained nothing
but rather had grown worse,

5:27
having heard about Jesus,
came up behind him in the crowd
and touched his garment.

5:28
For she had been saying: If I touch even his garments I will be healed.

5:29
And at once the flow of her blood dried up,
and she knew in her body that she was healed from her affliction.

5:30
And at once Jesus, perceiving in himself
the power that had gone out from him,
turned around in the crowd and said:
Who touched my garments?

5:31
And his disciples said to him:
You see the crowd pressing against you,
and you say: Who touched me?

5:32
And he was looking around to see the woman who had done this.

5:33
But the woman, afraid and trembling, knowing
what had happened to her, came
and fell before him
and told him the whole truth.

5:34
And he said to her:
Daughter, your faith has healed you.
Go in peace,
and be well from your affliction.

5:35
While he was still speaking, they come from the synagogue ruler's house
saying: Your daughter has died.
Why trouble the teacher any longer?

5:36
But Jesus, overhearing
the word being spoken,
says to the synagogue ruler:
Do not fear — only believe.

5:37
And he allowed no one to follow with him
except Peter and James and John the brother of James.

5:38
And they come to the house of the synagogue ruler,
and he sees a commotion, and people weeping and wailing loudly,

5:39
and going in he says to them:
Why are you making a commotion and weeping?
The child has not died but is sleeping.

5:40
And they were laughing at him.
But he, putting them all outside,
takes the father of the child
and the mother and those with him,
and goes in
where the child was.

5:41
And taking hold of the child's hand
he says to her:
Talitha koum,
which is translated:
Little girl, I say to you, arise.

5:42
And at once the girl stood up
and began walking,
for she was twelve years old.
And at once they were overcome with great amazement.

5:43
And he strictly ordered them that no one should know this,
and he said to give her something to eat.
