6:1
Brothers,
If even should be overcome a man in some trespass,
You the spiritual ones restore such a one in a spirit of gentleness considering yourself
Lest also you be tempted.

6:2
One another’s burdens bear you,
And thus you shall fulfill the law of Christ.

6:3
If for thinks himself anyone to be something nothing being,
He deceives himself;

6:4
But the work of himself test each,
And then as to himself alone the ground of boasting he will have
And not as to another,

6:5
For each his own load shall bear.

6:6
Let share now the one being taught in the word
With the one teaching in all good things.

6:7
Not be misled God not is mocked;
For whatever if might sow a man,
That also he will reap;

6:8
For the one sowing to the flesh of himself from the flesh will reap decay,
The one however sowing to the Spirit from the Spirit will reap life eternal.

6:9
And in well doing not we should grow weary,
In time for due we will reap a harvest not giving up.

6:10
So then as occasion have,
We should work good toward all,
Especially now toward those of the household of the of faith.

6:11
See in how large to you letters I have written my with own hand.

6:12
As many as wish to have a fair appearance in the flesh,
These compel you to be circumcised,
Only that for the cross of Christ not they might be persecuted;

6:13
Not even for those being circumcised themselves the Law keep,
But they desire you to be circumcised
So that your in flesh they may boast.

6:14
For me however never may it be to boast
If never in the cross our of Lord Jesus Christ,
Through which to me the world has been crucified and I to the world.

6:15
For neither circumcision anything is nor uncircumcision instead a new creation.

6:16
And as many as those who rule by this will walk,
Peace be upon them and mercy and upon the Israel of God.

6:17
The henceforth troubles to me no one let give,
For i the marks of Jesus on my of body bear.

6:18
The grace our of Lord Jesus Christ be with the spirit of you,
Brothers;
Amen.
