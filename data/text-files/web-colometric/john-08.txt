8:1
But Jesus went to the Mount of Olives.

8:2
And at dawn he came again to the temple, and all the people were coming.
And having sat down he was teaching them.

8:3
And the scribes and the Pharisees bring to him a woman
caught in adultery.
And having stood her in the midst,

8:4
they say to him, testing him,
Teacher, this woman was caught in the very act of committing adultery.

8:5
Now in the law Moses commanded us to stone such women.
So what do you say?

8:6
Now they were saying this
testing him,
so that they might have grounds to accuse him.
But Jesus, having stooped down, was writing on the ground with his finger, not responding.

8:7
But as they continued asking him, he straightened up and said to them,
The one without sin among you, let him throw the first stone at her.

8:8
And again having stooped down he was writing on the ground.

8:9
And having heard, and being convicted by their conscience, they were going out one by one,
beginning from the elders. And Jesus was left alone
with the woman standing in the midst.

8:10
And Jesus, having straightened up and having seen no one except the woman,
said to her,
Where are those accusers of yours?
Has no one condemned you?

8:11
And she said,
No one, Lord. Jesus
then said,
Neither do I condemn you.
Go
and sin no longer.]]

8:12
So again Jesus spoke to them saying,
I am the light of the world.
The one who follows me
will never walk in the darkness,
but will have the light of life.

8:13
So the Pharisees said to him,
You testify about yourself.
Your testimony is not valid.

8:14
Jesus answered
and said to them,
Even if I testify about myself,
my testimony is valid, because I know where I came from and where I am going.
But you do not know where I come from
or where I am going.

8:15
You judge according to the flesh. I judge no one.

8:16
And even if I do judge, my judgment is true,
because I am not alone,
but I and the Father who sent me.

8:17
And in your own law it is written that the testimony of two people is valid.

8:18
I am the one who testifies about myself
and the Father who sent me testifies about me.

8:19
So they were saying to him,
Where is your Father?
Jesus answered,
You know neither me
nor my Father.
If you knew me, you would know my Father also.

8:20
These words he spoke in the treasury while teaching in the temple.
And no one seized him,
because his hour had not yet come.

8:21
So he said to them again,
I am going away
and you will seek me,
and you will die in your sin.
Where I am going
you cannot come.

8:22
So the Judeans were saying,
He will not kill himself, will he,
since he says,
Where I am going
you cannot come?

8:23
And he was saying to them,
You are from below,
I am from above.
You are of this world,
I am not of this world.

8:24
Therefore I said to you that you will die in your sins.
For if you do not believe that I am,
you will die in your sins.

8:25
So they were saying to him,
Who are you?
Jesus said to them,
What I have been telling you from the beginning.

8:26
I have many things to speak and to judge about you.
But the one who sent me is true,
and what I heard from him,
these things I speak to the world.

8:27
They did not understand that he was speaking to them about the Father.

8:28
So Jesus said,
When you lift up the Son of Man,
then you will know that I am, and I do nothing from myself,
but just as the Father taught me, these things I speak.

8:29
And the one who sent me
is with me.
He has not left me alone,
because I always do the things pleasing to him.

8:30
As he was speaking these things
many believed in him.

8:31
So Jesus was saying to the Judeans who had believed him, If you remain in my word,
you are truly my disciples,

8:32
and you will know the truth,
and the truth will set you free.

8:33
They answered him,
We are offspring of Abraham
and have never been enslaved to anyone.
How do you say, You will become free?

8:34
Jesus answered them, Truly truly I say to you that everyone who commits sin
is a slave of sin.

8:35
And the slave does not remain in the house into the age.
The son remains into the age.

8:36
So if the Son sets you free,
you will be truly free.

8:37
I know that you are offspring of Abraham.
But you seek to kill me,
because my word has no place in you.

8:38
What I have seen in the presence of the Father
I speak.
And you therefore
do what you heard from your father.

8:39
They answered
and said to him,
Our father is Abraham.
Jesus says to them,
If you are children of Abraham,
you would be doing
the works of Abraham.

8:40
But now you seek to kill me, a man
who has spoken the truth to you
that I heard from God.
This Abraham did not do.

8:41
You are doing the works of your father.
They said to him,
We were not born from sexual immorality.
We have one Father, God.

8:42
Jesus said to them,
If God were your Father
you would love me, for I came forth from God
and I am here.
For I have not come of myself,
but that one sent me.

8:43
Why do you not understand my speech?
Because you cannot hear my word.

8:44
You are of your father the devil
and you want to do the desires of your father.
That one was a murderer from the beginning,
and has not stood in the truth,
because there is no truth in him.
When he speaks falsehood,
he speaks from his own nature,
because he is a liar
and the father of it.

8:45
But because I speak the truth,
you do not believe me.

8:46
Which of you convicts me of sin?
If I speak truth,
why do you not believe me?

8:47
The one who is from God hears the words of God.
For this reason you do not hear,
because you are not from God.

8:48
The Judeans answered
and said to him,
Do we not rightly say that you are a Samaritan
and have a demon?

8:49
Jesus answered,
I do not have a demon,
but I honor my Father,
and you dishonor me.

8:50
But I do not seek my own glory.
There is one who seeks and judges.

8:51
Truly truly I say to you,
if anyone keeps my word,
he will never see death into the age.

8:52
The Judeans said to him,
Now we know that you have a demon.
Abraham died
and the prophets, and you say,
If anyone keeps my word,
he will never taste death into the age.

8:53
You are not greater than our father Abraham, are you,
who died?
And the prophets died.
Who do you make yourself to be?

8:54
Jesus answered,
If I glorify myself,
my glory is nothing.
It is my Father
who glorifies me,
whom you say
that he is our God,
he is,

8:55
and you have not known him.
But I know him.
And if I were to say
that I do not know him, I would be like you, a liar.
But I know him and I keep his word.

8:56
Abraham your father rejoiced
that he might see my day, and he saw it and was glad.

8:57
So the Judeans said to him,
You are not yet fifty years old
and you have seen Abraham?

8:58
Jesus said to them,
Truly truly I say to you,
before Abraham came into being, I am.

8:59
So they picked up stones to throw at him.
But Jesus hid himself
and went out from the temple.
