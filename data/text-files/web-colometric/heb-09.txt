9:1
Now the first covenant indeed had regulations of worship and the earthly sanctuary.

9:2
For a tent was constructed, the first one,
in which were the lampstand and the table and the presentation of the loaves,
which is called the Holy Place;

9:3
and behind the second curtain a tent called the Holy of Holies,

9:4
having a golden altar of incense and the ark of the covenant
covered on all sides with gold
in which was a golden jar
holding the manna and the rod of Aaron that budded and the tablets of the covenant,

9:5
and above it cherubim of glory overshadowing the mercy seat;
concerning which things it is not now possible to speak in detail.

9:6
Now these things having been thus prepared,
into the first tent the priests go continually
performing the acts of worship,

9:7
but into the second only the high priest goes once a year, not without blood,
which he offers for himself and for the sins of ignorance of the people,

9:8
the Holy Spirit indicating this, that the way into the holy places has not yet been revealed while the first tent still has its standing,

9:9
which is a symbol for the present time, according to
which both gifts and sacrifices are offered
that are unable to perfect the worshiper in conscience,

9:10
dealing only with foods and drinks and various washings, regulations of the flesh
imposed until the time of setting things right.

9:11
that is, not of this creation,
But when Christ appeared as high priest of the good things that have come
through the greater and more perfect tent not made with hands,

9:12
not through the blood of goats and calves but through his own blood, he entered once for all into the holy places,
having obtained eternal redemption.

9:13
For if the blood of goats and bulls and the ashes of a heifer
sprinkling those who have been defiled
sanctify for the purification of the flesh,

9:14
how much more will the blood of Christ,
who through the eternal Spirit offered himself without blemish to God,
purify our conscience from dead works to serve the living God.

9:15
And for this reason he is the mediator of a new covenant,
so that since a death has taken place for redemption of the transgressions under the first covenant
those who have been called may receive the promise of the eternal inheritance.

9:16
For where there is a covenant,
the death of the one who made it must be established;

9:17
for a covenant is in force over dead bodies, since it is never in force
while the one who made it lives.

9:18
Therefore not even the first covenant was inaugurated without blood;

9:19
for when every commandment according to the law had been spoken by Moses to all the people,
he took the blood of calves with water and scarlet wool and hyssop
and sprinkled both the book itself and all the people,

9:20
saying:
This is the blood of the covenant
which God commanded for you;

9:21
and he likewise sprinkled both the tent and all the vessels of the ministry with the blood.

9:22
And according to the law nearly everything is cleansed with blood,
and apart from the shedding of blood there is no forgiveness.

9:23
Therefore it was necessary for the copies of the heavenly things to be cleansed with these,
but the heavenly things themselves with better sacrifices than these.

9:24
For Christ did not enter into holy places made with hands, copies of the true ones,
but into heaven itself,
now to appear in the presence of God on our behalf;

9:25
nor that he should offer himself many times,
as the high priest enters the holy places year by year with blood not his own,

9:26
since then he would have had to suffer many times since the foundation of the world;
but now once at the consummation of the ages
he has been revealed for the abolition of sin through his sacrifice.

9:27
And inasmuch as it is appointed for human beings
once to die,
and after this judgment,

9:28
so also Christ, having been offered once to bear the sins of many,
will appear a second time apart from sin to those who eagerly await him for salvation.
