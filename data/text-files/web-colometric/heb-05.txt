5:1
For every high priest taken from among human
beings is appointed on behalf of human beings in things
pertaining to God, to offer both gifts and sacrifices for sins,

5:2
able to deal gently
with the ignorant and wayward, since he himself also is beset with weakness,

5:3
and because of it he is
obligated, just as for the people,
so also for himself to offer sacrifices for sins.

5:4
And no one takes this honor for himself,
but only when called by God,
just as Aaron was.

5:5
So also Christ did not glorify himself to become a high priest,
but the one who said to him:
You are my Son,
today I have begotten you;

5:6
just as also in another place he says:
You are a priest forever according to the order of Melchizedek,

5:7
who in the days of his flesh, both prayers and supplications
to the one able to save him from death
with loud cries and tears having offered up
and having been heard because of his reverence,

5:8
although being a Son, he learned
from what he suffered obedience,

5:9
and having been made perfect he became for all who obey him
the source of eternal salvation,

5:10
having been designated by God as high priest according to the order of Melchizedek.

5:11
Concerning him we have much to say and it is hard to explain,
since you have become sluggish in hearing;

5:12
for indeed you ought to be teachers by this time,
yet again you need someone to teach you the basic elements of the oracles of God, and you
have become in need of milk, not solid food.

5:13
For everyone who lives on milk
is inexperienced in the word of righteousness, for he is an infant;

5:14
but solid food is for the
mature, who through practice have their faculties trained
to distinguish both good and evil.
