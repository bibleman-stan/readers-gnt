5:1
For every high priest taken from among men
is appointed on behalf of men in things pertaining to God,
that he may offer both gifts and sacrifices for sins,

5:2
being able to deal gently with the ignorant and wayward,
since he himself is also clothed with weakness,

5:3
and because of it he is obligated,
just as for the people,
so also for himself to offer sacrifices for sins.

5:4
And no one takes the honor upon himself,
but is called by God,
just as Aaron was.

5:5
So also Christ did not glorify himself to become a high priest,
but the one who said to him:
You are my Son,
today I have begotten you;

5:6
just as he also says in another place:
You are a priest forever according to the order of Melchizedek,

5:7
who in the days of his flesh
offered up both prayers and supplications to the one able to save him from death
with loud crying and tears
and was heard because of his reverence,

5:8
although being a Son,
he learned obedience from the things he suffered,

5:9
and having been made perfect he became to all who obey him
the source of eternal salvation,

5:10
being designated by God as high priest according to the order of Melchizedek.

5:11
Concerning him we have much to say and it is hard to explain,
since you have become dull of hearing;

5:12
for though by this time you ought to be teachers,
you need someone to teach you again the elementary principles of the oracles of God,
and you have come to need milk, not solid food.

5:13
For everyone who partakes only of milk is unacquainted with the word of righteousness,
for he is an infant;

5:14
but solid food is for the mature,
for those who because of practice have their senses trained
to discern both good and evil.
