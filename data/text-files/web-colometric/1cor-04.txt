4:1
Let a person so consider us as servants of Christ and stewards of the mysteries of God.

4:2
In this case, moreover, it is required of stewards that one be found faithful.

4:3
But to me it is a very small thing
that I should be examined by you or by any human court;
indeed, I do not even examine myself;

4:4
for I am aware of nothing against myself,
yet I am not justified by this,
but the one who examines me is the Lord.

4:5
Therefore do not judge anything before the time,
until the Lord comes,
who will both bring to light the hidden things of darkness
and will reveal the purposes of the hearts,
and then each one will have praise from God.

4:6
Now these things, brothers, I have applied to myself and Apollos for your sake,
so that in us you might learn the principle: Nothing beyond what is written,
so that none of you may be puffed up for the one against the other.

4:7
For who regards you as superior?
And what do you have that you did not receive?
And if you did receive it, why do you boast as though you had not received it?

4:8
Already you are satisfied,
already you have become rich,
without us you have reigned;
and I wish indeed
you had reigned,
so that we also might reign with you.

4:9
For I think God has exhibited us apostles last of all,
as men sentenced to death, because we have become a spectacle to the world and to angels and to men.

4:10
We are fools for Christ's sake,
but you are prudent in Christ; we are
weak, but you are
strong; you are
honored, but we are dishonored.

4:11
To the present hour we are both hungry
and thirsty and poorly clothed and beaten and homeless

4:12
and we labor, working with our own hands;
when reviled we bless, when persecuted we endure,

4:13
when slandered we encourage;
we have become as the scum of the world, the refuse of all things until now.

4:14
I do not write these things
to shame you,
but to admonish you as my beloved children;

4:15
for if you were to have ten thousand tutors in Christ,
yet you would not have many fathers,
for in Christ Jesus through the gospel I fathered you.

4:16
I urge you therefore, become imitators of me.

4:17
For this reason I have sent Timothy to you,
who is my beloved and faithful child in the Lord,
who will remind you of my ways which are in Christ Jesus,
just as I teach everywhere in every church.

4:18
Now some have become puffed up as though I were not coming to you;

4:19
but I will come to you soon, if the Lord wills,
and I will find out not the speech of those who are puffed up
but the power,

4:20
for the kingdom of God is not in speech but in power.

4:21
What do you want?
Shall I come to you with a rod,
or in love and a spirit of gentleness?
