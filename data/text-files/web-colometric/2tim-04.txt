4:1
And by the appearing his and the kingdom his;
[...]
And and the dead,

4:2
Preach the word,
Be ready in season and out of season convict,
Rebuke and exhort,
With complete patience and instruction.

4:3
There will be for a time when sound
Teaching not they will endure
But according to the own desires to themselves they will gather around them teachers
Having an itching ear,

4:4
And from indeed the truth hearing they will turn away,
Unto then myths they will be turned aside.

4:5
But you be sober in all things endure afflictions,
The work do of an evangelist,
The ministry of you fully carry out.

4:6
For i already am being poured out,
And the time of my departure is come.

4:7
The good fight I have fought,
The race I have finished,
The faith I have kept;

4:8
From now on is laid up for me the of righteousness crown,
Which will award to me the Lord in that day the righteous judge,
Not only however to me but also to all those loving the appearing his.

4:9
Be diligent to come to me quickly;

4:10
For demas me has deserted
Having loved the present age,
And he has gone to Thessalonica,
Crescens to Galatia and Titus to Dalmatia;

4:11
Luke is alone with me Mark having taken
Bring him with you,
He is for to me useful for the ministry,

4:12
But tychicus I have sent to Ephesus.

4:13
The cloak that I left in Troas with Carpus upon coming bring,
And the books especially the parchments.

4:14
Alexander the coppersmith great to me harm
Will render to him the Lord according to the deeds

4:15
Whom also you beware of,
Exceedingly for he has opposed our message.

4:16
In first my defense no one me has stood with,
But all me
Not to them

4:17
And the Lord me stood by
And strengthened me,
So that through me the proclamation would be fully accomplished
And should hear all the Gentiles,
And I was delivered out of the mouth of the lion.

4:18
Will deliver me the Lord from every deed evil
And will bring me safely into the kingdom his heavenly;
To whom is the glory unto the ages of the ages Amen.

4:19
Greet Prisca and Aquila and the of Onesiphorus house.

4:20
Erastus remained in Corinth,
But trophimus I left in Miletus ailing.

4:21
Be earnest before winter to come.
Greets you Eubulus and Pudens and Linus and Claudia and the brothers all.

4:22
The Lord be with the spirit of you.
Grace be with you all.
