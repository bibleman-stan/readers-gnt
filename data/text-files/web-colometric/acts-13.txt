13:1
Now there were in Antioch in the local
church prophets and teachers:
both Barnabas and Simeon who was called Niger,
and Lucius of Cyrene, and Manaen a close friend of Herod the tetrarch, and Saul.

13:2
And while they were serving the Lord
and fasting the Holy Spirit said:
Set apart for me Barnabas and Saul for the work
to which I have called them.

13:3
Then after fasting and praying and laying their hands on them they sent them off.

13:4
So being sent out by the Holy Spirit they went down to Seleucia,
and from there they sailed to Cyprus,

13:5
and arriving in Salamis they proclaimed the word of God
in the synagogues of the Jews,
and they also had John as an assistant.

13:6
And passing through the whole island
as far as Paphos they found a certain magician a Jewish false prophet
whose name was Bar-Jesus,

13:7
who was with the proconsul Sergius Paulus, an intelligent man.
This man summoned Barnabas and Saul
and sought to hear the word of God.

13:8
But Elymas the magician opposed them,
for so his name is translated,
seeking to turn the proconsul away from the faith.

13:9
But Saul, who is also Paul, filled with the Holy Spirit
looked intently at him

13:10
and said:
Will you not stop making crooked the straight paths of the Lord?
O you who are full of all deceit and all fraud,
you son of the devil, you enemy of all righteousness,

13:11
and now behold the hand of the Lord is upon you, and you will be blind
not seeing the sun for a time.
And immediately mist and darkness fell upon him
and darkness,
and going about he was seeking someone to lead him by the hand.

13:12
Then the proconsul seeing what had happened
believed, being astonished at the teaching of the Lord.

13:13
Now setting sail from Paphos
Paul and his companions came to Perga in Pamphylia, and John
departing from them returned to Jerusalem.

13:14
But passing through from Perga they arrived at Pisidian Antioch,
and entering the synagogue on the Sabbath day they sat down.

13:15
And after the reading of the Law
and the Prophets the synagogue rulers sent to them
saying:
Brothers, if you have any word of encouragement for the people, speak.

13:16
And Paul stood up and motioning with his hand said:
Men of Israel
and you who fear God, listen.

13:17
The God of this people Israel chose our fathers,
and exalted the people during their stay in the land of Egypt,
and with an uplifted arm he led them out of it,

13:18
and for about forty years he put up with them in the wilderness,

13:19
and having destroyed seven nations in the land of Canaan
he gave them their land as an inheritance

13:20
for about four hundred and fifty years.
And after these things he gave them judges until Samuel the prophet.

13:21
And then they asked for a king,
and God gave them Saul the son of Kish, a man
from the tribe of Benjamin, for forty years.

13:22
And removing him
he raised up David as their king,
to whom he also testified saying:
I have found David the son of Jesse, a man after my own heart,
who will do all my will.

13:23
From this man's offspring God according to promise brought to Israel a Savior, Jesus,

13:24
John having proclaimed before his coming a baptism of repentance
to all the people of Israel.

13:25
And as John was finishing his course
he said:
What do you suppose me to be?
I am not he;
but behold one is coming after me
whose sandals I am not worthy
to untie from his feet.

13:26
Brothers, sons of Abraham's family and those among you who fear God,
to us the message of this salvation has been sent.

13:27
For those dwelling in Jerusalem and their rulers
not recognizing him
and the voices of the prophets that are read every Sabbath
by condemning him fulfilled them,

13:28
and finding no charge worthy of death
they asked Pilate to have him executed.

13:29
And when they had carried out everything written about him,
they took him down from the tree and placed him in a tomb.

13:30
But God raised him from the dead,

13:31
and for many days he appeared to those who came up with him from Galilee to Jerusalem,
who are now his witnesses to the people.

13:32
And we bring you the good news of the promise made to the fathers

13:33
that God has fulfilled this for us their children
by raising Jesus,
as also it is written in the second psalm:
You are my Son,
today I have begotten you.

13:34
And that he raised him from the dead
no longer to return to decay,
he has spoken thus: I will give you the holy and sure blessings of David.

13:35
Therefore he also says in another psalm:
You will not let your Holy One see decay.

13:36
For David after serving God's purpose in his own generation fell asleep
and was gathered to his fathers
and saw decay,

13:37
but the one whom God raised
did not see decay.

13:38
Let it be known to you therefore,
brothers, that through this one forgiveness of sins is proclaimed to you,
and from everything from which you could not be justified by the law of Moses

13:39
in this one everyone who believes is justified.

13:40
Watch out
therefore that what is said in the prophets may not come upon you:

13:41
Look, you scoffers,
and marvel
and perish,
for I am working a work in your days,
a work that you will never believe,
even if someone tells you.

13:42
And as they were going out
they urged that these words be spoken to them the next Sabbath.

13:43
And when the synagogue was dismissed
many of the Jews and the devout proselytes followed Paul and Barnabas,
who speaking to them urged
them to continue in the grace of God.

13:44
And the next Sabbath almost the whole city gathered to hear the word of the Lord.

13:45
But the Jews seeing the crowds were filled with jealousy
and began contradicting what was spoken by Paul, blaspheming.

13:46
And Paul and Barnabas speaking boldly said:
It was necessary
that the word of God be spoken to you first;
since you thrust it aside
and judge yourselves unworthy of eternal life,
behold we are turning to the Gentiles.

13:47
For so the Lord has commanded us:
I have set you as a light for the Gentiles that you may bring salvation to the end of the earth.

13:48
And hearing this the Gentiles began rejoicing
and glorifying the word of the Lord,
and as many as were appointed to eternal life believed.

13:49
And the word of the Lord was spreading throughout the whole region.

13:50
But the Jews stirred up the devout prominent women
and the leading men of the city and incited a persecution against Paul and Barnabas,
and drove them out of their district.

13:51
And shaking off the dust from their feet against them
they went to Iconium,

13:52
and the disciples were filled with joy and with the Holy Spirit.
