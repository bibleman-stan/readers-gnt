1:1
Paul, a servant of Christ Jesus, called as an apostle, set apart for the gospel of God

1:2
which he promised beforehand through his prophets in holy scriptures

1:3
concerning his Son, who came from the seed of David according to the flesh,

1:4
who was appointed Son of God in power according to the spirit of holiness by the resurrection of the dead,
Jesus Christ our Lord,

1:5
through whom we received grace and apostleship for the obedience of faith
among all the nations for the sake of his name,

1:6
among whom you also are called to belong to Jesus Christ,

1:7
to all who are in Rome, beloved of God, called as saints:
grace to you and peace
from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.

1:8
First, I give thanks to my God through Jesus Christ for all of you,
because your faith is proclaimed in the whole world.

1:9
For God is my witness,
whom I serve in my spirit in the gospel of his Son,
how unceasingly I make mention of you

1:10
always in my prayers, asking whether somehow at last I may succeed
by the will of God in coming to you.

1:11
For I long to see you,
so that I may share with you some spiritual gift to strengthen you,

1:12
that is, to be mutually encouraged among you
through the faith that is in one another, both yours and mine.

1:13
I do not want you to be
brothers,
unaware that many times I planned to come
to you, and I was prevented until
now, so that I might have some fruit among you
also, just as among the rest of the nations.

1:14
To Greeks and to barbarians, to the wise and to the foolish, I am a debtor;

1:15
so I am eager to proclaim the gospel also to you who are in Rome.

1:16
For I am not ashamed of the gospel,
for it is the power of God for salvation to everyone who believes,
to the Jew first and also to the Greek;

1:17
for the righteousness of God is revealed in it from faith to faith, as it is written:
The righteous one shall live by faith.

1:18
For the wrath of God is revealed from heaven against all ungodliness and unrighteousness
of those who suppress the truth in unrighteousness,

1:19
because what is known about God is evident among them, for God made
it evident to them.

1:20
For his invisible attributes since the creation of the world are perceived through the things made,
both his eternal power and divine nature, so that they are without excuse,

1:21
because although they knew God
they did not glorify him as God or give thanks,
but they became futile in their reasonings
and their senseless heart was darkened;

1:22
claiming to be wise they became fools,

1:23
and of four-footed creatures and reptiles.
and they exchanged the glory of the incorruptible God
for the likeness of an image of corruptible man and birds

1:24
Therefore God handed them over in the desires of their hearts
to impurity, to the dishonoring of their bodies among themselves,

1:25
who exchanged the truth of God for the lie,
and worshiped and served the creature rather than the Creator,
who is blessed forever; amen.

1:26
For this reason God handed them over to dishonorable passions;
for their females exchanged the natural function
for what is contrary to nature,

1:27
and likewise the males, abandoning the natural function of the female,
were inflamed in their desire for one another,
males with males committing shamelessness
and receiving in themselves the due penalty for their error.

1:28
And just as they did not see fit to hold God in knowledge,
God handed them over to an unfit mind, to do what is not proper,

1:29
filled with all unrighteousness, wickedness, greed, evil,
full of envy, murder, strife, deceit, malice, whisperers,

1:30
slanderers, God-haters, insolent, arrogant, boastful, inventors
of evil, disobedient to parents,

1:31
senseless, faithless, heartless, merciless;

1:32
who, knowing the righteous decree of God
that those who practice such things
are worthy of death,
not only do them
but also approve of those who practice them.
