21:1
And looking up he saw the rich putting their gifts into the treasury.

21:2
And he saw a certain poor widow putting in two small coins,

21:3
and he said,
Truly I tell you that this poor widow has put in more than all;

21:4
for all these out of their abundance put into the gifts,
but she out of her poverty put in all the livelihood
that she had.

21:5
And while some were speaking about the temple, that it was adorned with beautiful stones and offerings, he said,

21:6
As for these things which you see, days will come
in which not one stone will be left upon another
that will not be thrown down.

21:7
And they asked him, saying,
Teacher, when
then will these things be,
and what will be the sign
when these things are about to happen?

21:8
And he said,
Watch out
that you are not led astray;
for many will come in my name, saying,
I am he,
and, The time has drawn near;
do not go
after them.

21:9
And when you hear of wars and upheavals,
do not be terrified;
for these things must happen first,
but the end is not immediately.

21:10
Then he was saying to them, Nation will rise against nation and kingdom against kingdom,

21:11
and there will be great earthquakes and famines and plagues in various places,
and there will be terrors and great signs from heaven.

21:12
But before all these things they will lay their hands on you
and persecute you, handing you over to synagogues and prisons,
being brought before kings and governors on account of my name.

21:13
It will turn out for you as a testimony.

21:14
Settle it therefore in your hearts not to rehearse beforehand your defense,

21:15
for I will give you a mouth and wisdom
which none of your adversaries will be able to resist
or contradict,
all who oppose you.

21:16
And you will be handed over even by parents and brothers and relatives and friends,
and some of you they will put to death,

21:17
and you will be hated by all on account of my name.

21:18
Yet not a hair of your head will perish.

21:19
By your endurance you will gain your lives.

21:20
But when you see Jerusalem surrounded by armies, then know that its desolation has drawn near.

21:21
Then let those in Judea flee to the mountains,
and let those in the midst of her go out,
and let not those in the countryside enter into her,

21:22
for these are days of vengeance, to fulfill all that has been written.

21:23
Woe
to those who are with child and to those who are nursing in those days;
for there will be great distress upon the land
and wrath against this people,

21:24
and they will fall by the edge of the sword
and will be led captive into all the nations,
and Jerusalem will be trampled by the nations,
until the times of the nations are fulfilled.

21:25
And there will be signs in sun and moon and stars,
and on the earth distress of nations in perplexity at the roaring of the sea and waves,

21:26
people fainting from fear and expectation of the things coming upon the inhabited world,
for the powers of the heavens will be shaken.

21:27
And then they will see the Son of Man coming
in a cloud with power and great glory.

21:28
And when these things begin to happen,
stand up
and lift up your heads,
because your redemption is drawing near.

21:29
And he told them a parable:
Look at the fig tree and all the trees;

21:30
when they have already put forth leaves,
seeing this you know for yourselves that the summer is already near;

21:31
so also you,
when you see these things happening, know that the kingdom of God is near.

21:32
Truly I tell you
that this generation will not pass away until all things have happened.

21:33
Heaven and earth will pass away,
but my words will never pass away.

21:34
But watch yourselves,
lest your hearts be weighed down with dissipation and drunkenness and cares of this life,
and that day come upon you suddenly

21:35
like a trap;
for it will come upon all who dwell on the face of the whole earth.

21:36
But stay awake at all times,
praying that you may have strength to escape all these things
that are about to happen, and to stand before the Son of Man.

21:37
And during the days he was teaching in the temple,
but during the nights he would go out and lodge on the mount called Olivet.

21:38
And all the people would come early in the morning to him
in the temple to hear him.
