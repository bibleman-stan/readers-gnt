2:1
But false prophets also arose among the people, just as among you also there will be false teachers,
who will secretly bring in destructive heresies,
even denying the Master
who bought them,
bringing upon themselves swift destruction;

2:2
and many will follow their sensuality,
on account of whom the way of truth will be blasphemed;

2:3
and in greed they will exploit you with fabricated words;
for whom the judgment from long ago is not idle,
and their destruction is not asleep.

2:4
For if God did not spare angels who sinned,
but cast them into chains of darkness in Tartarus and handed them over
to be kept for judgment,

2:5
and did not spare the ancient world,
but preserved Noah, a herald of righteousness, as the eighth person,
when he brought a flood upon the world of the ungodly,

2:6
and reducing the cities of Sodom and Gomorrah to ashes
he condemned them to destruction,
having made them an example of what is coming to the ungodly,

2:7
and he rescued righteous Lot, who was oppressed by the
sensual conduct of the lawless--

2:8
for that righteous man, living among them day after day
by what he saw and heard was tormenting his righteous soul with their lawless deeds--

2:9
the Lord knows how to rescue the godly from trial, and to keep the unrighteous
under punishment for the day of judgment,

2:10
especially those who go after the flesh in the desire of defilement
and despise authority.
Bold, self-willed, they do not tremble when they blaspheme the glorious ones,

2:11
whereas angels, though greater in strength and power, do not bring a blasphemous judgment against them.

2:12
But these, like irrational animals born by nature
for capture and destruction, blaspheming in matters they do not understand,
in their destruction they will also be destroyed,

2:13
suffering wrong as the wages of wrongdoing;
counting pleasure the daytime indulgence,
stains and blemishes, reveling in their deceptions while feasting with you,

2:14
having eyes full of adultery and unceasing in sin,
enticing unstable souls,
having a heart trained in greed, accursed children,

2:15
having forsaken the straight way
they went astray,
having followed the way of Balaam of Bosor
who loved the wages of unrighteousness

2:16
but received a rebuke for his own lawlessness;
a speechless donkey speaking in a human voice restrained the madness of the prophet.

2:17
These are waterless springs and mists
driven by a storm,
for whom the gloom of darkness has been reserved.

2:18
For speaking pompous words of emptiness
they entice by desires of the flesh, by sensuality, those who are barely escaping
from those who live in error,

2:19
promising them freedom
while they themselves are slaves of corruption;
for by whatever a person is overcome,
to this he is also enslaved.

2:20
For if after escaping the defilements of the world
through the knowledge of the Lord and Savior Jesus Christ
they are again entangled in them and overcome,
the last state has become worse for them than the first.

2:21
For it would have been better for them
not to have known the way of righteousness
than having known it to turn back from the holy commandment delivered to them;

2:22
it has happened to them according to the true proverb:
A dog returns to its own vomit, and: A sow, having washed, to wallowing in the mud.
