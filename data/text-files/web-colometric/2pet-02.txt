2:1
But false prophets also arose among the people, just as there will also be false teachers among you,
who will secretly introduce destructive heresies,
even denying the Master
who bought them,
bringing swift destruction upon themselves;

2:2
and many will follow their sensuality,
and because of them the way of truth will be maligned;

2:3
and in their greed they will exploit you with fabricated words;
their judgment from long ago is not idle,
and their destruction is not asleep.

2:4
For if God did not spare angels when they sinned,
but cast them into chains of darkness, consigning them to Tartarus,
to be kept for judgment,

2:5
and did not spare the ancient world,
but preserved Noah, a herald of righteousness, with seven others,
when he brought a flood upon the world of the ungodly,

2:6
and reducing the cities of Sodom and Gomorrah to ashes
he condemned them with destruction,
making them an example of what is coming for the ungodly,

2:7
and rescued righteous Lot, oppressed by the sensual conduct
of the lawless —

2:8
for by what he saw and heard the righteous man living among them
day after day was tormented in his righteous soul by their lawless deeds —

2:9
the Lord knows how to rescue the godly from trial, and to keep the unrighteous
under punishment for the day of judgment,

2:10
especially those who walk after the flesh in the lust of defilement
and despise authority.
Bold, self-willed, they do not tremble when they blaspheme glorious ones,

2:11
whereas angels, though greater in might and power, do not bring a blasphemous judgment against them.

2:12
But these, like irrational animals born as creatures of instinct
for capture and destruction, blaspheming where they are ignorant,
will also be destroyed in their destruction,

2:13
suffering wrong as the wages of wrongdoing;
they count it pleasure to revel in the daytime,
stains and blemishes, reveling in their deceptions as they feast with you,

2:14
having eyes full of adultery and never ceasing from sin,
enticing unstable souls,
having a heart trained in greed, accursed children,

2:15
forsaking the right way
they have gone astray,
having followed the way of Balaam the son of Bosor,
who loved the wages of unrighteousness

2:16
but received a rebuke for his own transgression;
a speechless donkey speaking with a human voice restrained the madness of the prophet.

2:17
These are waterless springs and mists
driven by a storm,
for whom the gloom of darkness has been reserved.

2:18
For uttering great swelling words of emptiness
they entice by sensual desires of the flesh those who are barely escaping
from those who live in error,

2:19
promising them freedom,
while they themselves are slaves of corruption;
for by whatever a person is overcome,
by this he is enslaved.

2:20
For if after escaping the defilements of the world
through the knowledge of the Lord and Savior Jesus Christ
they are again entangled in them and overcome,
the last state has become worse for them than the first.

2:21
For it would have been better for them
not to have known the way of righteousness
than having known it to turn away from the holy commandment delivered to them;

2:22
what the true proverb says has happened to them:
A dog returns to its own vomit, and: A sow, after washing, returns to wallowing in the mire.
