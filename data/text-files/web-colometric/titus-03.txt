3:1
Remind them to rulers to authorities to be subject to be obedient,
For every work good ready to be,

3:2
No one to speak evil of peaceable to be,
Gentle all showing humility toward all men.

3:3
For were once also we ourselves foolish disobedient,
Being deceived serving lusts and pleasures various,
In malice and envy living hateful hating one another.

3:4
But when the kindness and the love of mankind appeared our of Savior God,

3:5
Not by works in righteousness
That did we
But according to His mercy He saved us through the washing of regeneration
And renewing of the Spirit Holy,

3:6
Whom He poured out on us richly through Jesus our Christ Savior,

3:7
So that having been justified by that grace heirs we should become according to the hope of life eternal.

3:8
Trustworthy is the saying,
And concerning these things I want you to affirm strongly so that may take care
Good works to be devoted to
Those believing God.
These things are excellent
And profitable to men;

3:9
But foolish controversies and genealogies and contentions and quarrels about the Law avoid,
They are for unprofitable and worthless.

3:10
A factious man after one and a second admonition reject,

3:11
Knowing that is corrupt such a man and is sinning being self-condemned.

3:12
When I shall send Artemas to you or Tychicus,
Be diligent to come to me into Nicopolis,
There for I have decided to winter.

3:13
The lawyer and Apollos earnestly equip,
So that nothing to them should be lacking.

3:14
Let learn now our also people good works to devote themselves to
For necessary needs,
So that not they should be unfruitful.

3:15
Greet you those with me all.
Greet those loving us in the faith.
Grace be with all of you.
