3:1
Therefore no longer enduring we thought it best to be left behind in Athens alone,

3:2
And we sent our Timothy brother and fellow worker of God
In the gospel of Christ in order to strengthen you
And to encourage concerning the faith of you

3:3
That no one be moved in the tribulations these.
For yourselves you know that for this we are destined;

3:4
And indeed when with you we were,
We were telling beforehand you that we are about to suffer affliction,
Just as also it came to pass and you know.

3:5
Because of this I also no longer enduring sent in order to know the faith of you lest somehow had tempted you the one tempting
And in vain would our be labor.

3:6
But presently having come Timothy to us from you
And having brought good news to us of the faith and the love of you,
And that you have a our remembrance good always
Longing us to see just as also we you,

3:7
Because of this we were encouraged brothers as to you in all the distress and our tribulation
Your through faith,

3:8
For now we live if you are standing firm in the Lord.

3:9
For what thanksgiving are we able to God to give concerning you in return for all the joy
That we rejoice because of you our before God,

3:10
Night and day exceedingly imploring for us to your see face
And to supply the things lacking in the faith of you?

3:11
Himself now God and our Father
Our and Lord Jesus may our direct way to you;

3:12
You now the Lord may make to increase and to abound in love toward one another
And toward all just as
Also we toward you,

3:13
In the coming our of Lord Jesus with all the saints his.
