4:1
Masters,
That which is righteous and that which is equal to the slaves give,
Knowing that also you have a Master in heaven.

4:2
In prayer continue steadfastly watching in it with thanksgiving,

4:3
Praying at the same time also for us that God may open to us a door for the word,
To declare the mystery of Christ,
On account of which also I have been bound,

4:4
So that I should make clear it as it behooves me to speak.

4:5
In wisdom walk toward those outside,
The time redeeming.

4:6
Let the speech of you be always in grace,
With salt having been seasoned to know how it behooves you one each to answer.

4:7
The things concerning me all will make known to you Tychicus the beloved brother
And faithful servant and fellow bond-servant in the Lord,

4:8
Whom I sent to you for this very purpose,
That you might know the things concerning us
And he may encourage the hearts of you,

4:9
With Onesimus the faithful and beloved brother,
Who is one of you;
All things to you they will make known here.

4:10
Greets you Aristarchus the fellow prisoner my and Mark the cousin of Barnabas
Concerning whom you have received instructions,
If he comes to you
Welcome him,

4:11
And also Jesus called Justus
Being among the circumcision,
These only fellow workers for the kingdom of God,
Who have been to me a comfort.

4:12
Greets you Epaphras who is one of you a servant of Christ,
Always struggling for you in the prayers so that you may stand mature
And fully assured in all the will of God.

4:13
I bear witness for to him that he has great concern for you and those in Laodicea
And them in Hiera.

4:14
Greets you Luke the physician beloved and also Demas.

4:15
Greet the in Laodicea brothers and also Nympha and the in house of her church.

4:16
And when shall be read among you the letter cause
That also in the of the Laodiceans church it may be read,
And the one from Laodicea
That also you may read.

4:17
And say to Archippus;
Take heed to the ministry
That you have received in the Lord,
That it you may fulfill.

4:18
The greeting is in the own hand Paul.
Remember my chains.
Grace be with you.
