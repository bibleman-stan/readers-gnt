4:1
And again he began to teach beside the sea.
And a vast crowd gathers to him,
so that he got into a boat and sat on the sea,
and the whole crowd was by the sea on the land.

4:2
And he was teaching them many things in parables,
and he said to them in his teaching:

4:3
Listen!
Look — the sower went out to sow.

4:4
And it happened as he sowed:
some fell along the path,
and the birds came
and devoured it.

4:5
And other fell on the rocky ground where it did not have much soil,
and at once it sprang up because it had no depth of soil.

4:6
And when the sun rose
it was scorched,
and because it had no root it withered.

4:7
And other fell among the thorns,
and the thorns grew up
and choked it,
and it gave no fruit.

4:8
And others fell into the good soil,
and were giving fruit, growing up and increasing,
and bearing thirtyfold
and sixtyfold
and a hundredfold.

4:9
And he said:
Whoever has ears to hear, let him hear.

4:10
And when he was alone,
those around him with the twelve asked him about the parables.

4:11
And he said to them:
To you the mystery of the kingdom of God has been given;
but to those outside, everything comes in parables,

4:12
so that seeing they may see and not perceive,
and hearing they may hear and not understand,
lest they turn back
and it be forgiven them.

4:13
And he says to them:
You do not understand this parable —
and how will you understand all the parables?

4:14
The sower sows the word.

4:15
Now these are the ones along the path where the word is sown:
and when they hear,
at once Satan comes
and takes away the word that was sown in them.

4:16
And these likewise are the ones sown on the rocky ground,
who when they hear the word
at once receive it with joy,

4:17
and they have no root in themselves
but are short-lived;
then when trouble or persecution comes because of the word,
at once they fall away.

4:18
And others are the ones sown among the thorns;
these are the ones who heard the word,

4:19
and the cares of the age
and the lure of wealth
and the desires for other things creeping in
choke the word,
and it becomes fruitless.

4:20
And those are the ones sown on the good soil,
who hear the word
and embrace it
and bear fruit:
thirtyfold
and sixtyfold
and a hundredfold.

4:21
And he said to them:
Does the lamp come
to be put under a basket or under a bed?
Does it not come to be put on the lampstand?

4:22
For nothing is hidden except to be revealed,
nor has anything been secret but to come into the open.

4:23
If anyone has ears to hear, let him hear.

4:24
And he said to them:
Watch what you hear.
With the measure you measure, it will be measured to you,
and more will be added to you.

4:25
For whoever has, it will be given to him;
and whoever does not have, even what he has will be taken from him.

4:26
And he said:
The kingdom of God is thus —
as if a man should cast the seed upon the ground,

4:27
and he sleeps
and rises, night and day,
and the seed sprouts and grows tall,
in a way he does not know.

4:28
By itself the earth bears fruit:
first a shoot,
then a head of grain,
then full wheat in the head.

4:29
But when the crop allows it,
at once he sends out the sickle,
because the harvest has come.

4:30
And he said:
How shall we compare the kingdom of God,
or in what parable shall we set it forth?

4:31
Like a grain of mustard,
which when it is sown on the ground,
is smaller than all the seeds on the earth —

4:32
and when it is sown,
it grows up
and becomes greater than all the garden plants,
and puts out large branches,
so that the birds of the sky can nest under its shade.

4:33
And with many such parables he was speaking the word to them,
as they were able to hear.

4:34
But without a parable he did not speak to them;
yet in private to his own disciples he was explaining everything.

4:35
And he says to them on that day, when evening had come:
Let us cross over to the other side.

4:36
And leaving the crowd,
they take him along, just as he was, in the boat,
and other boats were with him.

4:37
And a great windstorm arises,
and the waves were crashing into the boat,
so that the boat was already filling up.

4:38
And he was in the stern,
sleeping on the cushion.
And they wake him
and say to him:
Teacher, do you not care that we are perishing?

4:39
And having risen he rebuked the wind
and said to the sea:
Silence!
Be muzzled!
And the wind ceased,
and there came a great calm.

4:40
And he said to them:
Why are you afraid?
Do you still have no faith?

4:41
And they feared with a great fear,
and were saying to one another:
Who then is this,
that even the wind and the sea obey him?
