1:1
and Savior Jesus Christ;
Simeon Peter, a servant and apostle
of Jesus Christ, to those who have obtained a faith of equal standing with ours in the righteousness of our God

1:2
may grace and peace be multiplied to you in the knowledge of God
and of Jesus our Lord.

1:3
Seeing that his divine power has granted to us
all things pertaining to life and godliness through
the knowledge of the one who called us by his own glory and excellence,

1:4
through which he has granted to us precious and very great promises,
so that through these you may become sharers of the divine nature,
having escaped from the corruption in the world caused by desire.

1:5
And for this very reason applying all diligence,
supply in your faith excellence,
and in excellence knowledge,

1:6
and in knowledge self-control,
and in self-control endurance,
and in endurance godliness,

1:7
and in godliness brotherly affection,
and in brotherly affection love;

1:8
for if these things are yours and are increasing
they render you neither idle nor unfruitful in the knowledge of our Lord
Jesus Christ;

1:9
for the one who lacks these
things is blind, being
short-sighted, having forgotten the cleansing from his former sins.

1:10
Therefore be all
brothers,
the more diligent to make your calling and election sure; for
if you practice these things you will never stumble;

1:11
and Savior Jesus Christ. for in this way the
entrance into the eternal kingdom of our Lord will be richly supplied to you

1:12
Therefore I will always be ready to remind you about these things,
even though you know them and are established in the truth that is present with you.

1:13
And I consider it
right, as long as I am in this tent, to
stir you up by way of reminder,

1:14
knowing that the putting off of my tent is imminent,
just as also our Lord Jesus Christ made clear to me;

1:15
and I will also make every effort that after
my departure you may be able at any time to recall these things.

1:16
For we did not follow cleverly devised myths
when we made known to you the power and coming of our Lord Jesus Christ,
but having become eyewitnesses of his majesty.

1:17
For when he received honor and glory from God the Father
when a voice was borne to him of this kind by the majestic glory:
This is my beloved Son, in whom
I am well pleased--

1:18
and this voice we ourselves heard borne from heaven
being with him on the holy mountain.

1:19
And we have the prophetic word made more sure,
to which you do well to pay attention
as to a lamp shining in a dark place
until the day dawns
and the morning star rises in your hearts;

1:20
knowing this first, that
no prophecy of Scripture comes about by one's own interpretation,

1:21
for no prophecy was ever brought about by the will of a human being,
but carried along by the Holy Spirit people spoke from God.
