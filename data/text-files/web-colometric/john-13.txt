13:1
Now before the feast of the Passover, Jesus, knowing that his hour had come
to depart from this world to the Father,
having loved his own who were in the world,
he loved them to the end.

13:2
And during dinner,
the devil having already put it into the heart
of Judas son of Simon Iscariot to betray him,

13:3
knowing that the Father had given all things into his hands, and that he came forth from God
and was going to God,

13:4
he rises from the dinner
and lays aside his garments
and having taken a towel
he tied it around himself.

13:5
Then he pours water into the basin,
and began to wash the feet of the disciples and to wipe them with the towel
with which he was tied.

13:6
So he comes to Simon Peter.
He says to him,
Lord, are you washing my feet?

13:7
Jesus answered
and said to him,
What I am doing
you do not know now, but you will understand after these things.

13:8
Peter says to him,
You will never wash my feet, into the age.
Jesus answered him,
If
I do not wash you,
you have no part with me.

13:9
Simon Peter says to him,
Lord, not my feet only but also my hands and my head.

13:10
Jesus says to him,
The one who has bathed has no need except to wash his feet,
but is entirely clean.
And you are clean,
but not all of you.

13:11
For he knew the one who was betraying him.
Because of this he said, Not all of you are clean.

13:12
So when he had washed their feet
and taken his garments
and reclined again,
he said to them,
Do you know what I have done to you?

13:13
You call me Teacher and Lord,
and you speak well, for I am.

13:14
If then I, the Lord and Teacher, washed your feet,
you also ought to wash one another's feet.

13:15
For I have given you an example,
so that just as I did to you,
you also should do.

13:16
Truly truly I say to you,
a servant is not greater than his lord,
nor is a messenger greater than the one who sent him.

13:17
If you know these things,
blessed are you if you do them.

13:18
I am not speaking about all of you.
I know the ones I chose.
But so that the scripture may be fulfilled,
The one who eats my bread
has lifted his heel against me.

13:19
From now on I tell you before it happens,
so that when it happens you may believe that I am.

13:20
Truly truly I say to you,
the one who receives whomever I send
receives me, and the one who receives me receives
the one who sent me.

13:21
Having said these things,
Jesus was troubled in his spirit
and testified
and said,
Truly truly I say to you that one of you will betray me.

13:22
The disciples were looking at one another,
at a loss about whom he was speaking.

13:23
One of his disciples was reclining in the bosom of Jesus,
the one whom Jesus loved.

13:24
So Simon Peter nods to this one to ask who it might be about whom he speaks.

13:25
So that one, having leaned back thus on the chest of Jesus, says to him,
Lord, who is it?

13:26
Jesus answers,
That one it is,
to whom I will dip the morsel
and give it to him.
So having dipped the morsel he gives it to Judas son of Simon Iscariot.

13:27
And after the morsel, then Satan entered into that one.
So Jesus says
to him,
What you are doing,
do it more quickly.

13:28
Now no one of those reclining knew why he said this to him.

13:29
For some were thinking, since Judas had the money box,
that Jesus was saying to him,
Buy what we need for the feast,
or that he should give something to the poor.

13:30
So having received the morsel
that one went out immediately.
And it was night.

13:31
So when he had gone out,
Jesus says,
Now the Son of Man has been glorified,
and God has been glorified in him.

13:32
If God has been glorified in him,
God will also glorify him in himself,
and will glorify him immediately.

13:33
Little children, yet a little while I am with you.
You will seek me,
and just as I said to the Judeans, Where I am going
you cannot come, so I say to you now.

13:34
A new command I give you, that you love one another.
Just as I have loved you,
so you also should love one another.

13:35
By this everyone will know that you are my disciples,
if you have love among one another.

13:36
Simon Peter says to him,
Lord, where are you going?
Jesus answered,
Where I am going you cannot follow me now,
but you will follow later.

13:37
Peter says to him,
Lord, why can I not follow you now?
I will lay down my life for you.

13:38
Jesus answers,
Will you lay down your life for me?
Truly truly I say to you,
a rooster will certainly not crow
until you have denied me three times.
