7:1
And the Pharisees and some of the scribes gathered around him,
having come from Jerusalem.

7:2
And they saw that some of his disciples ate with defiled hands,
that is, unwashed,
eating their bread --

7:3
for the Pharisees and all the Jews
do not eat unless they wash their hands with a fist, holding
to the tradition of the elders,

7:4
and when they come from the marketplace they do not eat
unless they wash;
and there are many other traditions
that they observe, such as the washing of cups and pitchers and copper vessels and dining couches --

7:5
and the Pharisees and the scribes asked him:
Why do your disciples not walk according to the tradition of the elders,
but eat their bread with unclean hands?

7:6
And he said to them:
Well did Isaiah prophesy about you hypocrites, as it is written: This people honors me with their lips, but their heart is far from me;

7:7
but in vain do they worship me,
teaching as doctrines the commandments of men.

7:8
You have let go of the commandment of God
and hold to the tradition of men.

7:9
And he said to them:
You have a fine way of rejecting the commandment of God
in order to keep your tradition!

7:10
For Moses said:
Honor your father and your mother,
and: Whoever speaks evil of father or mother must surely die.

7:11
But you say:
If a man tells his father or mother: It is Corban (that is, a gift to God) --
whatever you might have been helped by me --

7:12
then you no longer permit him to do anything for his father or mother,

7:13
thus making void the word of God by your tradition
that you have handed down;
and you do many similar things.

7:14
And calling the crowd to him again
he said to them:
Listen to me, all of you, and understand.

7:15
There is nothing outside a person that by going in
can defile him;
but the things that come out of a person are what defile him.

7:17
And when he had entered the house, away from the crowd,
his disciples asked him about the parable.

7:18
And he said to them:
Are you also without understanding?
Do you not see that whatever goes in from outside
into a person cannot defile him,

7:19
because it does not enter his heart
but his stomach, and goes out into the drain?
(Thus he declared all foods clean.)

7:20
And he said: What comes out of a person,
that is what defiles a person.

7:21
For from within, out of the heart of man, come evil thoughts:
sexual immorality, theft, murder,

7:22
adultery, greed, wickedness, deceit, sensuality, envy, slander, pride, foolishness.

7:23
All these evil things come from within
and defile a person.

7:24
And rising up from there he went away to the region of Tyre.
And he entered
a house and wanted no one to know,
yet he could not escape notice.

7:25
But immediately a woman whose little daughter had an unclean spirit
heard about him,
and she came and fell at his feet.

7:26
Now the woman was a Greek,
a Syrophoenician by birth;
and she kept asking him to cast the demon out of her daughter.

7:27
And he said to her:
Let the children be satisfied first,
for it is not right to take the children's bread
and throw it to the dogs.

7:28
But she answered
and said to him:
Lord, even the dogs under the table eat the children's crumbs.

7:29
And he said to her:
Because of this word, go; the demon has left your daughter.

7:30
And going home
she found the child lying on the bed
and the demon gone.

7:31
through the region of the Decapolis.
And again departing from the region of Tyre he went through Sidon
to the Sea of Galilee

7:32
And they brought to him a man who was deaf and had a speech impediment,
and they begged him to lay his hand on him.

7:33
And taking him aside from the crowd privately,
he put his fingers into his ears
and after spitting he touched his tongue.

7:34
And looking up to heaven he sighed,
and he said to him:
Ephphatha!
which means: Be opened!

7:35
And his ears were opened,
and the bond of his tongue was released,
and he spoke clearly.

7:36
And he ordered them to tell no one; but the more
he ordered them,
the more widely they proclaimed it.

7:37
And they were astonished beyond measure, saying:
He has done all things well;
he even makes the deaf hear and the mute speak.
