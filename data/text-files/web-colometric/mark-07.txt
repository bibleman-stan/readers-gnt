7:1
And the Pharisees and some of the scribes gather to him,
having come from Jerusalem.

7:2
And seeing that some of his disciples eat bread with defiled hands,
that is, unwashed,
they found fault.

7:3
For the Pharisees and all the Jews
do not eat unless they wash their hands with a fist, holding to the tradition of the elders,

7:4
and when they come from the marketplace they do not eat
unless they wash themselves,
and there are many other things
they have received to observe: washings of cups and pitchers and bronze vessels and beds.

7:5
And the Pharisees and the scribes ask him:
Why do your disciples not walk according to the tradition of the elders,
but eat bread with defiled hands?

7:6
And he said to them:
Well did Isaiah prophesy about you hypocrites, as it is written: This people honors me with their lips, but their heart is far from me.

7:7
In vain do they worship me,
teaching as doctrines the commandments of men.

7:8
Having set aside the commandment of God,
you hold to the tradition of men.

7:9
And he said to them:
How well you set aside the commandment of God
in order to keep your tradition.

7:10
For Moses said:
Honor your father and your mother,
and: The one who speaks evil of father or mother must surely die.

7:11
But you say:
If a person says to father or mother: Corban — that is, a gift —
whatever you might have gained from me,

7:12
you no longer allow him to do anything for his father or mother,

7:13
making void the word of God by your tradition
which you have handed down.
And many such things like this you do.

7:14
And calling the crowd to him again,
he said to them:
Listen to me, all of you, and understand.

7:15
There is nothing outside a person that going into him
is able to defile him,
but the things coming out of a person are what defile a person.

7:17
And when he entered a house away from the crowd,
his disciples asked him about the parable.

7:18
And he says to them:
Are you also without understanding?
Do you not perceive that everything entering from outside
into a person is not able to defile him,

7:19
because it does not enter his heart
but his stomach, and goes out into the drain?
— thus declaring all foods clean.

7:20
And he said: What comes out of a person,
that defiles a person.

7:21
For from within, out of the hearts of people, the evil thoughts come out:
sexual immorality, theft, murder,

7:22
adultery, greed, wickedness, deceit, sensuality, envy, slander, pride, foolishness.

7:23
All these evil things come out from within
and defile a person.

7:24
And rising up from there he went away to the region of Tyre.
And entering
a house he wanted no one to know,
but he was not able to remain hidden.

7:25
But at once a woman who heard about him,
whose little daughter had an unclean spirit,
came and fell at his feet.

7:26
Now the woman was a Gentile,
a Syrophoenician by birth.
And she kept asking him to cast the demon out of her daughter.

7:27
And he said to her:
Let the children be fed first,
for it is not right to take the bread of the children
and throw it to the dogs.

7:28
But she answered
and says to him:
Lord, even the dogs under the table eat from the children's crumbs.

7:29
And he said to her:
Because of this word, go — the demon has gone out of your daughter.

7:30
And going away to her house
she found the child lying on the bed
and the demon gone out.

7:31
through the middle of the region of the Decapolis.
And again going out from the region of Tyre he came through Sidon
to the Sea of Galilee

7:32
And they bring to him a man deaf and with a speech impediment,
and they beg him to lay his hand on him.

7:33
And taking him aside from the crowd privately,
he put his fingers into his ears
and spitting he touched his tongue,

7:34
and looking up to heaven he groaned deeply,
and says to him:
Ephphatha,
that is: Be opened.

7:35
And his ears were opened,
and the bond of his tongue was released,
and he began speaking clearly.

7:36
And he ordered them to tell no one. But as much
as he ordered them,
they proclaimed it all the more.

7:37
And they were astonished beyond all measure, saying:
He has done all things well.
He makes the deaf hear and the mute speak.
