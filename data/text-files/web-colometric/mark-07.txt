7:1
And are gathered together to him the Pharisees and some of the scribes
having come from Jerusalem

7:2
And they had seen some of the disciples of him that with defiled hands
that is unwashed
are eating the bread

7:3
For the Pharisees and all the Jews
if not carefully they wash the hands not eat
holding the tradition of the elders

7:4
and on coming from the market if not wash
not they eat
and other things many there are
which they received for them to hold to washings of cups and vessels and utensils and dining couches

7:5
And questioned Him the Pharisees and the scribes
Because of why not walk the disciples of You according to the tradition of the elders
but with unwashed hands eat the bread

7:6
And He said to them
Rightly prophesied Isaiah concerning you hypocrites as it has been written This people with the lips Me honors but the heart of them far away is kept from Me

7:7
In vain now they worship Me
teaching as doctrines the precepts of men

7:8
Having neglected the commandment of God
you hold to the tradition of men

7:9
And He was saying to them
Neatly do you set aside the commandment of God
that the tradition of you you might keep

7:10
Moses for said
Honor the Father of you and the mother of you
and The one speaking evil of father or mother in death must end

7:11
You however say that
if says a man to the father or the mother It is Corban that is a gift
whatever if from me you might be profited

7:12
no longer do you allow him anything to do for the father or the mother

7:13
making void the word of God for the tradition of you
which you have handed down
And like things such many you do

7:14
And having called to him again the crowd
He was saying to them
Listen to me all and understand

7:15
Nothing there is from outside the man entering into him
which is able to defile him
but the things out of the man proceeding are the things defiling the man

7:17
And when He went into the house from the crowd
were asking Him the disciples of him the parable

7:18
And He says to them
Thus also you without understanding are
Not understand you that everything from outside entering
into the man not is able him to defile

7:19
because not it enters of him into the heart
but into the belly and into the sewer goes out
purifying all the food

7:20
He was saying now That out of the man going forth
that defiles the man

7:21
Within for from the heart of men thoughts evil go forth
sexual immorality thefts murders

7:22
adulteries covetous desires wickednesses deceit sensuality an eye evil slander pride foolishness

7:23
All these evils from within go forth
and they defile the man

7:24
From there also having risen up He went away into the region of Tyre
And having entered
into a house no one He was wishing to know it
and not He was able to be hidden

7:25
But immediately having heard a woman about him
of whom had the little daughter of her unclean spirit
having come fell at the feet of him

7:26
Now the woman was Gentile
Syrophoenician by race
and kept asking Him that the demon He should cast forth out of the daughter of her

7:27
And He was saying to her
Permit first to be satisfied the children
for it is not good to take the bread of the children
and to the dogs to cast it

7:28
But she answered
and says to him
Lord even the dogs under the table eat of the crumbs of the children

7:29
And He said to her
Because of this word go has gone forth out of the daughter of you the demon

7:30
And having gone away to the home of her
she found the child lying on the bed
and the demon having gone out

7:31
through the midst the region of the Decapolis
And again having departed from of the region of Tyre He came through Sidon
to the Sea of Galilee

7:32
And they bring to him a deaf man and who spoke with difficulty
and they implore Him that He might lay on him the hand

7:33
And having taken away him from the crowd aside privately
He put the fingers of him to the ears of him
and having spit He touched the tongue of him

7:34
and having looked up to heaven He sighed deeply
and He says to him
Ephphatha
that is Be opened

7:35
And were opened his ears
and was loosed the band of the tongue of him
and he began speaking plainly

7:36
And He instructed them that no one they should tell As much as
however He kept instructing
them more abundantly they were proclaiming it

7:37
And above measure they were astonished saying
Well all things He has done
both the deaf He makes to hear and mute to speak
