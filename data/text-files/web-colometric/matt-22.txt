22:1
And Jesus answered and again spoke to them in parables saying,

22:2
The kingdom of heaven is like a man, a king,
who gave a wedding feast for his son.

22:3
And he sent his servants to call those who had been invited to the feast,
and they were unwilling to come.

22:4
Again he sent other servants saying,
Tell those who have been invited,
Look — I have prepared my banquet,
my oxen and fattened cattle have been slaughtered, and everything is ready;
come to the feast.

22:5
But they paid no attention and went away,
one to his own field, and another to his business;

22:6
and the rest seized his servants
and mistreated and killed them.

22:7
And the king was angry,
and sending his armies
he destroyed those murderers
and burned their city.

22:8
Then he says to his servants,
The feast is ready,
but those invited were not worthy;

22:9
go therefore to the crossroads,
and whoever you find
invite to the feast.

22:10
And those servants went out into the roads and gathered all
whom they found, both evil and good;
and the wedding hall was filled with guests.

22:11
And the king came in to see the guests
and saw there a man not wearing a wedding garment;

22:12
and he says to him,
Friend, how did you get in here
without a wedding garment?
And he was speechless.

22:13
Then the king said to the servants,
Bind his feet and hands
and throw him into the outer darkness;
there will be
weeping and gnashing of teeth.

22:14
For many are called
but few are chosen.

22:15
Then the Pharisees went and took counsel
to trap him in a word.

22:16
And they send to him their disciples with the Herodians saying,
Teacher, we know that you are truthful
and teach the way of God in truth,
and you do not care about anyone,
for you do not look at the face of people;

22:17
tell us then what you think: is it lawful to pay tax to Caesar or not?

22:18
But Jesus, knowing their wickedness, said,
Why do you test me, hypocrites?

22:19
Show me the coin for the tax.
And they brought him a denarius.

22:20
And he says to them,
Whose image is this and the inscription?

22:21
They say to him, Caesar’s.
Then he says to them,
Give back
then the things of Caesar to Caesar
and the things of God to God.

22:22
And hearing this they marveled,
and leaving him they went away.

22:23
On that day Sadducees came to him,
saying there is no resurrection,
and they questioned him

22:24
saying,
Teacher, Moses said,
If someone dies
not having children,
his brother shall marry his wife
and raise up offspring for his brother.

22:25
Now there were among us seven brothers;
and the first married and died,
and having no offspring
left his wife to his brother;

22:26
likewise also the second and the third, through the seven;

22:27
and last of all the woman died.

22:28
In the resurrection then, whose wife of the seven will she be?
For they all had her.

22:29
And Jesus answered and said to them,
You are mistaken,
not knowing the scriptures
nor the power of God;

22:30
for in the resurrection they neither marry nor are given in marriage,
but are like angels of God in heaven;

22:31
but concerning the resurrection of the dead
have you not read what was spoken to you by God saying,

22:32
I am the God of Abraham and the God of Isaac and the God of Jacob?
He is not
the God of the dead but of the living.

22:33
And the crowds, hearing this, were astonished at his teaching.

22:34
And the Pharisees, hearing that he had silenced the Sadducees,
gathered together.

22:35
And one of them, a lawyer, tested him asking,

22:36
Teacher, which is the great commandment in the law?

22:37
And he said to him,
You shall love the Lord your God with all your heart
and with all your soul and with all your mind;

22:38
this is the great and first commandment.

22:39
And the second is like it:
You shall love your neighbor as yourself.

22:40
On these two commandments the whole law hangs
and the prophets.

22:41
And while the Pharisees were gathered
Jesus questioned them

22:42
saying,
What do you think about the Christ? Whose son is he?
They say to him, David’s.

22:43
He says to them,
How then does David in the Spirit call him Lord, saying,

22:44
The Lord said to my Lord,
Sit at my right hand
until I put your enemies under your feet?

22:45
If then David calls him Lord,
how is he his son?

22:46
And no one was able to answer him a word,
nor did anyone dare from that day on to question him any longer.
