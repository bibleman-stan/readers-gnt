1:1
Paul an apostle not from men nor through man
But through Jesus Christ and God the Father who raised Him out from the dead,

1:2
And the with me all brothers To the churches of Galatia;

1:3
Grace to you and peace from God our Father and Lord Jesus Christ,

1:4
Who given Himself our for sins,
So that He might deliver us out of the age of having come presently
Evil according to the will of God and our Father,

1:5
To whom be the glory to the ages of the ages Amen.

1:6
I am astonished that so quickly you are deserting from who called you in the grace of Christ
To a different gospel,

1:7
Which not is another if not some there are who are troubling you
And are desiring to pervert the gospel of Christ.

1:8
But even if we or an angel out of heaven preach gospel to you
Contrary to what we proclaimed to you accursed let him be.

1:9
As we have said before even now again I say,
If anyone to you is preaching a gospel
Contrary to what you received accursed let him be.

1:10
For presently of men do I seek approval or God?
Or do I seek men to please?
For if yet men I were pleasing of Christ a servant not I would be.

1:11
I make known for to you,
Brothers,
The gospel having been preached by me that not it is according to man;

1:12
For neither I from man received it,
Nor was I taught it,
But by a revelation of Jesus Christ.

1:13
You have heard of my for way of life former
In Judaism that beyond exceeding measure I was persecuting the church of God and was destroying it,

1:14
And I was advancing in Judaism beyond many contemporaries my in countrymen,
More abundantly zealous being my of fathers traditions.

1:15
But when was pleased having selected me from the womb of my mother
And having called me by the grace his

1:16
To reveal the Son his in me
That I might preach Him among the Gentiles,
Immediately not I consulted with flesh and blood,

1:17
Nor did I go up to Jerusalem to the before me apostles,
But I went away into Arabia,
And again returned to Damascus.

1:18
Then after years three I went up to Jerusalem to make acquaintance with Cephas,
And I remained with him days fifteen;

1:19
But other of the apostles none I saw if not James the brother of the Lord.

1:20
In what now I write to you,
Behold before God not I lie.

1:21
Then I went into the regions of Syria and Cilicia.

1:22
I was then unknown by face to the churches of Judea that are in Christ,

1:23
But only hearing they were that the one persecuting us formerly
Now is preaching the faith
Which once he was destroying,

1:24
And they were glorifying in me God.
