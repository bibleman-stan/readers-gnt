1:1
Paul, an apostle — not from men nor through a man,
but through Jesus Christ and God the Father who raised him from the dead —

1:2
and all the brothers who are with me, to the churches of Galatia:

1:3
Grace to you and peace from God our Father and our Lord Jesus Christ,

1:4
who gave himself for our sins
to rescue us from the present evil age,
according to the will of our God and Father,

1:5
to whom be the glory forever and ever. Amen.

1:6
I am astonished that you are so quickly deserting the one who called you by the grace of Christ
for a different gospel —

1:7
which is really no gospel at all; except that some are troubling you
and wanting to distort the gospel of Christ.

1:8
But even if we or an angel from heaven should preach to you a gospel
contrary to what we preached to you, let him be accursed.

1:9
As we have said before, and now I say again:
if anyone is preaching to you a gospel
contrary to what you received, let him be accursed.

1:10
For am I now seeking the approval of men, or of God?
Or am I trying to please men?
If I were still pleasing men, I would not be a servant of Christ.

1:11
For I would have you know,
brothers,
that the gospel preached by me is not of human origin;

1:12
for I did not receive it from any man,
nor was I taught it,
but it came through a revelation of Jesus Christ.

1:13
For you have heard of my former way of life
in Judaism — how beyond measure I persecuted the church of God and tried to destroy it,

1:14
and I was advancing in Judaism beyond many of my contemporaries among my people,
being far more zealous for the traditions of my fathers.

1:15
But when the one who set me apart from my mother's womb was pleased,
and called me through his grace,

1:16
to reveal his Son in me
so that I might preach him among the Gentiles,
I did not immediately consult with flesh and blood,

1:17
nor did I go up to Jerusalem to those who were apostles before me,
but I went away to Arabia,
and returned again to Damascus.

1:18
Then after three years I went up to Jerusalem to get to know Cephas,
and I stayed with him fifteen days.

1:19
But I saw none of the other apostles, except James the Lord's brother.

1:20
Now in what I am writing to you —
behold, before God, I am not lying.

1:21
Then I went into the regions of Syria and Cilicia.

1:22
And I was still unknown by face to the churches of Judea that are in Christ;

1:23
they only kept hearing that "The one who once persecuted us
is now preaching the faith
that he once tried to destroy,"

1:24
and they were glorifying God because of me.
