7:1
And after these things Jesus was walking about in Galilee,
for he did not want to walk about in Judea,
because the Judeans were seeking to kill him.

7:2
Now the feast of the Judeans, the Feast of Booths, was near.

7:3
So his brothers said to him,
Leave here
and go to Judea,
so that your disciples also may see the works you are doing.

7:4
For no one does anything in secret
and seeks to be known publicly;
if you do these things,
reveal yourself to the world.

7:5
For not even his brothers were believing in him.

7:6
So Jesus says to them,
My time has not yet arrived,
but your time is always ready.

7:7
The world cannot hate you,
but it hates me,
because I testify about it that its works are evil.

7:8
You go up to the feast;
I am not going up
to this feast,
because my time has not yet been fulfilled.

7:9
And having said these things,
he himself remained in Galilee.

7:10
But when his brothers had gone up to the feast,
then he also went up, not openly but as if in secret.

7:11
So the Judeans were looking for him at the feast
and were saying,
Where is that one?

7:12
And there was much grumbling about him among the crowds;
some were saying, He is good,
but others were saying,
No, rather he deceives the crowd.

7:13
Yet no one was speaking openly about him because of fear of the Judeans.

7:14
But already in the middle of the feast,
Jesus went up into the temple and was teaching.

7:15
So the Judeans were marveling, saying,
How does this one know the scriptures without having been taught?

7:16
So Jesus answered them
and said,
My teaching is not mine
but belongs to the one who sent me.

7:17
If anyone wills to do his will, he will know about the teaching whether it is from God
or whether I speak from myself.

7:18
The one who speaks from himself seeks his own glory;
but the one who seeks the glory of the one who sent him,
this one is true
and there is no unrighteousness in him.

7:19
Did not Moses give you the law?
And none of you keeps the law.
Why do you seek to kill me?

7:20
The crowd answered,
You have a demon!
Who is seeking to kill you?

7:21
Jesus answered
and said to them,
I did one work
and you all marvel.

7:22
Because of this Moses gave you circumcision—
not that it is from Moses
but from the fathers—and on the Sabbath you circumcise a man.

7:23
If a man receives circumcision on the Sabbath,
so that the law of Moses may not be broken,
are you angry with me because I made a whole man well on the Sabbath?

7:24
Do not judge by appearance,
but judge with righteous judgment.

7:25
So some of the Jerusalemites were saying,
Is this not the one whom they seek to kill?

7:26
And look, he speaks openly
and they say nothing to him;
could the rulers have truly recognized that this is the Christ?

7:27
But we know where this one is from;
and when the Christ comes,
no one knows where he is from.

7:28
So Jesus cried out in the temple, teaching
and saying,
You both know me
and you know where I am from;
and I have not come of myself,
but the one who sent me is true,
whom you do not know.

7:29
I know him, because I am from him
and that one sent me.

7:30
So they were seeking to seize him,
and no one laid a hand on him,
because his hour had not yet come.

7:31
And many from the crowd believed in him,
and were saying,
When the Christ comes,
will he do more signs
than this one has done?

7:32
The Pharisees heard
the crowd grumbling these things about him,
and the chief priests and the Pharisees sent officers
to seize him.

7:33
So Jesus said,
Yet a little time I am with you
and I am going to the one who sent me.

7:34
You will seek me
and will not find me,
and where I am
you cannot come.

7:35
So the Judeans said to themselves,
Where is this one about to go
that we will not find him?
Is he about to go to the Dispersion among the Greeks
and teach
the Greeks?

7:36
What is this word
that he spoke,
You will seek me
and will not find me,
and where I am
you cannot come?

7:37
Now on the last day, the great day of the feast, Jesus stood
and cried out, saying,
If anyone thirsts,
let him come to me and drink.

7:38
The one who believes in me, as the scripture said,
rivers of living water will flow from within him.

7:39
Now he said this about the Spirit
whom those believing in him were about
to receive;
for the Spirit was not yet given, because Jesus had not yet been glorified.

7:40
So some from the crowd, having heard these words,
were saying,
This is truly the Prophet.

7:41
Others were saying,
This is the Christ.
But others were saying,
Surely the Christ does not come from Galilee, does he?

7:42
Has not the scripture said that from the seed of David,
and from Bethlehem, the village where David was,
the Christ comes?

7:43
So a division arose in the crowd because of him.

7:44
And some of them wanted to seize him,
but no one laid hands on him.

7:45
So the officers came to the chief priests and Pharisees,
and those ones said to them,
Why did you not bring him?

7:46
The officers answered,
Never has a person spoken like this.

7:47
So the Pharisees answered them,
Have you also been deceived?

7:48
Has anyone of the rulers believed in him
or of the Pharisees?

7:49
But this crowd that does not know the law,
they are accursed.

7:50
Nicodemus says to them, the one who had come to him before,
being one of them,

7:51
Our law does not judge a person unless it first hears from him
and knows what he is doing, does it?

7:52
They answered
and said to him,
Are you also from Galilee?
Search
and see that no prophet arises from Galilee.

7:53
And each one went to his own house.
