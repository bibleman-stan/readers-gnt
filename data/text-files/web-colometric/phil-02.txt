2:1
If there is any therefore encouragement in Christ,
If any comfort of love,
If any fellowship of the Spirit,
If any affections and compassions,

2:2
Fulfill my,
So that the same you may be minded,
The same love having,
United in soul the same thing minding,

2:3
Nothing according to self-interest or according to vain conceit,
But in humility one another be esteeming surpassing themselves,

2:4
Not the things of themselves each considering,
But also the things of others each.

2:5
This let mind be in you
Which also in Christ Jesus was,

2:6
Who in the form of God existing
Not something to be grasped considered to be equal with God,

2:7
But Himself emptied
The form of a servant having taken,
In the likeness of men having been made;
And in appearance having been found as a man

2:8
He humbled Himself
Having become obedient unto death,
The death even of the cross.

2:9
Therefore also God Him highly exalted,
And granted to Him the name above every name,

2:10
That at the name of Jesus every knee should bow
In the heavens
And on earth
And under the earth,

2:11
And every tongue should confess
That is Lord Jesus Christ to the glory of God the Father.

2:12
Therefore,
Beloved my,
Just as always you have obeyed not as in my presence only
But now much more in my absence,
With fear and trembling your own salvation work out,

2:13
For god is the one working in you both to will
And to work according to His good pleasure.

2:14
All things do without murmurings and disputings,

2:15
So that you may be blameless and innocent children of God unblemished in the midst of a generation crooked and perverted
Among whom you shine as lights in the world

2:16
The word of life holding forth unto a boast to me in the day of Christ,
That not in vain I did run
Nor in vain toil.

2:17
But if even I am being poured out as a drink offering on the sacrifice and service of the faith of you I am glad
And rejoice with all you;

2:18
And likewise also you be glad
And rejoice with me.

2:19
I hope however in the Lord Jesus Timothy soon to send to you,
That I also may be encouraged
Having known the things concerning you.

2:20
No one for I have like-minded
Who genuinely the things relative to you will care for,

2:21
Those all for the things of themselves are seeking,
Not the things Jesus of Christ.

2:22
But the proven worth of him you know that as a father with a child
With me he has served in the gospel.

2:23
Him indeed therefore I hope to send when I shall have seen the things concerning me immediately;

2:24
I am persuaded now in the Lord that also I myself soon I will come.

2:25
Necessary now I esteemed it Epaphroditus
The brother and fellow worker and fellow soldier my,
Of you now messenger and minster of my need,
To send to you,

2:26
Since longing after he was all you,
And being deeply distressed because you heard that he was ill.

2:27
And indeed he was sick nearly unto death;
But God had mercy on him not on him now alone
But also on me,
That not sorrow upon sorrow I should have.

2:28
All the more speedily therefore I have sent him,
That having seen him again you may rejoice and I less anxious might be.

2:29
Receive therefore him in the Lord with all joy,
And such in honor hold,

2:30
Because for the sake of the work of Christ,
Unto death he came near,
Having disregarded his life,
So that he might fill up the of you deficit of toward me service.
