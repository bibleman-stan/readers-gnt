19:1
Then Pilate took Jesus
and had him flogged.

19:2
And the soldiers, having woven a crown from thorns,
placed it on his head,
and they threw a purple robe around him.

19:3
And they were coming to him
and saying,
Hail, King of the Judeans!
And they were giving him slaps.

19:4
And Pilate went outside again
and says to them,
Look, I am bringing him out to you,
so that you may know that I find no grounds for a charge in him.

19:5
So Jesus came outside,
wearing the thorny crown and the purple robe.
And he says to them,
Behold the man.

19:6
So when the chief priests and the officers saw him,
they cried out saying,
Crucify!
Crucify!
Pilate says to them,
Take
him yourselves and crucify him,
for I find no grounds for a charge in him.

19:7
The Judeans answered him,
We have a law,
and according to the law he ought to die,
because he made himself Son of God.

19:8
So when Pilate heard this word,
he was even more afraid.

19:9
And he entered the headquarters again
and says to Jesus,
Where are you from?
But Jesus gave him no answer.

19:10
So Pilate says to him,
You do not speak to me?
Do you not know that I have authority to release you
and have authority to crucify you?

19:11
Jesus answered him,
You would have no authority over me unless it had been given you from above.
Because of this the one who handed me over to you
has the greater sin.

19:12
From this point Pilate was seeking to release him.
But the Judeans cried out saying,
If you release this one,
you are no friend of Caesar.
Everyone
who makes himself king
speaks against Caesar.

19:13
So Pilate, having heard these words,
brought Jesus outside
and sat on the judgment seat at a place called the Stone Pavement, and in Hebrew Gabbatha.

19:14
Now it was the day of preparation of the Passover.
It was about the sixth hour.
And he says to the Judeans,
Behold your king!

19:15
So those ones cried out,
Away with him!
Away with him!
Crucify him!
Pilate says to them,
Shall I crucify your king?
The chief priests answered,
We have no
king except Caesar.

19:16
So then he handed him over to them
to be crucified.
So they took Jesus.

19:17
And carrying his own cross
he went out to the place called Place of a Skull,
which is called in Hebrew Golgotha,

19:18
where they crucified him,
and with him two others on either side, and Jesus in the middle.

19:19
And Pilate also wrote an inscription
and placed it on the cross.
Now it was
written, Jesus
the Nazarene, the King of the Judeans.

19:20
So this inscription
many of the Judeans read, because the place was near the city
where Jesus was crucified.
And it was written in Hebrew, in Latin, in Greek.

19:21
So the chief priests of the Judeans were saying to Pilate, Do not write, The King of the Judeans,
but that that one said, I am King of the Judeans.

19:22
Pilate answered,
What I have written I have written.

19:23
So the soldiers,
when they crucified Jesus,
took his garments
and made four parts,
a part for each soldier, and the tunic.
Now the tunic was seamless,
woven from the top through the whole.

19:24
So they said to one another, Let us not tear it, but cast lots for it to decide whose it will be.
So that the scripture might be fulfilled that says,
They divided my garments among themselves
and for my clothing they cast lots. So
the soldiers did these things.

19:25
Mary the wife of Clopas and Mary Magdalene.
Now standing beside the cross of Jesus were his mother
and his mother's sister,

19:26
So when Jesus saw
his mother and the disciple standing nearby
whom he loved,
he says to his mother, Woman, behold your son.

19:27
Then he says to the disciple,
Behold your mother.
And from that hour the disciple took her into his own home.

19:28
After this, Jesus, knowing that all things had already been accomplished,
so that the scripture might be fulfilled,
says, I thirst.

19:29
A jar full of sour wine was standing there.
So having put a sponge full of the sour wine
on a hyssop branch
they brought it to his mouth.

19:30
So when he received the sour wine,
Jesus said, It is finished.
And having bowed his head,
he gave up his spirit.

19:31
So the Judeans, since it was the day of preparation,
so that the bodies would not remain on the cross on the Sabbath,
for that
Sabbath day was a great day,
asked Pilate that their legs might be broken and they might be taken away.

19:32
So the soldiers came,
and they broke the legs of the first and of the other who had been crucified with him.

19:33
But when they came to Jesus,
since they saw that he had already died, they did not break his legs.

19:34
But one of the soldiers pierced his side with a spear,
and immediately blood and water came out.

19:35
And the one who has seen has testified,
and his testimony is true,
and that one knows that he speaks truth,
so that you also may believe.

19:36
For these things happened
so that the scripture might be fulfilled, Not a bone of his will be broken.

19:37
And again another scripture says, They will look
on the one whom they pierced.

19:38
And after these things, Joseph of Arimathea, being a disciple of Jesus, asked Pilate,
but secretly because of fear of the Judeans,
that he might take away the body of Jesus.
And Pilate permitted it.
So he came
and took away his body.

19:39
And Nicodemus also came, the one who had come to him by night at first,
bringing a mixture of myrrh and aloes, about a hundred pounds.

19:40
So they took the body of Jesus
and bound it in linen cloths with the spices,
just as is the custom of the Judeans in burial.

19:41
Now in the place
where he was crucified
was a garden, and in the garden a new tomb
in which no one had yet been laid.

19:42
So there, because of the day of preparation of the Judeans, since the tomb was nearby,
they laid Jesus.
