1:1
In many parts and in many ways God spoke long ago to the fathers through the prophets

1:2
at the end of these days he has spoken to us by a Son,
whom he appointed heir of all things,
through whom also he made the ages;

1:3
who being the radiance of his glory and the exact imprint of his essence,
and sustaining all things by the word of his power,
having made purification for sins through himself
he sat down at the right hand of the Majesty on high,

1:4
having become so much superior to the angels
as he has inherited a more distinguished name than theirs.

1:5
For to which of the angels did he ever say:
You are my Son,
today I have begotten you,
and again:
I will be to him a Father,
and he will be to me a Son?

1:6
And when he again brings the firstborn into the world,
he says:
And let all the angels of God worship him.

1:7
And concerning the angels he says:
The one who makes his angels winds,
and his ministers a flame of fire;

1:8
but concerning the Son:
Your throne, O God, is forever and ever,
and the scepter of uprightness is the scepter of your kingdom.

1:9
You have loved righteousness
and hated lawlessness;
therefore God, your God, has anointed you
with the oil of exultation beyond your companions;

1:10
and: You, Lord, in the beginning laid the foundation of the earth,
and the heavens are the works of your hands;

1:11
they will perish,
but you remain;
and they will all grow old like a garment,

1:12
and like a cloak you will roll them up,
like a garment they will also be changed;
but you are the same,
and your years will never run out.

1:13
But to which of the angels has he ever said:
Sit at my right hand
until I make your enemies a footstool for your feet?

1:14
Are they not all ministering spirits
sent out
for service on behalf of those who are about to inherit salvation?
