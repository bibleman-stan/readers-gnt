3:1
Therefore, holy
holy brothers, partakers of a heavenly calling,
brothers, sharers in a heavenly calling, consider the apostle and high priest of our confession, Jesus,

3:2
who was faithful
to the one who appointed him, as Moses also was in his house.

3:3
For this one has been counted worthy of more glory than Moses
inasmuch as the builder of a house has more honor than the house itself;

3:4
for every house is built by someone,
but the one who built all things is God.

3:5
And Moses indeed was faithful in all his house
as a servant, for a testimony of the things that would be spoken,

3:6
but Christ was faithful as a Son over his house;
whose house we are, if
we hold fast our confidence and the boast of our hope.

3:7
Therefore, as the Holy Spirit says:
Today if you hear his voice,

3:8
do not harden your hearts
as in the rebellion,
in the day of testing in the wilderness,

3:9
where your fathers tested me by trial
and saw my works

3:10
for forty years;
therefore I was provoked with that
generation and said: They always go astray in
their heart; and they did not know my ways;

3:11
as I swore in my anger:
They shall never enter into my rest.

3:12
Watch
brothers,
out lest there be in any of you an evil heart
of unbelief in turning away from the living God,

3:13
but encourage one another every day, as long
as it is called Today,
so that none of you may be hardened by the deceitfulness of sin;

3:14
for we have become partners of Christ
if indeed we hold the beginning of our confidence firm until the end.

3:15
In the saying:
Today if you hear his voice,
do not harden your hearts as in the rebellion.

3:16
For who, having heard, rebelled?
Was it not all those who came out of Egypt through Moses?

3:17
And with whom was he provoked for forty years?
Was it not with those who sinned,
whose bodies fell in the wilderness?

3:18
And to whom did he swear they would not enter his rest
if not to those who were disobedient?

3:19
And we see that they were unable to enter because of unbelief.
