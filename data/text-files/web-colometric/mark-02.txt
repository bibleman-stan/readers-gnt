2:1
And having entered again into Capernaum after some days it was heard that he was at home;

2:2
and many gathered together,
so that there was no longer room, not even at the door,
and he was speaking the word to them.

2:3
And they come bringing to him a paralyzed man
being carried by four men.

2:4
And not being able to bring him to Jesus
because of the crowd, they removed the roof
where he was,
and having dug through they lower the mat
where the paralyzed man was lying.

2:5
And seeing their faith
Jesus says to the paralyzed man:
Child, your sins are forgiven.

2:6
Now some of the scribes were sitting there
and reasoning in their hearts:

2:7
Why does this man speak like this?
He is blaspheming!
Who is able to forgive sins
except God alone?

2:8
And immediately Jesus, perceiving in his spirit that they were reasoning among themselves like this,
says to them:
Why are you reasoning these things in your hearts?

2:9
Which is easier, to say to the paralyzed man:
Your sins are forgiven,
or to say:
Rise
and pick up your mat and walk?

2:10
But so that you may know that the Son of Man has authority
on the earth to forgive sins --
he says to the paralyzed man:

2:11
I say to you, rise, pick up your mat
and go to your home.

2:12
And he rose
and immediately, picking up the mat,
he went out before them all,
so that they were all amazed
and were glorifying God
saying: We have never seen anything like this!

2:13
And he went out again along the sea;
and the whole crowd was coming to him,
and he was teaching them.

2:14
And passing by he saw Levi the son of Alphaeus sitting at the tax booth, and he says to him:
Follow me.
And rising up he followed him.

2:15
And it happens that he reclines at table in his house,
and many tax collectors and sinners were reclining with Jesus and his disciples,
for there were
many, and they were following him.

2:16
And the scribes of the Pharisees,
seeing that he eats with sinners and tax collectors,
were saying to his disciples: Why does he eat with tax collectors and sinners?

2:17
And hearing this Jesus says to them: Those who are strong have no need
of a physician, but those who are ill;
I did not come to call the righteous
but sinners.

2:18
And the disciples of John and the Pharisees were fasting.
And they come
and say to him:
Why is it that
the disciples of John and the disciples of the Pharisees fast,
but your disciples do not fast?

2:19
And Jesus said to them: Can the wedding guests
while the bridegroom is with them fast?
As long as they have the bridegroom
with them
they are not able to fast;

2:20
but days will come
when the bridegroom is taken away from them,
and then they will fast in that day.

2:21
No one sews a patch of unshrunk cloth onto an old garment;
otherwise
the patch pulls away from it, the new from the old,
and a worse tear results.

2:22
And no one puts new wine into old wineskins;
otherwise
the wine will burst the wineskins,
and the wine is destroyed
and the wineskins too. Rather, new wine into fresh wineskins.

2:23
And it happened that on the Sabbath he was going through the grain fields,
and his disciples began to make their way
plucking the heads of grain.

2:24
And the Pharisees were saying to him:
Look, why are they doing on the Sabbath what is not lawful?

2:25
And he says to them:
Have you never read what David did, when he had need
and was hungry, he and those with him?

2:26
How he entered the house of God in the time of Abiathar the high priest
and ate the bread of the presence,
which is not lawful to eat
except for the priests,
and he gave also to those who were with him?

2:27
And he was saying to them:
The Sabbath was made for the sake of humanity
and not humanity for the sake of the Sabbath;

2:28
so that the Son of Man is lord even of the Sabbath.
