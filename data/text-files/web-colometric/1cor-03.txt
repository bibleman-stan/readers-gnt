3:1
And I, brothers, was not able to speak to you as to spiritual people
but as to fleshly, as to infants in Christ.

3:2
I gave you milk to drink,
not solid food, for you were not yet able.
But not even now are you yet able,

3:3
for you are still fleshly.
For where
there is jealousy and strife among you, are you not fleshly
and walking according to mere human ways?

3:4
For when one says:
I belong to Paul,
and another
says: I to Apollos,
are you not merely human?

3:5
What then is Apollos? And what
is Paul?
Servants through whom you believed, and to each as the Lord gave.

3:6
I planted,
Apollos watered,
but God was causing the growth;

3:7
so that neither the one who plants is anything
nor the one who waters, but God who causes the growth.

3:8
Now the one who plants and the one who waters are one,
and each will receive his own reward according to his own labor,

3:9
for we are God's fellow workers;
you are God's field, God's building.

3:10
According to the grace of God given to me, like a wise master builder I laid a foundation,
and another builds upon it.
Let each one
be careful
how
he builds upon it;

3:11
for no one can lay a foundation other than the one that is laid,
which is Jesus Christ;

3:12
and if anyone builds upon the foundation with gold, silver, precious stones, wood, hay, straw,

3:13
the work of each will become evident,
for the day will reveal it;
because it is revealed by fire, and the quality of each one's work
the fire itself will test.

3:14
If the work of anyone remains
which he built upon it,
he will receive a reward;

3:15
if the work of anyone is burned up, he will suffer loss,
but he himself will be saved,
yet so as through fire.

3:16
Do you not know that you are a temple of God and the spirit of God dwells in you?

3:17
If anyone destroys the temple of God,
God will destroy that person;
for the temple of God is holy,
which is what you are.

3:18
Let no one deceive himself;
if anyone among you thinks he is wise in this age,
let him become foolish, so that he may become wise,

3:19
for the wisdom of this world is foolishness before God;
for it is written:
He catches the wise in their craftiness;

3:20
and again:
The Lord knows the reasonings of the wise,
that they are futile.

3:21
So let no one boast in men;
for all things belong to you,

3:22
whether things to come, all things are yours,
whether Paul or Apollos or Cephas
or the world or life or death or things present

3:23
and you belong to Christ,
and Christ belongs to God.
