3:1
Now Peter and John were going up to the temple
at the hour of prayer, the ninth hour,

3:2
and a certain man lame from his mother's
womb was being carried, whom they placed daily
at the gate of the temple called Beautiful
to ask alms from those entering the temple,

3:3
who seeing Peter and John
about to enter the
temple asked to receive alms.

3:4
And Peter looked intently at him with John and said:
Look at us.

3:5
And he gave attention to them
expecting to receive something from them.

3:6
But Peter said: Silver and
gold I do not have, but
what I have I give
to you: in the name of
Jesus Christ the Nazarene, walk.

3:7
And grasping him by the
right hand he raised him
up, and immediately his feet
and ankles were made strong,

3:8
and leaping up he stood
and was walking,
and he entered the temple with them
walking and leaping and praising God.

3:9
And all the people saw him walking and praising God,

3:10
and they recognized him
that this was the one who sat for alms at the Beautiful Gate of the temple,
and they were filled with wonder and amazement at what had happened to him.

3:11
While he was holding on to Peter and John all the people
ran together to them at the portico called Solomon's, utterly astonished.

3:12
And Peter seeing this addressed the people: Men of Israel, why do you marvel at this, or why
do you stare at us as though by our own power or godliness we made him walk?

3:13
The God of Abraham and Isaac and
Jacob, the God of our fathers,
glorified his servant Jesus, whom you indeed
delivered over and denied before Pilate,
when he had decided to release him.

3:14
But you denied the Holy and Righteous One
and asked for a murderer to be granted to you,

3:15
and you killed the Author of life,
whom God raised from the dead,
of which we are witnesses.

3:16
And on the basis of faith in his name
this one whom you see and know
his name has strengthened,
and the faith that is through him gave him this complete health
before all of you.

3:17
And now, brothers, I know that you acted in ignorance, just as your rulers also did.

3:18
But what God foretold through the mouth of all the prophets,
that his Christ would suffer,
he fulfilled in this way.

3:19
Repent therefore and turn back
so that your sins may be wiped away,

3:20
so that times of refreshing may come from the presence of the Lord
and he may send the Christ appointed for you, Jesus,

3:21
whom heaven must receive,
until the times of restoration of all things
which God spoke through the mouth of his holy prophets from of old.

3:22
Moses indeed said that
The Lord God will raise up for you a prophet from your brothers like me;
you shall listen to him in everything
whatever he speaks to you.

3:23
And it shall be that every soul that does not
listen to that prophet shall be destroyed from the people.

3:24
And all the prophets from Samuel and those after him
as many as spoke also proclaimed these days.

3:25
You are the sons of the prophets and of the covenant
which God made with your fathers, saying to Abraham: And in
your offspring all the families of the earth shall be blessed.

3:26
God having raised up his servant first for you
sent him to bless you
by turning each of you from your wickedness.
