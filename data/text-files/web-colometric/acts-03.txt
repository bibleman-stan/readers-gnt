3:1
And Peter and John were going up to the temple
at the hour of prayer, the ninth hour,

3:2
and a certain man, lame from his mother's womb, was being carried,
whom they placed daily at the gate of the temple called Beautiful,
to ask for alms from those entering the temple,

3:3
who, seeing Peter and John about to enter the temple,
was asking to receive alms.

3:4
And Peter, gazing at him together with John, said,
Look at us.

3:5
And he was giving them his attention,
expecting to receive something from them.

3:6
But Peter said,
Silver and gold I do not have,
but what I do have, this I give to you:
in the name of Jesus Christ the Nazarene, walk.

3:7
And seizing him by the right hand, he raised him up;
and immediately his feet and ankles were made strong,

3:8
and leaping up he stood
and was walking,
and he entered with them into the temple,
walking and leaping and praising God.

3:9
And all the people saw him walking and praising God,

3:10
and they recognized him,
that this was the one who sat for alms at the Beautiful Gate of the temple,
and they were filled with wonder and amazement at what had happened to him.

3:11
And while he was holding on to Peter and John,
all the people ran together to them
in the portico called Solomon's, utterly astonished.

3:12
And seeing this, Peter answered the people,
Men of Israel,
why do you marvel at this,
or why do you stare at us,
as though by our own power or godliness we had made him walk?

3:13
The God of Abraham and Isaac and Jacob,
the God of our fathers,
glorified his servant Jesus,
whom you indeed delivered up
and denied in the presence of Pilate,
when he had decided to release him;

3:14
but you denied the Holy and Righteous One
and asked for a murderer to be granted to you,

3:15
and the Author of life you killed,
whom God raised from the dead,
of which we are witnesses.

3:16
And on the basis of faith in his name,
this man whom you see and know,
his name has made strong,
and the faith that is through him has given him this perfect health
before all of you.

3:17
And now,
brothers,
I know that you acted in ignorance,
as did also your rulers;

3:18
but what God foretold through the mouth of all the prophets,
that his Christ would suffer,
he has thus fulfilled.

3:19
Repent therefore and turn back,
so that your sins may be blotted out,

3:20
so that times of refreshing may come from the presence of the Lord,
and that he may send the Christ appointed for you, Jesus,

3:21
whom heaven must receive,
until the times of restoration of all things,
which God spoke through the mouth of his holy prophets from of old.

3:22
Moses indeed said,
The Lord God will raise up for you a prophet from your brothers like me;
you shall listen to him in everything,
whatever he says to you.

3:23
And it shall be that every soul
that does not listen to that prophet
shall be utterly destroyed from the people.

3:24
And indeed all the prophets from Samuel and those after him,
as many as spoke, also proclaimed these days.

3:25
You are the sons of the prophets and of the covenant
which God made with your fathers,
saying to Abraham,
And in your seed all the families of the earth shall be blessed.

3:26
God, having raised up his servant,
sent him to you first, blessing you
by turning each of you from your wickedness.
