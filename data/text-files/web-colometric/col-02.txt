2:1
I want for you to know how great a struggle I am having for you and those in Laodicea
And as many as not have my seen face in the flesh,

2:2
That may be encouraged the hearts of them,
Having been knit together in love and to all the riches of the full assurance of understanding,
To the knowledge of the mystery of God which is Christ,

2:3
In whom are all the treasures of wisdom and of knowledge hidden.

2:4
This I say so that no one you might delude by persuasive speech.

2:5
If truly indeed in the flesh I am absent,
Yet in spirit with you I am,
Rejoicing and your seeing good order and the firmness of the in Christ faith of you.

2:6
Just as therefore you have received Christ Jesus the Lord,
In Him walk,

2:7
Having been rooted and being built up in Him
And being strengthened in the faith just as you were taught,
Abounding with thanksgiving.

2:8
Take heed lest anyone you there will be taking captive through philosophy and empty deceit
According to the tradition of men,
According to the principles of the world and not according to Christ;

2:9
For in Him dwells all the fullness of the Deity bodily,

2:10
And you are in Him complete,
Who is the head of all rule and authority,

2:11
In the circumcision of Christ,
In circumcision
In the flesh,

2:12
Having been buried with Him in baptism
In which also you were raised with Him through the faith of the working of God
Who raised Him out from the dead;

2:13
And you dead being in the trespasses
And in the uncircumcision of the flesh of you He made alive together you with Him;
Having forgiven us all the transgressions,

2:14
Having blotted out the against us handwriting in the decrees
Which was adverse to us,
And it He has taken out of the way having nailed it to the cross;

2:15
Having disarmed the rulers and the authorities
He made a show of them in public,
Having triumphed over them in it.

2:16
Not therefore anyone you let judge in regard to food or in regard to drink
Or in regard to a feast or a New Moon or Sabbaths,

2:17
Which are a shadow the things coming,
The however body is of Christ.

2:18
No one you let disqualify delighting
In humility and the worship of the angels which he has seen
Detailing vainly being puffed up by the mind of the flesh of him,

2:19
And not holding fast to the head
From whom all the body by the joints
And ligaments being supplied and being knit together increases with the increase of God.

2:20
If you have died with Christ away from the principles of the world,
Why as if living in the world do you submit to decrees;

2:21
Not you should handle Not you should taste Not you should touch,

2:22
Which are all unto decay with the use,
According to the commandments and teachings of men?

2:23
Which are an appearance indeed having of wisdom in self-imposed worship and humility and harsh treatment of the body,
Not of honor a certain against the indulgence of the flesh.
