18:1
And he was telling them a parable about the need
to always pray
and not lose heart,

18:2
saying,
There was a judge in a certain city
who did not fear God
and did not respect people.

18:3
And there was a widow in that city,
and she kept coming to him, saying,
Grant me justice against my adversary.

18:4
And he was unwilling for a time, but afterward he said to himself,
Even though I do not fear God
and do not respect people,

18:5
yet because this widow keeps causing me trouble,
I will grant her justice,
so that she will not wear me out by continually coming.

18:6
And the Lord said,
Hear what the unrighteous judge says;

18:7
and will not God bring about justice for his chosen ones who cry out to him day and night,
and will he delay long over them?

18:8
I tell you that he will bring about justice for them quickly.
Yet when the Son of Man comes, will he
find faith on the earth?

18:9
And he also told this parable to some who trusted in themselves that they were righteous
and looked down on everyone else,
this parable:

18:10
Two men went up to the temple to pray,
one a Pharisee and the other a tax collector.

18:11
The Pharisee, standing by himself, was praying these things:
God, I thank you that I am not like the rest of people, swindlers, unjust, adulterers, or even like this tax collector;

18:12
I fast twice a week;
I give a tenth of all I acquire.

18:13
But the tax collector, standing far off, would not even lift his eyes to heaven,
but was beating his chest, saying,
God, be merciful to me, the sinner.

18:14
I tell you, this man went down to his house justified rather than the other;
for everyone who exalts himself will be humbled,
but the one who humbles himself will be exalted.

18:15
And they were bringing even infants to him
so that he might touch them;
but when the disciples saw it, they rebuked them.

18:16
But Jesus called them to himself, saying,
Let the children come to me
and do not hinder them,
for the kingdom of God belongs to such as these.

18:17
Truly I tell you,
whoever does not receive the kingdom of God like a child will never enter it.

18:18
And a certain ruler asked him, saying,
Good Teacher,
what must I do
to inherit eternal life?

18:19
And Jesus said to him,
Why do you call me good?
No one is good except God alone.

18:20
You know the commandments:
Do not commit adultery,
Do not murder,
Do not steal,
Do not bear false witness,
Honor your father and mother.

18:21
And he said,
All these things I have kept from my youth.

18:22
And when Jesus heard this, he said to him,
One thing you still lack;
sell all that you have
and distribute to the poor,
and you will have treasure in heaven,
and come, follow me.

18:23
But when he heard these things,
he became very sad,
for he was extremely rich.

18:24
And Jesus, looking at him,
said,
How hard it is for those who have wealth
to enter the kingdom of God;

18:25
for it is easier
for a camel to go through the eye of a needle
than for a rich person to enter the kingdom of God.

18:26
And those who heard it said,
Then who can be saved?

18:27
But he said,
What is impossible with people is possible with God.

18:28
And Peter said,
Behold, we have left our own things
and followed you.

18:29
And he said to them,
Truly I tell you,
there is no one
who has left house or wife or brothers or parents or children
for the sake of the kingdom of God,

18:30
who will not receive many times more in this present time
and in the age to come, eternal life.

18:31
And taking the twelve aside,
he said to them,
Behold, we are going up to Jerusalem,
and everything written through the prophets about the Son of Man will be fulfilled;

18:32
for he will be delivered over to the Gentiles and will be mocked
and treated shamefully
and spit upon,

18:33
and after flogging him they will kill him,
and on the third day he will rise.

18:34
And they understood none of these things,
and this word was hidden from them,
and they did not grasp what was said.

18:35
And it happened as he drew near to Jericho,
a certain blind man was sitting beside the road begging.

18:36
And hearing a crowd going by,
he inquired what this might be;

18:37
and they told him that Jesus of Nazareth was passing by.

18:38
And he cried out, saying,
Jesus, Son of David, have mercy on me.

18:39
And those who were leading the way rebuked him, telling him to be silent;
but he cried out all the more,
Son of David, have mercy on me.

18:40
And Jesus stopped and commanded him to be brought to him.
And when he came near,
he asked him,

18:41
What do you want me to do for you?
And he said,
Lord, that I may see again.

18:42
And Jesus said to him,
Receive your sight;
your faith has saved you.

18:43
And immediately he received his sight,
and he was following him,
glorifying God.
And all the people, when they saw it, gave praise to God.
