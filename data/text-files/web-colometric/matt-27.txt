27:1
And when morning came
all the chief priests took counsel
and the elders of the people against Jesus,
to put him to death;

27:2
and binding him they led him away
and handed him over to Pilate the governor.

27:3
Then Judas, seeing
that he who betrayed him had been condemned,
felt remorse and returned the thirty pieces of silver to the chief priests and elders

27:4
saying,
I sinned by betraying innocent blood.
And they said,
What is that to us? See to it yourself.

27:5
And throwing the silver pieces into the temple he departed,
and going away he hanged himself.

27:6
And the chief priests took the silver pieces
and said,
It is not lawful to put them into the treasury,
since it is blood money;

27:7
and taking counsel
they bought with them the Potter’s Field as a burial place for strangers.

27:8
Therefore that field has been called the Field of Blood to this day.

27:9
Then what was spoken through Jeremiah the prophet was fulfilled, saying,
And they took the thirty pieces of silver, the price of the one whose price had been set
by the sons of Israel,

27:10
and they gave them for the potter’s field, as
the Lord directed me.

27:11
And Jesus stood before the governor;
and the governor questioned him saying,
Are you the King of the Jews?
And Jesus said, You say so.

27:12
And while he was being accused by the chief priests and elders
he answered nothing.

27:13
Then Pilate says to him,
Do you not hear how many things they testify against you?

27:14
And he did not answer him, not even to a single charge,
so that the governor was greatly amazed.

27:15
Now at the feast the governor was accustomed to release one prisoner to the crowd whom they wanted.

27:16
And they had then a notorious prisoner called Jesus Barabbas.

27:17
So when they had gathered
Pilate said to them,
Whom do you want me to release for you, Jesus Barabbas or Jesus who is called Christ?

27:18
For he knew that it was out of envy that they had handed him over.

27:19
And while he was sitting on the judgment seat
his wife sent word to him saying,
Have nothing to do with that righteous man,
for I suffered much today in a dream because of him.

27:20
But the chief priests and the elders
persuaded the crowds to ask for Barabbas
and to destroy Jesus.

27:21
And the governor answered and said to them,
Which of the two do you want me to release for you?
And they said, Barabbas.

27:22
Pilate says to them,
What then shall I do with Jesus who is called Christ?
They all say, Let him be crucified.

27:23
And he said,
Why, what evil has he done?
But they shouted all the more saying, Let him be crucified.

27:24
And Pilate, seeing that he was gaining nothing
but rather a riot was starting,
took water
and washed his hands before the crowd saying,
I am innocent of this blood; see to it yourselves.

27:25
And all the people answered and said,
His blood be on us and on our children.

27:26
Then he released Barabbas to them,
and having flogged Jesus
he handed him over to be crucified.

27:27
Then the soldiers of the governor took Jesus into the praetorium
and gathered the whole cohort around him.

27:28
And stripping him
they put a scarlet robe on him,

27:29
and weaving a crown of thorns
they placed it on his head
and a reed in his right hand,
and kneeling before him they mocked him saying,
Hail, King of the Jews,

27:30
and spitting on him they took the reed
and struck him on the head.

27:31
And when they had mocked him,
they stripped him of the robe
and put his own garments on him
and led him away to crucify him.

27:32
And going out they found a man of Cyrene
named Simon;
they compelled this man
to carry his cross.

27:33
And coming to a place
called Golgotha,
which means Place of the Skull,

27:34
they gave him wine mixed with gall to drink;
and tasting it he was unwilling
to drink.

27:35
And having crucified him
they divided his garments
by casting lots,

27:36
and sitting down they kept watch over him there.

27:37
And they placed above his head the charge against him written:
This is Jesus, the King of the Jews.

27:38
Then two robbers are crucified with him,
one at his right and one at his left.

27:39
And those passing by were blaspheming him
shaking their heads

27:40
and saying,
You who destroy the temple
and build it in three days, save yourself; if you are the Son of God,
come down from the cross.

27:41
Likewise also the chief priests, mocking him with the scribes and elders, were saying,

27:42
He saved others,
himself he cannot save;
he is the King of Israel,
let him come down now from the cross
and we will believe in him.

27:43
He trusts in God; let God deliver him now if he wants him;
for he said, I am the Son of God.

27:44
And in the same way the robbers who were crucified with him were reviling him.

27:45
And from the sixth hour darkness came over all the land until the ninth hour.

27:46
And about the ninth hour Jesus cried out with a loud voice saying,
Eli, Eli, lema sabachthani?
That is,
My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?

27:47
And some of those standing there, hearing this, were saying, This man is calling Elijah.

27:48
And immediately one of them ran and took a sponge
and filling it with sour wine and putting it on a reed gave him a drink.

27:49
But the rest were saying,
Wait, let us see if Elijah comes to save him.

27:50
And Jesus, crying out again with a loud voice, yielded up his spirit.

27:51
And look — the curtain of the temple was torn in two from top to bottom,
and the earth shook,
and the rocks were split,

27:52
and the tombs were opened
and many bodies of the saints who had fallen asleep were raised,

27:53
and coming out of the tombs
after his resurrection they entered the holy city
and appeared to many.

27:54
And the centurion and those with him keeping watch over Jesus,
seeing the earthquake and the things that happened,
were terrified greatly saying,
Truly this was the Son of God.

27:55
And many women were there
watching from a distance,
who had followed Jesus from Galilee
serving him;

27:56
among whom was Mary Magdalene and Mary the mother of James and Joseph
and the mother of the sons of Zebedee.

27:57
And when evening came a rich man from Arimathea came,
named Joseph, who himself had also become a disciple of Jesus;

27:58
this man went to Pilate and asked for the body of Jesus.
Then Pilate ordered it to be given.

27:59
And taking the body
Joseph wrapped it in clean linen cloth,

27:60
and placed it in his own new tomb
which he had cut in the rock,
and rolling a great stone to the door of the tomb he went away.

27:61
And Mary Magdalene was there and the other Mary sitting opposite the tomb.

27:62
And the next day,
which is after the Preparation,
the chief priests and the Pharisees gathered before Pilate

27:63
saying,
Sir, we remember
that that deceiver said while still alive,
After three days I rise;

27:64
so give orders for the tomb to be made secure until the third day,
lest his disciples come and steal him
and say to the people,
He has been raised from the dead,
and the last deception will be worse than the first.

27:65
Pilate said to them,
You have a guard;
go
make it as secure as you know how.

27:66
And they went and made the tomb secure
by sealing the stone
along with the guard.
