10:1
Truly truly I say to you,
that one is a thief and a robber:
the one who does not enter through the gate
into the sheepfold but climbs up another way.

10:2
But the one who enters through the gate is the shepherd of the sheep.

10:3
To this one the gatekeeper opens,
and the sheep hear his voice,
and he calls his own sheep by name and leads them out.

10:4
When he has put out all his own,
he goes before them,
and the sheep follow him,
because they know his voice.

10:5
But a stranger they will never follow,
rather they will flee from him,
because they do not know the voice of strangers.

10:6
This figure of speech Jesus spoke to them,
but they did not understand what the things were
that he was saying to them.

10:7
So Jesus said to them again, Truly truly I say to you that I am the gate of the sheep.

10:8
All who came before me are thieves
and robbers,
but the sheep did not listen to them.

10:9
I am the gate.
If anyone enters through me he will be saved
and will come in
and will go out
and will find pasture.

10:10
The thief comes only to steal and to slaughter
and to destroy.
I came
so that they may have life
and have it abundantly.

10:11
I am the good shepherd.
The good shepherd
lays down his life for the sheep.

10:12
The hired hand who is not a shepherd,
whose own the sheep are not,
sees the wolf coming and abandons the sheep
and flees-- and the wolf snatches them and scatters them--

10:13
because he is a hired hand
and the sheep do not matter to him.

10:14
I am the good shepherd,
and I know my own
and my own know me,

10:15
just as the Father knows me
and I know the Father,
and I lay down my life for the sheep.

10:16
And I have other sheep
that are not of this fold.
Those also I must lead,
and they will hear my voice,
and there will be one flock, one shepherd.

10:17
Because of this the Father loves me,
because I lay down my life,
so that I may take it up again.

10:18
No one takes it from me,
but I lay it down of myself.
I have authority to lay it down, and I have authority
to take it up again.
This command I received from my Father.

10:19
A division arose again among the Judeans because of these words.

10:20
And many of them were saying,
He has a demon
and is mad.
Why do you listen to him?

10:21
Others were saying,
These are not the words of one who is demon-possessed.
A demon cannot open the eyes of the blind, can it?

10:22
Then came the Feast of Dedication in Jerusalem.
It was winter,

10:23
and Jesus was walking in the temple in the colonnade of Solomon.

10:24
So the Judeans surrounded him
and were saying to him,
How long will you keep us in suspense? If you are the Christ,
tell us plainly.

10:25
Jesus answered them,
I told you
and you do not believe.
The works that I do in the name of my Father,
these testify about me.

10:26
But you do not believe,
because you are not of my sheep.

10:27
My sheep hear my voice,
and I know them,
and they follow me.

10:28
And I give them eternal life,
and they will never perish into the age,
and no one will snatch them from my hand.

10:29
My Father, what he has given me,
is greater than all,
and no one is able to snatch them from the hand of the Father.

10:30
I and the Father are one.

10:31
So the Judeans again picked up stones,
to stone him.

10:32
Jesus answered them,
I showed you many good works from the Father.
For which of them are you stoning me?

10:33
The Judeans answered him,
We do not stone you for a good work
but for blasphemy, and because you, being a man, make yourself God.

10:34
Jesus answered them, Is it not written
in your law, I said, You are gods?

10:35
If he called them gods
to whom the word of God came,
and the scripture cannot be broken,

10:36
do you say of the one whom the Father consecrated
and sent into the world
that you blaspheme,
because I said,
I am
the Son of God?

10:37
If I do not do the works of my Father,
do not believe me.

10:38
But if I do, even if you do not believe me,
believe the works,
so that you may know and understand
that the Father is in me and I am in the Father.

10:39
So they were seeking again to seize him,
and he went out from their grasp.

10:40
And he went away again beyond the Jordan to the place
where John was first baptizing,
and he stayed there.

10:41
And many came to him
and were saying, John did no sign,
but everything that John said about this one was true.

10:42
And many believed in him there.
