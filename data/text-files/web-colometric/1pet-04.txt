4:1
Since Christ therefore suffered in the flesh,
arm yourselves also with the same mind,
because the one who has suffered in the flesh has ceased from sin,

4:2
so as no longer for human desires but for the will of God
to live the remaining time in the flesh.

4:3
For the time already past is sufficient for having carried out the desire of the nations,
having walked in sensuality, lusts, drunkenness, carousing, drinking parties,
and lawless idolatries.

4:4
In this they are surprised
that you do not run with them into the same flood of dissipation, blaspheming;

4:5
they will give account to the one who stands ready to judge the living and the dead;

4:6
for this is why the good news was proclaimed even to the dead,
so that they might be judged according to human beings in the flesh but live
according to God in the spirit.

4:7
Now the end of all things has drawn near.
Be self-controlled
therefore and be sober-minded for the sake of prayers;

4:8
above all keeping your love for one another fervent,
because love covers a multitude of sins;

4:9
be hospitable to one another without grumbling;

4:10
as each one has received a gift,
minister it to one another as good stewards of the manifold grace of God;

4:11
if anyone speaks,
as the oracles of God;
if anyone serves,
as from the strength which God supplies;
so that in all things God may be glorified through Jesus Christ,
to whom belongs the glory and the dominion forever and ever. Amen.

4:12
Beloved, do not be surprised at the fiery trial among you
which is happening to you for testing as though something strange were happening to you,

4:13
but insofar as you share in the sufferings of Christ
rejoice,
so that also at the revelation of his glory you may rejoice with exultation.

4:14
If you are reproached in the name of Christ,
you are blessed, because the Spirit of glory and of God rests upon you.

4:15
For let none of you suffer as a murderer or a thief or an evildoer or as a meddler;

4:16
but if as a Christian,
let him not be ashamed,
but let him glorify God in this name.

4:17
Because it is time for judgment to begin from the household of God;
and if it begins first from us,
what will be the outcome of those who disobey the gospel of God?

4:18
And if the righteous person is scarcely saved,
where will the ungodly and the sinner appear?

4:19
Therefore let those also who suffer according to the will of God
entrust their souls to a faithful Creator in doing good.
