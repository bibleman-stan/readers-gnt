8:1
In those days again great the crowd being
and not having what they might eat
having called to him the disciples He says to them

8:2
I am moved with compassion upon the crowd because already days three they continue with Me
and nothing have that they might eat

8:3
And if I shall send away them hungry to the homes of them they will faint on the way
For some of them
from afar come

8:4
And answered Him the disciples of him From where these will be able anyone here to satisfy with bread in this desolate place

8:5
And He was asking them
How many have you loaves
And they said Seven

8:6
And He directs the crowd to recline on the ground
And having taken the seven loaves
having given thanks He broke them
and kept giving them to disciples of him
that they might set before them
And they set it before the crowd

8:7
And they had small fish a few
And having blessed them
He ordered also these to be set before them

8:8
And they ate and were satisfied
And they took up over and above of fragments seven baskets

8:9
There were now about four thousand
And He sent away them

8:10
And immediately having entered into the boat with the disciples of him He came
into the district of Dalmanutha

8:11
And went out the Pharisees
and began to dispute with him
seeking from him a sign from heaven
testing Him

8:12
And having sighed deeply in the spirit of him He says
Why the generation this seeks a sign
Truly I say to you
if there will be given to the generation this a sign

8:13
And having left them
again having embarked He went away to the other side

8:14
And they forgot to take loaves
and if not one loaf not they had with them in the boat

8:15
And He was instructing them saying
Watch out take heed of the leaven of the Pharisees and of the leaven of Herod

8:16
And they were reasoning with one another because loaves not they have

8:17
And having known it He says to them
Why reason you because loaves not you have
Not yet do you perceive nor understand Hardened
do you have the heart of you

8:18
Eyes having
not do you see
And ears having not do you hear
And not do you remember

8:19
When the five loaves I broke for the five thousand
how many hand-baskets of fragments full took you up
They say to him Twelve

8:20
When also the seven to the four thousand
of how many baskets the fillings of fragments took you up
And said to him Seven

8:21
And He was saying to them Not yet do you understand

8:22
And they come to Bethsaida
And they bring to him a blind man
and implore Him that him He might touch

8:23
And having taken hold of the hand of the blind man
He led forth him out of the village
and having spit upon the eyes of him
having laid the hands upon him
He was asking him
if anything you see

8:24
And having looked up he was saying
I see the men for as trees I see them walking

8:25
Then again He laid the hands upon the eyes of him
and he opened his eyes and he was restored
and he began to see clearly everything

8:26
And He sent him to the home of him saying
Not into the village may you enter

8:27
And went forth Jesus and the disciples of him into the villages of Caesarea Philippi
And on the way He was questioning the disciples of him
saying to them
Whom Me do pronounce
men to be

8:28
And they answered Him
saying John the Baptist and others Elijah others now one of the prophets

8:29
And He was questioning them
You however whom Me
do pronounce to be
Answering Peter says to him
You are the Christ

8:30
And He warned them that no one they should tell concerning Him

8:31
And He began to teach them that it is necessary for the Son of Man many things to suffer
and to be rejected by the elders and the chief priests and the scribes
and to be killed
and after three days to rise again

8:32
And openly the word He was speaking
And having taken Him Peter to him
he began to rebuke Him

8:33
And having turned and having looked upon the disciples of him
He rebuked Peter
and said
Get behind Me Satan
for not you have in mind the things of God
but the things of men

8:34
And having called to him the crowd with the disciples of him
He said to them
If anyone desires after Me to come
let him deny himself
and let him take up the cross of him and let him follow Me

8:35
Whoever for if might desire life his to save
will lose it
whoever now will lose the life of him on account of me and of the gospel he will save it

8:36
What for does it profit a man to gain the world whole
and to lose the soul of him

8:37
What for shall give a man as an exchange for the soul of him

8:38
Whoever for if may be ashamed of me and My words in the generation this
adulterous and sinful
also the Son of Man will be ashamed of him
when He shall come in the glory of the Father of him with the angels holy
