8:1
In those days when again there was a great crowd
and they had nothing to eat,
he called the disciples and says to them:

8:2
I have compassion on the crowd because already three days they have stayed with me
and they have nothing to eat.

8:3
And if I send them away hungry to their homes, they will collapse on the way,
and some of them
have come from far away.

8:4
And his disciples answered him: From where will anyone be able to satisfy these people with bread here in the wilderness?

8:5
And he asked them:
How many loaves do you have?
And they said: Seven.

8:6
And he directs the crowd to sit down on the ground.
And taking the seven loaves,
having given thanks he broke them
and kept giving them to his disciples
to set before the people,
and they set them before the crowd.

8:7
And they had a few small fish.
And having blessed them,
he said to set these before them also.

8:8
And they ate and were satisfied,
and they picked up seven baskets of leftover fragments.

8:9
Now there were about four thousand.
And he sent them away.

8:10
And at once getting into the boat with his disciples he came
to the district of Dalmanutha.

8:11
And the Pharisees came out
and began to argue with him,
seeking from him a sign from heaven,
testing him.

8:12
And groaning deeply in his spirit he says:
Why does this generation seek a sign?
Truly I say to you:
no sign will be given to this generation.

8:13
And leaving them,
he got back in the boat and went away to the other side.

8:14
And they had forgotten to bring bread,
and except for one loaf they had nothing with them in the boat.

8:15
And he was warning them, saying:
Watch out — beware of the leaven of the Pharisees and the leaven of Herod.

8:16
And they were reasoning with one another: It is because we have no bread.

8:17
And knowing this he says to them:
Why are you reasoning that you have no bread?
Do you not yet perceive or understand? Is your heart
hardened?

8:18
Having eyes
do you not see,
and having ears do you not hear?
And do you not remember:

8:19
when I broke the five loaves for the five thousand,
how many baskets full of fragments did you pick up?
They say to him: Twelve.

8:20
When the seven for the four thousand,
how many baskets full of fragments did you pick up?
And they say to him: Seven.

8:21
And he said to them: Do you not yet understand?

8:22
And they come to Bethsaida.
And they bring a blind man to him
and beg him to touch him.

8:23
And taking hold of the blind man's hand,
he led him out of the village.
And spitting on his eyes,
laying his hands on him,
he asked him:
Do you see anything?

8:24
And looking up he said:
I see people — I see them like trees, walking.

8:25
Then again he laid his hands on his eyes,
and he looked intently and was restored
and saw everything clearly.

8:26
And he sent him to his home, saying:
Do not even enter the village.

8:27
And Jesus went out, and his disciples, to the villages of Caesarea Philippi.
And on the way he asked his disciples,
saying to them:
Who do people say
I am?

8:28
And they told him,
saying: John the baptizer, and others Elijah, and others that he is one of the prophets.

8:29
And he himself asked them:
But who do you say
I am?
Peter answered and says to him:
You are the Christ.

8:30
And he warned them to tell no one about him.

8:31
And he began to teach them that the Son of Man must suffer many things
and be rejected by the elders and the chief priests and the scribes
and be killed
and after three days rise again.

8:32
And he was speaking the word openly.
And Peter, taking him aside,
began to rebuke him.

8:33
But he, turning and seeing his disciples,
rebuked Peter
and says:
Get behind me, Satan,
because you do not have in mind the things of God
but the things of men.

8:34
And calling the crowd together with his disciples,
he said to them:
If anyone wants to come after me,
let him deny himself
and take up his cross and follow me.

8:35
For whoever wants to save his life
will lose it,
but whoever loses his life for my sake and the sake of the good news will save it.

8:36
For what does it profit a person to gain the whole world
and forfeit his life?

8:37
For what could a person give in exchange for his life?

8:38
For whoever is ashamed of me and my words in this generation,
this adulterous and sinful generation,
the Son of Man will also be ashamed of him
when he comes in the glory of his Father with the holy angels.
