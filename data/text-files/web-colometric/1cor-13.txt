13:1
If I speak with the tongues of men and of angels,
but do not have love,
I have become a resounding gong or a clanging cymbal.

13:2
And if I have prophecy
and know all mysteries and all knowledge,
and if I have all faith so as to move mountains, but do not have love, I am nothing.

13:3
And if I give away all my possessions
to feed the poor, and if I deliver my body
to be burned,
but do not have love,
I gain nothing.

13:4
Love is patient, love is kind,
love does not envy,
it does not boast, it is not puffed up,

13:5
it does not act improperly,
it does not seek its own things,
it is not provoked,
it does not reckon the wrong,

13:6
it does not rejoice over unrighteousness,
but rejoices with the truth;

13:7
it bears all things,
believes all things, hopes all things, endures all things.

13:8
Love never falls.
But whether prophecies, they will be done away;
whether tongues, they will cease;
whether knowledge, it will be done away.

13:9
For we know in part
and we prophesy in part;

13:10
but when the perfect comes,
the partial will be done away.

13:11
When I was a child,
I spoke as a child,
I thought as a child,
I reasoned as a child;
when I became a man,
I did away with childish things.

13:12
For now we see through a mirror in an enigma,
but then face to face;
now
I know in part,
but then
I shall know fully, just as I also have been fully known.

13:13
But now faith, hope, love remain, these three;
and the greatest of these is love.
