13:1
If in the tongues of men I speak and of angels
love however not have
I have become a brass sounding or a cymbal clanging

13:2
And if I should have prophecy
and understand the mysteries all and all the knowledge
and if I should have all the faith
so as mountains to remove
love however not have
nothing I am

13:3
And if I may give away all the possessions of me
and if I may deliver up the body of me
that I may boast
love however not have
nothing I am profited

13:4
Love is patient
is kind love
not is envious love
not is boastful
not is puffed up

13:5
not acts unbecomingly
not seeks the things of its own
not is easily provoked
not it keeps account of wrongs

13:6
not delights at unrighteousness
rejoices however in the truth

13:7
All things it bears
all things believes
all things hopes
all things endures

13:8
Love never fails
if however there are prophesies they will be done away
if tongues they will be ceased
if knowledge it will pass away

13:9
In part for we know
and in part we prophesy

13:10
when however should come the perfect
the in part will be done away

13:11
When I was a child
I was speaking like a child
I was thinking like a child
I was reasoning like a child
when I became a man
I did away with the things of the child

13:12
We see for presently through a glass in obscurity
then however face to face
presently I know in part
then however I will know fully
as also I have been fully known

13:13
Now however abide
faith hope love
the things three these
the greatest however of these is love
