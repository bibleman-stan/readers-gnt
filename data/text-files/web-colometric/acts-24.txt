24:1
And after five days the high priest Ananias came down
with some elders and an attorney named Tertullus,
and they brought their case before the governor against Paul.

24:2
And when he was called
Tertullus began to accuse him
saying:
Since through you we enjoy much peace
and since reforms are being made for this nation through your foresight,

24:3
we accept this in every way and everywhere,
most excellent Felix, with all gratitude.

24:4
But so that I may not detain you further,
I beg you in your kindness to hear us briefly.

24:5
For we have found this man to be a plague
and one who stirs up riots among all the Jews throughout the world and a ringleader of the sect of the Nazarenes,

24:6
who even tried to profane the temple,
and we seized him,

24:8
from whom you yourself will be able, by examining him, to learn about all these things
of which we accuse him.

24:9
And the Jews also joined in the attack,
asserting that these things were so.

24:10
And Paul answered
when the governor nodded to him to speak:
Knowing that for many years you have been a judge over this nation
I cheerfully make my defense regarding my own affairs,

24:11
since you can verify that it has been no more than twelve days
since I went up to worship in Jerusalem,

24:12
and neither in the temple did they find me disputing with anyone
or stirring up a crowd
nor in the synagogues nor throughout the city,

24:13
nor can they prove to you
the things
of which they now accuse me.

24:14
But this I confess to you, that according to the Way
which they call a sect
I worship the God of our fathers,
believing everything that is in accordance with the law
and that is written in the prophets,

24:15
having a hope in God
which these men themselves also accept,
that there will be a resurrection
of both the righteous and the unrighteous;

24:16
In this I also do my best to maintain a blameless conscience before God
and before people at all times.

24:17
And after many years I came bringing charitable gifts to my nation
and offerings,

24:18
in the midst of which they found me purified in the temple, without a crowd or any disturbance,

24:19
but some Jews from Asia, who ought
to be here before you and bring charges
if they have anything against me,

24:20
or else let these men themselves say what wrongdoing they found when I stood before the council

24:21
other than this one statement which I cried out
while standing among them: Concerning the resurrection of the dead I am on trial before you today.

24:22
But Felix put them off,
having a rather accurate knowledge of the Way,
saying:
When Lysias the tribune comes down I will decide your case;

24:23
and he gave orders to the centurion to keep him in custody but to allow him some freedom
and not to prevent any of his friends from attending to his needs.

24:24
And after some days Felix arrived with Drusilla his own wife
who was Jewish, and sent for Paul
and heard him concerning faith in Christ Jesus.

24:25
And as he was discussing righteousness and self-control
and the coming judgment,
Felix became alarmed and answered:
Go away for the present,
and when I find a convenient time
I will summon you;

24:26
at the same time he was hoping that money would be given to him by Paul;
therefore
he also sent for him more often and conversed with him.

24:27
And when two years had passed
Felix was succeeded by Porcius Festus;
and wanting to do the Jews a favor
Felix left Paul in prison.
