12:1
During which time, when the myriads of the crowd had gathered together,
so that they were trampling one another,
he began to say to his disciples first,
Guard yourselves from the yeast,
which is hypocrisy, of the Pharisees.

12:2
But nothing covered up exists
that will not be revealed,
and nothing hidden that will not be made known.

12:3
Therefore whatever you said in the dark
will be heard in the light,
and what you whispered in private rooms will be proclaimed on the housetops.

12:4
And I say to you, my friends, do not fear those who kill the body
and after that have nothing more they can do.

12:5
But I will show you whom to fear:
fear the one who, after killing, has authority to cast into Gehenna;
yes, I tell you, fear this one.

12:6
Are not five sparrows sold for two pennies?
And not one of them is forgotten before God.

12:7
But even the hairs of your head are all numbered;
do not fear;
you are worth more than many sparrows.

12:8
And I tell you,
everyone who confesses me before people,
the Son of Man will also confess him before the angels of God;

12:9
but the one who denies me before people will be denied before the angels of God.

12:10
And everyone who speaks a word against the Son of Man, it will be forgiven him,
but the one who blasphemes against the Holy Spirit will not be forgiven.

12:11
And when they bring you before the synagogues and the rulers and the authorities,
do not worry about how or what you should say in your defense, or what you should speak;

12:12
for the Holy Spirit will teach you in that very hour what must be said.

12:13
And someone from the crowd said to him,
Teacher, tell my brother to divide the inheritance with me.

12:14
But he said to him,
Man, who appointed me a judge or arbitrator over you?

12:15
And he said to them,
Watch out
and guard yourselves from all greed,
because not in someone's abundance
does his life consist of his possessions.

12:16
And he told them a parable, saying,
The land of a certain rich man produced abundantly.

12:17
And he was reasoning within himself, saying,
What shall I do, since I do not have
a place to store my crops?

12:18
And he said,
This is what I will do:
I will tear down my barns
and build larger ones,
and I will gather there all my grain and my goods,

12:19
and I will say to my soul,
Soul, you have many goods stored up for many years;
rest,
eat,
drink, be merry.

12:20
But God said to him,
Fool, this very night your soul is demanded from you;
and the things you prepared, whose will they be?

12:21
So is the one who stores up treasure for himself and is not rich toward God.

12:22
And he said to his disciples,
Therefore I tell you, do not worry about your life, what you will eat,
nor about your body, what you will wear.

12:23
For life is more than food
and the body more than clothing.

12:24
Consider the ravens, that they do not sow
nor reap,
for whom there is no storeroom or barn,
and God feeds them;
how much more are you worth than the birds.

12:25
And which of you by worrying can add a single cubit to his life span?

12:26
If then you cannot do even the smallest thing,
why do you worry about the rest?

12:27
Consider the lilies,
how they grow;
they do not toil
nor spin;
but I tell you, not even Solomon
in all his glory clothed himself like one of these.

12:28
how much more will he clothe you, O you of little faith.
But if God so clothes the grass in the field,
which is here today and tomorrow is thrown into the furnace,

12:29
then do not seek what you will eat and what you will drink, and do not be anxious,

12:30
for all the nations of the world seek after these things,
and your Father knows that you need them.

12:31
But seek his kingdom,
and these things will be added to you.

12:32
Do not fear, little flock,
because your Father was pleased to give you the kingdom.

12:33
Sell your possessions
and give to the poor;
make for yourselves purses that do not wear out,
an inexhaustible treasure in the heavens,
where no thief comes near
and no moth destroys;

12:34
for where your treasure is,
there your heart will be also.

12:35
Let your waists be girded and your lamps burning,

12:36
and be like people
waiting for their master
when he returns from the wedding feast,
so that when he comes and knocks they may open the door for him at once.

12:37
Blessed are those servants,
whom the master finds watching when he comes;
truly I tell you
that he will gird himself
and have them recline at table
and will come and serve them.

12:38
And if he comes in the second or the third watch
and finds them so,
blessed are those servants.

12:39
But know this:
that if the master of the house had known at what hour the thief was coming,
he would not have allowed his house to be broken into.

12:40
You also be ready,
because at an hour you do not expect
the Son of Man is coming.

12:41
And Peter said,
Lord, are you telling this parable to us
or to everyone?

12:42
And the Lord said,
Who then is the faithful and wise steward,
whom the master will put in charge of his household to give them their food at the proper time?

12:43
Blessed is that servant,
whom his master when he comes will find doing so;

12:44
truly I tell you
that he will put him in charge of all his possessions.

12:45
But if that servant says in his heart,
My master is delayed in coming,
and begins to beat the male servants
and the female servants, and to eat and drink and get drunk,

12:46
the master of that servant will come on a day
when he does not expect him
and at an hour he does not know,
and will cut him in pieces
and assign him a place with the unfaithful.

12:47
And that servant who knew his master's will
but did not prepare or act according to his will, will be beaten with many blows;

12:48
but the one who did not know yet did things deserving blows will be beaten with few.
And from everyone who has been given much,
much will be required of him,
and from the one entrusted with much, even more will be demanded of him.

12:49
I came to cast fire upon the earth,
and how I wish it were already kindled!

12:50
But I have a baptism to be baptized with,
and how distressed I am until it is accomplished.

12:51
Do you think that I came to bring peace on the earth?
No, I tell you, but rather division.

12:52
For from now on five in one household will be divided,
three against two and two against three,

12:53
father will be divided against son
and son against father,
mother against daughter
and daughter against mother,
mother-in-law against her daughter-in-law
and daughter-in-law against mother-in-law.

12:54
And he was also saying to the crowds,
When you see a cloud rising in the west, you immediately say, A rainstorm is coming,
and so it happens;

12:55
and when you see the south wind blowing, you say, It will be hot, and it happens.

12:56
Hypocrites,
you know how to evaluate the face of the earth and the sky,
but how is it you do not know how to evaluate this present time?

12:57
And why do you not even judge for yourselves what is right?

12:58
For as you are going with your accuser before the magistrate,
on the way make an effort to settle with him,
lest he drag you before the judge,
and the judge hand you over to the officer, and the officer throw you into prison.

12:59
I tell you, you will never get out of there until you have paid the very last penny.
